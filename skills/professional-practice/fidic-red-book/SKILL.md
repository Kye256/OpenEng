# FIDIC Red Book -- Contract Administration

> **Skill ID:** 42
> **Domain:** Professional Practice
> **Version:** 0.1
> **Status:** Draft
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-21
> **Reviewed By:** ---

## Purpose

Guide the Engineer (FIDIC contract role) through FIDIC Red Book contract administration -- knowing which clause applies, what action to take, and within what time limit. This skill enables the agent to advise on the Conditions of Contract for Construction (1st Edition 1999), covering ~20 key clauses organised by theme with decision trees for the Engineer's most common actions.

## When to Use This Skill

- **Use when:** Administering a construction contract where the Employer provides the design and the Contractor builds to that design. The Red Book is the standard form for road construction projects in East Africa.
- **Do not use when:** The contract is design-build (use Skill #43 FIDIC Yellow Book). Not for EPC/turnkey projects (Silver Book). Not for services contracts (White Book).
- **Prerequisite skills:** None -- this is a standalone foundation skill. Downstream skills #43-46 build on this.

## 1. Knowledge (Universal)

### 1.1 Key Concepts

**Contract Structure:** FIDIC Red Book 1999 comprises General Conditions (standard clauses), Particular Conditions (project-specific modifications), Appendix to Tender (contract data), Specifications, Drawings, and Schedules. The Particular Conditions always take precedence over the General Conditions -- the agent must always caveat that Particular Conditions may modify any time limit, threshold, or procedure described here.

**Key Roles:**
- **Employer** (Clause 2): The party who commissions the works. Provides right of access to site (Sub-Clause 2.1), makes payments (Sub-Clause 14.7), and may terminate for cause (Sub-Clause 15.2) or convenience (Sub-Clause 15.5).
- **Contractor** (Clause 4): The party who executes the works to the Employer's design. Bears construction risk but not design risk. Submits payment applications, claims, and programmes.
- **Engineer** (Clause 3): Appointed by the Employer to administer the contract. Key duties include certifying payments (Sub-Clause 14.6), issuing variation instructions (Sub-Clause 13.1), determining claims (Sub-Clause 3.5), inspecting works (Sub-Clause 7.3), and issuing the Taking-Over Certificate (Sub-Clause 10.1).
- **Engineer's Representative** (Sub-Clause 3.2): May be delegated specific duties by the Engineer.

**Engineer's Impartiality (Sub-Clause 3.5):** When making determinations, the Engineer shall consult with each Party, endeavour to reach agreement, and failing agreement, make a fair determination in accordance with the Contract. The Engineer is NOT the Employer's agent for determinations -- this is a critical principle. The Engineer acts impartially when exercising this Sub-Clause 3.5 function.

**Contract Documents Priority (Sub-Clause 1.5):** In case of ambiguity or discrepancy, the priority of documents is: (1) the Contract Agreement, (2) the Letter of Acceptance, (3) the Letter of Tender, (4) the Particular Conditions, (5) the General Conditions, (6) the Specification, (7) the Drawings, (8) the Schedules and any other documents forming part of the Contract.

**Default Edition:** This skill defaults to the 1999 First Edition. The 2017 Second Edition introduced significant changes including: Sub-Clause 3.5 (Determinations) replaced by Sub-Clause 3.7 (Agreement or Determination) with longer timelines; Clause 20 split into Clause 20 (Claims) and Clause 21 (Disputes); Clause 19 (Force Majeure) replaced by Clause 18 (Exceptional Events); introduction of DAAB (Dispute Avoidance/Adjudication Board) replacing DAB. For East African contracts, the 1999 edition remains the standard form. Where 2017 differences are significant, they are noted.

### Key Clauses by Theme

**Commencement & Time:**
- **Sub-Clause 8.1 -- Commencement of Works:** Contractor shall commence within 28 days after receiving notice from the Engineer (or as stated in Particular Conditions).
- **Sub-Clause 8.2 -- Time for Completion:** The Time for Completion is stated in the Appendix to Tender. Contractor shall complete the whole of the Works within this time.
- **Sub-Clause 8.4 -- Extension of Time for Completion:** Contractor is entitled to extension if delayed by: (a) a Variation or substantial change in quantity, (b) a cause of delay giving entitlement under any Sub-Clause, (c) exceptionally adverse climatic conditions, (d) unforeseeable shortages due to epidemic or governmental actions, (e) any delay, impediment, or prevention caused by the Employer. The Contractor must give notice under Sub-Clause 20.1.
- **Sub-Clause 8.7 -- Delay Damages:** If the Contractor fails to comply with Sub-Clause 8.2, the Contractor shall pay delay damages at the rate stated in the Appendix to Tender.

**Payment:**
- **Sub-Clause 14.1 -- The Contract Price:** The Contract Price shall be the lump sum or re-measurement total as specified.
- **Sub-Clause 14.2 -- Advance Payment:** Employer shall make advance payment as stated in the Appendix to Tender. Security (guarantee) required. Repayment by deduction from interim payments.
- **Sub-Clause 14.3 -- Application for Interim Payment Certificate:** Contractor submits monthly Statement with supporting documents to the Engineer.
- **Sub-Clause 14.6 -- Issue of Interim Payment Certificates (IPC):** Engineer shall issue IPC within 28 days after receiving the Contractor's Statement.
- **Sub-Clause 14.7 -- Payment:** Employer shall pay the amount certified within 56 days after the Engineer receives the Statement (i.e., 28 days after the IPC is issued).
- **Sub-Clause 14.8 -- Delayed Payment:** If payment is late, Contractor is entitled to financing charges (interest).
- **Sub-Clause 14.9 -- Payment of Retention Money:** First half released upon issue of Taking-Over Certificate. Second half released upon expiry of Defects Notification Period (or as modified by Particular Conditions).
- **Sub-Clause 14.11 -- Application for Final Payment Certificate:** Contractor submits draft final statement; then final statement with written discharge.
- **Sub-Clause 14.13 -- Issue of Final Payment Certificate:** Engineer issues within 28 days of receiving the Final Statement and discharge.

**Variations:**
- **Sub-Clause 13.1 -- Right to Vary:** The Engineer may initiate a Variation by instruction, or request a proposal from the Contractor. The Contractor shall not make any alteration unless instructed.
- **Sub-Clause 13.2 -- Value Engineering:** The Contractor may propose changes that reduce cost or improve efficiency. If accepted, a value engineering fee is shared.
- **Sub-Clause 13.3 -- Variation Procedure:** The Engineer may request a proposal before instructing a Variation, with the Contractor's response including a description, programme, and proposal for evaluation.
- **Sub-Clause 12.3 -- Evaluation:** Variations valued using contract rates where applicable, otherwise agreed or determined new rates.

**Claims:**
- **Sub-Clause 20.1 -- Contractor's Claims:** The Contractor shall give notice within 28 days after becoming aware of the event. If notice is late, the Contractor shall not be entitled to additional payment or extension of time, and the Employer is discharged from liability. Detailed particulars due within 42 days after notice. Engineer responds within 42 days. **The 28-day time bar is the single most important procedural requirement in FIDIC claims.**
- **Sub-Clause 3.5 -- Determinations:** If agreement cannot be reached, the Engineer makes a fair determination. The Engineer consults each Party, endeavours to reach agreement, and if not, determines fairly. This is NOT an adversarial role.

**Defects:**
- **Sub-Clause 11.1 -- Completion of Outstanding Work and Remedying Defects:** During the Defects Notification Period, the Contractor completes outstanding work and remedies defects.
- **Sub-Clause 11.3 -- Extension of Defects Notification Period:** The Engineer may extend the DNP if defect or damage cannot be remedied expeditiously.

**Termination:**
- **Sub-Clause 15.2 -- Termination by Employer:** Employer may terminate if Contractor: fails to comply with Sub-Clause 4.2 (Performance Security), abandons Works, subcontracts whole Works without consent, becomes bankrupt, or gives/offers bribe.
- **Sub-Clause 16.1 -- Contractor's Entitlement to Suspend Work:** If Engineer fails to certify or Employer fails to pay, Contractor may give 21 days' notice and then suspend work (or reduce rate of work).

**Force Majeure (Clause 19):**
- **Sub-Clause 19.1 -- Definition:** Exceptional event or circumstance beyond control, could not have been foreseen, not substantially attributable to the Party, and could not reasonably have been avoided.
- **Sub-Clause 19.2 -- Notice:** Party affected shall give notice within 14 days.
- **Sub-Clause 19.4 -- Consequences:** Excused from performance obligations; time extension; additional Cost if caused by Employer risk events.

**Insurance (Clause 18):**
- Contractor's obligations for insurance of Works, Plant, third-party liability. Specific requirements stated in Particular Conditions.

**Dispute Resolution (Clause 20):**
- **Sub-Clause 20.2 -- Appointment of Dispute Adjudication Board (DAB):** Parties appoint 1 or 3 members within 28 days of first party receiving Letter of Acceptance.
- **Sub-Clause 20.4 -- Obtaining DAB's Decision:** Either Party may refer dispute to DAB. DAB gives decision within 84 days. Decision binding unless notice of dissatisfaction within 28 days.
- **Sub-Clause 20.5 -- Amicable Settlement:** 56-day cooling-off period after notice of dissatisfaction.
- **Sub-Clause 20.6 -- Arbitration:** ICC Rules, unless otherwise agreed. Final and binding.

### 1.2 Key Relationships (No Formulas)

This is a knowledge/judgment skill. There are no engineering formulas. The key relationships are:

**Timeline Chain (Payment):**
```
Contractor submits Statement (14.3)
  --> Engineer issues IPC within 28 days (14.6)
    --> Employer pays within 56 days of Statement receipt (14.7)
      --> If late: financing charges (14.8)
        --> If > 42 days late: Contractor may suspend (16.1, after 21-day notice)
```

**Timeline Chain (Claims):**
```
Event occurs
  --> Contractor gives notice within 28 days (20.1) -- TIME BAR
    --> Detailed particulars within 42 days (20.1)
      --> Engineer determines within 42 days (3.5)
        --> If dissatisfied: refer to DAB (20.4) within 84 days
          --> DAB decision within 84 days
            --> Notice of dissatisfaction within 28 days
              --> Amicable settlement 56 days (20.5)
                --> Arbitration (20.6)
```

## 2. Reasoning (Universal)

### 2.1 Decision Logic

```
Step 1: Determine contract form
  |-- Red Book (construction, Employer's design) --> This skill
  |-- Yellow Book (design-build, Contractor's design) --> Skill #43
  +-- If unclear: check who bears design responsibility

Step 2: Identify the contract administration scenario
  |-- (A) Payment application received --> Decision Tree A
  |-- (B) Time extension claim received --> Decision Tree B
  |-- (C) Variation required --> Decision Tree C
  |-- (D) Additional payment claim received --> Decision Tree D
  |-- (E) Dispute arises --> See Skill #46
  +-- (F) Other --> identify relevant Sub-Clause from Section 1

Step 3: Check Particular Conditions
  --> Do Particular Conditions modify the relevant Sub-Clause?
  --> If YES: apply modified provisions
  --> If NO: apply General Conditions as described
  --> ALWAYS flag this check -- even if answer is "not modified"
```

**Decision Tree A -- IPC Application Received:**
```
A1: Contractor submits monthly Statement (Sub-Clause 14.3)
  --> Check: Statement includes supporting documents?
    |-- NO --> request missing documents (do NOT reject Statement)
    +-- YES --> proceed to A2

A2: Verify measured quantities and valuations
  --> Cross-check against site records, test certificates, approved drawings
  --> Adjust for: defective work, overmeasurement, items not yet due
  --> Apply retention percentage (typically 5%, to max stated in Appendix)
  --> Deduct advance payment repayment instalment

A3: Issue IPC within 28 days (Sub-Clause 14.6)
  --> Certify the amount the Engineer considers correct
  --> NOTE: Engineer certifies what is correct, NOT what Contractor claims
  --> If amounts disputed, certify the undisputed amount and state reasons
  --> Employer pays within 56 days of receiving Statement (14.7)
```

**Decision Tree B -- Time Extension Claim:**
```
B1: Check 28-day notice requirement (Sub-Clause 20.1)
  --> Was notice given within 28 days of the Contractor becoming aware?
    |-- NO --> Claim is time-barred. Contractor has no entitlement.
    |       Note: Particular Conditions may modify this time bar.
    |       Note: Engineer should still consider fairness (Sub-Clause 3.5).
    +-- YES --> proceed to B2

B2: Check eligibility under Sub-Clause 8.4
  --> Which cause applies?
    |-- (a) Variation or substantial quantity change
    |-- (b) Cause giving entitlement under another Sub-Clause
    |-- (c) Exceptionally adverse climatic conditions
    |-- (d) Unforeseeable shortages (epidemic/government)
    +-- (e) Delay caused by Employer

B3: Assess the extension
  --> Request detailed particulars (due within 42 days of notice)
  --> Assess actual delay to critical path
  --> Determine fair extension per Sub-Clause 3.5
  --> Consider concurrent delays (Contractor's own delays)
  --> Issue determination with reasons
```

**Decision Tree C -- Variation Instruction:**
```
C1: Engineer identifies need for change
  --> Check: Is the change within the scope of the Contract?
    |-- Fundamental change in nature of Works --> STOP. This may not be a Variation.
    +-- Change within scope --> proceed

C2: Draft variation instruction per Sub-Clause 13.1
  --> Alternatively, request a proposal from Contractor (Sub-Clause 13.3)
  --> Contractor's proposal should include: description, programme, valuation

C3: Agree or determine value (Sub-Clause 12.3)
  --> Contract rates applicable? Use them.
  --> No applicable rate? Agree new rate.
  --> Cannot agree? Engineer determines per Sub-Clause 3.5.
  --> Day rates available as alternative (Sub-Clause 13.6 -- Appendix to Tender)
```

**Decision Tree D -- Additional Payment Claim:**
```
D1: Check 28-day notice (Sub-Clause 20.1) -- same as B1
D2: Identify the Sub-Clause giving entitlement
  --> Common entitlement clauses:
    - Sub-Clause 1.9: Delayed Drawings or Instructions (Cost + profit)
    - Sub-Clause 2.1: Late access to site (Cost + profit)
    - Sub-Clause 4.12: Unforeseeable physical conditions (Cost)
    - Sub-Clause 4.24: Fossils (Cost + profit) [not in 1999; see 4.23 in 2017]
    - Sub-Clause 13.7: Adjustments for changes in legislation (Cost)
    - Sub-Clause 17.4: Employer's risks (Cost + profit for some)
    - Sub-Clause 19.4: Force Majeure consequences (Cost, limited)
  --> Check whether entitlement is "Cost" or "Cost + reasonable profit"
  --> "Cost" is defined in Sub-Clause 1.1.6.4 (excludes profit)

D3: Engineer determines per Sub-Clause 3.5
  --> Consult both Parties
  --> Endeavour to reach agreement
  --> If no agreement, make fair determination
```

### 2.2 Professional Judgment (Universal)

**The Engineer is not the Employer's servant for determinations.** This is the most important principle in FIDIC contract administration. While the Engineer is employed by the Employer (or the Employer's consultant), when making determinations under Sub-Clause 3.5, the Engineer must act impartially. Many disputes arise from Engineers who confuse their advisory role to the Employer with their determination role under the Contract.

**Time limits are strict but may be modified.** Always check Particular Conditions first. The 28-day notice bar under Sub-Clause 20.1 is the most significant time limit -- late notice means no entitlement. However, some jurisdictions and some Particular Conditions modify or remove this bar.

**Document everything.** The Engineer's contemporaneous records are often the most valuable evidence in disputes. Site diaries, measurement records, test certificates, and minutes of meetings should all be maintained systematically.

**Concurrent delay is complex.** When the Contractor's own delays run alongside Employer-caused delays, the assessment of time extension requires professional judgment. FIDIC does not prescribe a specific method for analysing concurrent delay.

### 2.3 Common Errors

1. **Mixing 1999 and 2017 clause numbers.** The 2017 edition renumbered and restructured several clauses. Using Sub-Clause 3.7 (2017 determination) when the contract is based on 1999 (Sub-Clause 3.5) creates confusion and may be legally incorrect.
2. **Ignoring Particular Conditions.** Time limits, thresholds, and procedures in the General Conditions are frequently modified by Particular Conditions. Always check.
3. **Acting as Employer's agent for determinations.** The Engineer must make fair determinations per Sub-Clause 3.5 -- not act in the Employer's commercial interest.
4. **Missing the 28-day notice bar (Sub-Clause 20.1).** The Contractor's claim is time-barred if notice is late. This is the single most litigated provision in FIDIC contracts.
5. **Failing to issue IPCs on time.** The 28-day deadline for issuing IPCs (Sub-Clause 14.6) is mandatory. Failure may entitle the Contractor to suspend work.

### 4.2 Consistency Checks

- **Clause number vs edition:** Verify that clause numbers cited match the contract edition (1999 vs 2017).

- **Entitlement basis:** Verify whether the entitlement clause provides "Cost" or "Cost plus reasonable profit".
- **Notice compliance:** Before assessing any claim, verify the 28-day notice was given under Sub-Clause 20.1.

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1: Confirm Contract Form and Edition
Verify that the contract is FIDIC Red Book (Conditions of Contract for Construction). If it is design-build, redirect to Skill #43 (Yellow Book). Confirm the edition (default 1999).

### Step 2: Load Country Module
Identify the jurisdiction. Load the Uganda module for UNRA/MoWT contracts. Note whether the contract is Government-funded (GoU) or donor-funded (World Bank, AfDB) as this affects Particular Conditions.

### Step 3: Check Particular Conditions
Review Particular Conditions for modifications to General Conditions. Key areas to check: Engineer's authority limits, payment time limits, variation approval thresholds, claims notice periods, dispute resolution mechanism, advance payment terms.

### Step 4: Identify the Contract Administration Issue
Classify the scenario: payment certification, time extension, variation, additional payment claim, dispute, defects, or other. Route to the appropriate decision tree (Section 2.1).

### Step 5: Apply Decision Tree
Follow the relevant decision tree from Section 2.1. At each decision point, reference the correct Sub-Clause and check whether Particular Conditions modify the provision.

### Step 6: Make Determination or Take Action
If a determination is required (Sub-Clause 3.5): consult both Parties, endeavour to reach agreement, and if no agreement, make a fair determination. Document the basis for the determination.

### Step 7: Run Validation
Apply all checks from Section 4 (time limits, clause number consistency, Particular Conditions caveat).

### Step 8: Produce Output
Format per Section 8. Include all clause references, time limits, actions required, and Particular Conditions caveats.
