### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads (Class A, B, C). KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads (Class D, E). KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads under devolution. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**PPRA Context:** The Public Procurement Regulatory Authority (PPRA) is the procurement regulator under the Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act 2015. All road procuring entities (KeNHA, KeRRA, KURA, County Governments) must comply with PPRA procurement regulations. PPRA sets procurement method thresholds and procedural requirements.

**KeNHA as Employer:** KeNHA is the primary employer for national and international trunk road projects. KeNHA uses FIDIC Red Book 1999 as the standard form for road construction contracts, with project-specific Conditions of Particular Application (COPA). KeNHA has established patterns for COPA that are adapted per project.

**The Engineer:** Typically a consulting engineering firm appointed by the Employer (KeNHA, KeRRA, or KURA) under a separate services contract. International and regional firms with Kenya presence commonly serve as Engineer on major road projects.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

FIDIC Red Book 1999 is the standard form for Kenya road construction where the Employer provides the design. The balanced risk allocation (Employer bears design risk, Contractor bears construction risk) applies equally in Kenya. The Engineer acts as contract administrator with fairness obligations under Sub-Clause 3.5.

In Kenya practice, the balance is commonly modified by COPA that:
- Limit the Engineer's authority for variations above a monetary threshold (requiring KeNHA or donor no-objection)
- Adjust payment timelines to reflect government cash flow and exchequer release cycles
- Specify Kenya-specific insurance requirements and performance security formats
- Include PPRA compliance requirements for contract management

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Common Kenya Conditions of Particular Application (COPA)

The following COPA modifications are commonly found in KeNHA contracts. The agent must always check the specific contract's COPA, as these vary by project.

**Engineer's Authority Limits:**
- Variations below a threshold (commonly KES 50 million or 5% of Contract Price, whichever is less) may be approved by the Engineer without Employer approval -- **ILLUSTRATIVE threshold; verify against current project-specific COPA and PPRA regulations**
- Variations above the threshold require written approval from KeNHA (the Employer)
- For World Bank-funded projects, variations above the prior review threshold also require World Bank no-objection

**Payment Modifications:**
- KeNHA contracts may extend the Employer's payment period from 56 days, reflecting government exchequer release cycles
- Advance payment is typically 10-15% of Contract Price, with bank guarantee from a Kenyan or international bank acceptable to KeNHA -- **ILLUSTRATIVE range; verify Appendix to Tender**
- Retention is typically 10% of each IPC up to 5% of Contract Price -- **ILLUSTRATIVE; verify COPA**

**Defects Liability Period:**
- Typically 12 months (365 days) for road construction -- **ILLUSTRATIVE; verify COPA**
- May be extended for specific elements (bridges, structures, special works)

**Claims and Time Limits:**
- The 28-day notice period under Sub-Clause 20.1 is generally retained in Kenya FIDIC contracts
- Some COPA add requirements for contemporaneous record-keeping by the Contractor

**Dispute Resolution:**
- DAB/DAAB commonly required for large contracts
- Seat of arbitration is typically Nairobi
- Some contracts specify arbitration under the Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration (NCIA) or Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (CIArb) Kenya Chapter rules
- Kenya Arbitration Act 1995 (as amended 2010) provides the legal framework

**World Bank Overlay:**
- World Bank Standard Bidding Documents include modified FIDIC Red Book conditions
- Prior review thresholds apply: contracts above specified USD value require World Bank prior review
- No-objection required for: contract award, variation orders above threshold, time extensions beyond threshold, any termination
- Fraud and corruption clause (mandatory) with right of inspection/audit

**AfDB Overlay:**
- AfDB uses similar FIDIC-based conditions with AfDB-specific modifications
- AfDB is a significant funder for Kenya road projects alongside World Bank

### M.2.2 Kenya-Specific Considerations

**Currency:** Kenya Shilling (KES). Large contracts may have multi-currency provisions (KES for local costs, USD/EUR for foreign costs). Sub-Clause 14.15 governs currencies of payment.

**Taxation:** Value Added Tax (VAT) at 16%. Withholding tax applies to payments to non-resident contractors (per Kenya Income Tax Act). The Contract Price typically states whether it is inclusive or exclusive of VAT.

**Performance Security:** Typically 10% of Contract Price (Sub-Clause 4.2). Bank guarantee from a bank acceptable to the Employer. For donor-funded projects, the guarantee must be from a bank acceptable to both KeNHA and the funding agency.

**Insurance Requirements:** Kenya COPA typically require:
- Contractor's All Risks insurance (Sub-Clause 18.2)
- Third party liability insurance
- Workers' compensation per the Work Injury Benefits Act (WIBA)
- Professional indemnity for design elements (if any)

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Step 2a (from core skill): Determine funding source**
```
Is the project donor-funded (World Bank, AfDB, EU, JICA)?
  |-- YES --> Apply donor-modified conditions
  |         Check prior review thresholds
  |         No-objection required for major decisions
  +-- NO (GoK funded) --> Apply standard KeNHA COPA
                          Simpler approval process
                          BUT may have exchequer release delays
```

**Step 3a: Check Engineer's authority for the specific action**
```
Is the action (variation, determination, certification) within the Engineer's authority?
  |-- YES (below threshold) --> Engineer may proceed
  +-- NO (above threshold) --> Seek KeNHA approval
                                If donor-funded: also seek donor no-objection
                                Document the approval chain
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Government payment cycles affect cash flow.** Kenya exchequer release cycles mean that GoK-funded KeNHA projects may experience payment delays beyond the 56-day contractual deadline. The Engineer should:
- Document every late payment and the Contractor's entitlement to financing charges (Sub-Clause 14.8)
- Advise both parties of the contractual position
- If suspension is threatened, escalate to KeNHA management immediately

**Donor-funded projects have additional approval layers.** World Bank and AfDB no-objection processes can add 2-4 weeks to decision timelines. The Engineer should plan for this and advise the Contractor accordingly.

**Land acquisition and resettlement are common delay sources.** Kenya's Land Act 2012 and Land Registration Act 2012 govern land acquisition for road projects. National Land Commission (NLC) involvement in compulsory acquisition adds procedural steps. Delays in resettlement action plans (RAPs) frequently generate claims.

**County government interface.** Devolution means County Governments have jurisdiction over county roads and some urban infrastructure. Projects crossing county boundaries may involve multiple stakeholders. COPA should address the institutional arrangements clearly.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Assuming World Bank or AfDB conditions are identical to standard FIDIC.** Donor SBDs modify numerous provisions. Always read the specific project's conditions.
2. **Ignoring the prior review/no-objection process.** Approving a variation that requires donor no-objection without obtaining it can create misprocurement issues.
3. **Not planning for exchequer release cycles.** Government payment timing is driven by exchequer cycles, not contractual deadlines alone.
4. **Not adjusting for Kenya's rainfall patterns.** Extension of time claims for "exceptionally adverse climatic conditions" (Sub-Clause 8.4(c)) must be assessed against historical rainfall data for the specific project location. Kenya has two rainy seasons (March-May long rains, October-December short rains), and these are not "exceptional."

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Contract edition matches clause numbers used
- **Standard:** FIDIC Red Book 1999 (or 2017 if specified)
- **Pass condition:** All Sub-Clause numbers are from the correct edition
- **Fail action:** Flag mismatched clause numbers and identify correct references

- **Check:** Donor no-objection obtained for actions above threshold
- **Standard:** World Bank/AfDB Procurement Regulations
- **Pass condition:** Evidence of no-objection for applicable actions
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Action may require donor no-objection

- **Check:** PPRA compliance for contract management actions
- **Standard:** Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act 2015
- **Pass condition:** Contract management actions comply with PPRA requirements
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Verify PPRA compliance for this action

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

FIDIC does not have a formal "departures" process like DMRB. However, COPA serve the same function -- they modify General Conditions for project-specific needs. In Kenya:
- KeNHA has established COPA patterns that are adapted per project
- Donor-funded projects have mandatory conditions that cannot be modified (fraud/corruption, inspection/audit)
- Any modification to donor mandatory provisions is a misprocurement risk
- PPRA regulations may impose additional requirements on contract management

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example 1: KeNHA Class A Road Construction -- FIDIC Red Book with World Bank Financing

**Project:** Construction of a Class A trunk road (Nairobi to [County Border]), KES 12 billion, World Bank IDA funded. FIDIC Red Book 1999 with KeNHA COPA.

**Scenario:** The Engineer is advising on the appropriate contract form and key COPA amendments for this project. KeNHA is the Employer. The detailed design is complete, prepared by a consulting firm under a separate design contract.

**Solution:**

Step 1: Contract form selection
- Design is complete and provided by the Employer (KeNHA). Red Book is the correct form.
- Red Book is the standard form for KeNHA road construction contracts.
- World Bank accepts FIDIC Red Book with World Bank SBD modifications.

Step 2: Key COPA amendments for Kenya practice
- **Engineer's authority:** Limit to KES 50 million or 5% of Contract Price for variation instructions -- **ILLUSTRATIVE threshold; verify against project-specific COPA**.
- **Payment terms:** Employer payment within 56 days of Statement receipt (may be modified by COPA).
- **Advance payment:** 10% of Contract Price with bank guarantee from an acceptable bank.
- **Retention:** 10% of each IPC up to 5% of Contract Price.
- **Defects liability:** 12 months (365 days) for road works.
- **Performance security:** 10% of Contract Price.

Step 3: World Bank requirements
- Prior review thresholds apply per World Bank financing agreement.
- No-objection required for: contract award, variations above threshold, time extensions beyond threshold, termination.
- World Bank fraud and corruption clause is mandatory and cannot be modified.
- Standard bidding documents must follow World Bank SBD format.

Step 4: PPRA compliance
- Procurement method must comply with PPRA thresholds and procedures.
- For this contract value (KES 12 billion), Open Tendering is the required method under PPRA -- **ILLUSTRATIVE; verify against current PPRA regulations**.
- However, World Bank procurement rules override PPRA thresholds for donor-funded projects.

Step 5: Dispute resolution
- DAB/DAAB: required for this contract value.
- Seat of arbitration: Nairobi (per COPA).
- Arbitration under NCIA or CIArb Kenya rules (per COPA).
- Kenya Arbitration Act 1995 provides the legal framework.

**Result:** FIDIC Red Book 1999 selected with KeNHA COPA and World Bank SBD modifications. Key COPA provisions documented. PPRA compliance confirmed (World Bank rules override on procurement method). Dispute resolution via DAB/DAAB with Nairobi seat.

### Example 2: GoK-Funded KeNHA Road Rehabilitation -- IPC Processing

**Project:** Rehabilitation of Class B trunk road, KES 3.5 billion, Government of Kenya funded.

**Scenario:** The Contractor submits monthly Statement for Month 5 claiming KES 450 million of measured work. The Engineer verifies quantities.

**Solution:**

Step 1: Verify Statement (Sub-Clause 14.3)
- Earthworks: claimed 38,000 m3, site records show 35,500 m3. Adjust: KES -25 million.
- Pavement works: claimed KES 180 million, verified KES 165 million after quality test adjustments. Adjust: KES -15 million.
- Culvert works: claimed and verified at KES 85 million. No adjustment.

Step 2: Calculate certified amount
- Claimed: KES 450 million
- Adjustments: -KES 40 million
- Gross certified: KES 410 million
- Less retention (10%): -KES 41 million
- Less advance payment deduction (pro-rata): -KES 20 million
- Net IPC amount: KES 349 million

Step 3: Issue IPC within 28 days (Sub-Clause 14.6)
- IPC issued on Day 24 -- within deadline.
- Employer (KeNHA) shall pay within 56 days of receiving Statement (Sub-Clause 14.7).

Step 4: Monitor payment -- GoK exchequer cycle
- GoK-funded payments depend on exchequer release cycles.
- If payment exceeds 56 days: document and advise both parties.
- Financing charges accrue under Sub-Clause 14.8 from Day 57.
