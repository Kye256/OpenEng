## Purpose

Guide the assembly of a complete set of tender (bidding) documents for a road construction or rehabilitation contract. The tender document is the package issued to bidders, containing everything they need to prepare and submit a responsive bid. The skill covers: selecting the correct Standard Bidding Document (SBD) template based on procurement method and funding source, identifying all required document components, drafting project-specific sections (Particular Conditions, Bid Data Sheet), and checking completeness before issue.

## When to Use This Skill

- **Use when:** A procurement method has been selected (Skill #47) and tender documents need to be assembled before issuing to bidders.
- **Do not use when:** Procuring consultancy services (different SBDs). Evaluating bids (use Skill #49). Preparing the BoQ component (use Skill #51).
- **Prerequisite skills:** Skill #47 (Procurement Methods) determines which SBD to use. Skill #42 (FIDIC Red Book) or Skill #43 (FIDIC Yellow Book) provides the contract conditions. Skill #51 (BoQ Preparation) provides the bill of quantities component.

## 1. Knowledge (Universal)

### 1.1 Key Concepts

**Standard Bidding Documents (SBDs):** Pre-approved document templates issued by procurement authorities (national or donor). SBDs ensure consistency, legal compliance, and fairness. The procuring entity customises the SBD for each project by completing project-specific sections (Bid Data Sheet, Particular Conditions, technical specifications, BoQ, drawings list) -- NOT by modifying the standard text.

**Tender Document Components:**

1. **Invitation for Bids (IFB):** Public notice inviting bidders to participate. Contains: project name, employer, scope summary, deadline, location for obtaining documents. Published in newspapers and/or online per procurement rules.

2. **Instructions to Bidders (ITB):** Standard instructions governing bid preparation and submission. Changes to ITB are made ONLY through entries in the Bid Data Sheet -- never by modifying the ITB text itself.

3. **Bid Data Sheet (BDS):** Project-specific data that customises the ITB. Includes: employer details, procurement reference, bid validity period, bid security amount and form, submission deadline, bid opening time and place, evaluation method, currency provisions.

4. **General Conditions of Contract (GCC):** The standard contract conditions (e.g., FIDIC Red Book General Conditions). These are NOT modified -- changes are made through Particular Conditions only.

5. **Particular Conditions of Contract (PCC):** Project-specific modifications to the General Conditions. This is where the procuring entity customises: time for completion, defects period, performance security amount, advance payment terms, delay damages rate, Engineer's authority limits, dispute resolution details, applicable law.

6. **Technical Specifications:** Detailed technical requirements for the works. For road projects, typically references a national specification (e.g., MoWT General Specifications for Road and Bridge Works) with project-specific amendments and additional specifications.

7. **Bill of Quantities (BoQ):** Itemised list of work items with descriptions, units, and estimated quantities. Bidders enter unit rates and calculate total prices. Prepared per Skill #51 (BoQ Preparation).

8. **Drawings List:** Schedule of drawings issued with the tender documents. Actual drawings are listed by number and title. Full drawings may be issued as a separate volume.

9. **Bid Forms:** Standard forms that bidders must complete and submit:
   - Bid form / Letter of Bid (prices, validity, commitments)
   - Qualification information form (experience, capacity, financial)
   - Bid security form (bank guarantee or insurance bond)
   - Bid-securing declaration (alternative to security in some frameworks)

10. **Annexes and Schedules:** Activity schedules, equipment schedules, personnel schedules, work programme requirements, environmental and social requirements.

**Envelope Systems:**
- **Single-envelope:** Technical and financial proposals in one envelope. Standard for most works contracts under ICB and NCB (technical compliance evaluation followed by financial comparison).
- **Two-envelope:** Technical and financial proposals in separate envelopes. Technical envelopes opened first; financial envelopes opened only for technically qualifying bidders. Used for QCBS evaluation or where specified.

### 1.2 Key Relationships

**SBD Selection Flow:**
```
Procurement method (from Skill #47) + Funding source --> SBD selection
  |-- GoU funded --> National SBDs (e.g., PPDA SBDs)
  |-- World Bank funded --> World Bank SBDs for Procurement of Works
  |-- AfDB funded --> AfDB SBDs
  +-- Design-build --> Separate SBD for design-build (Yellow Book conditions)
```

**Document Assembly Checklist:**
```
For each tender document:
  1. Select correct SBD template
  2. Complete Bid Data Sheet (project-specific data)
  3. Draft Particular Conditions (modifications to GCC)
  4. Prepare/finalise Technical Specifications
  5. Prepare/finalise Bill of Quantities (Skill #51)
  6. Compile Drawings List
  7. Prepare Bid Forms (usually standard from SBD)
  8. Quality check: completeness, consistency, no contradictions
  9. Contracts Committee approval before issue
```

## 2. Reasoning (Universal)

### 2.1 Decision Logic

```
Step 1: Confirm procurement method and funding source
  --> From Skill #47 output: method, framework, funding source
  --> Determines SBD selection

Step 2: Select Standard Bidding Document
  |-- GoU + Open Domestic Bidding --> National SBD for works (ODB)
  |-- GoU + Restricted Bidding --> National SBD for works (restricted)
  |-- GoU + Quotation Method --> Simplified quotation forms
  |-- WB + ICB --> World Bank SBD: Procurement of Works (large)
  |-- WB + NCB --> World Bank SBD or acceptable national SBD
  |-- Design-build --> SBD for design-build with Skill #43 (Yellow Book) conditions
  +-- See country module for specific SBD versions

Step 3: Complete Bid Data Sheet
  --> Fill in all project-specific entries
  --> Cross-check with Particular Conditions for consistency
  --> Key entries: bid validity, bid security amount, evaluation method

Step 4: Draft Particular Conditions
  --> Modify General Conditions for project-specific needs
  --> Use Skill #42 (Red Book) or Skill #43 (Yellow Book) as basis
  --> Key modifications: time for completion, performance security,
      advance payment, delay damages, Engineer's authority, applicable law
  --> For WB projects: mandatory WB clauses must be included

Step 5: Prepare Technical Specifications
  --> Reference national standard specifications (e.g., MoWT)
  --> Add project-specific amendments and additional specifications
  --> Cross-check with BoQ items (every spec must have a BoQ item and vice versa)

Step 6: Prepare Bill of Quantities
  --> From Skill #51 (BoQ Preparation)
  --> Cross-check with specifications and drawings
  --> Ensure measurement methods are consistent with specifications

Step 7: Compile Drawings List
  --> List all drawings by number and title
  --> Verify drawings match specifications and BoQ

Step 8: Quality Check
  --> No contradictions between ITB/BDS and GCC/PCC
  --> All cross-references are correct
  --> BoQ items match specifications
  --> Bid security amount and form are specified
  --> Evaluation criteria are clearly stated
  --> Submission deadline allows adequate bidding period
```

### 2.2 Professional Judgment (Universal)

**Never modify the standard text of ITB or GCC.** All customisation is done through the Bid Data Sheet (for ITB) and Particular Conditions (for GCC). Modifying the standard text creates legal risk and may violate procurement rules.

**The tender document must be internally consistent.** Contradictions between components (e.g., different completion times in the BDS and PCC, or BoQ items not matching specifications) are the most common source of bid preparation errors and subsequent disputes.

**Adequate bidding period is essential.** Insufficient time for bidders to prepare responsive bids can lead to poor quality bids, complaints, and procurement challenges. National procurement rules specify minimum bidding periods.

**Evaluation criteria must be stated in the tender documents.** No criteria may be introduced or applied during evaluation that are not explicitly stated in the tender documents (PPDA Evaluation Regulations 2014, Regulation 7(2)). This is a fundamental principle.

### 2.3 Common Errors

1. **Modifying the standard ITB or GCC text directly.** Changes must go through BDS (for ITB) and Particular Conditions (for GCC).
2. **Inconsistency between document components.** Specifications referring to items not in the BoQ, or PCC time for completion differing from the BDS.
3. **Omitting mandatory clauses for donor-funded projects.** World Bank mandatory clauses (fraud/corruption, inspection/audit, eligibility) must not be omitted or modified.
4. **Insufficient evaluation criteria detail.** Vague criteria like "experience" without specifying minimum requirements lead to subjective evaluation.
5. **Bid security amount too high or too low.** Standard range is 1-3% of estimated contract value. Too high deters participation; too low provides insufficient security.
6. **Using the wrong SBD.** Using an old edition, or a supplies SBD for works procurement, is a fundamental error.

## 3. Defaults (Universal)

| Input | Default Value | Condition | Flag Text | Rationale |
|-------|--------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Bid validity | 90-120 days | Not specified | ASSUMED: 90-day bid validity. Verify BDS requirements. | Common for works contracts |
| Bid security | 2% of estimated value | Not specified | ASSUMED: 2% bid security. Verify BDS/SBD requirements. | Standard range 1-3% |
| Performance security | 10% of contract price | Not specified | ASSUMED: 10% performance security. Verify PCC. | FIDIC standard |
| Envelope system | Single envelope | Not specified | ASSUMED: Single envelope (standard for works ICB/NCB). | Standard for works |

### Default Behaviour Rules

1. **SBD instructions take precedence** over defaults.
2. **Cross-check all entries** in BDS against PCC for consistency.
3. **Flag any deviation** from standard SBD requirements.

## 4. Validation (Universal)

### 4.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Minimum | Maximum | Unit | Action if Violated |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|--------------------|
| Bid security | 1 | 3 | % of estimate | If outside range, verify with SBD requirements |
| Performance security | 5 | 15 | % of contract | Standard 10%. If different, check PCC justification |
| Bid validity | 60 | 180 | days | If shorter, may be insufficient; if longer, excessive |

### 4.2 Consistency Checks

- **BDS vs PCC:** Time for completion, performance security %, advance payment terms must be consistent.
- **BoQ vs Specifications:** Every BoQ item must have a corresponding specification clause and vice versa.
- **Drawings vs BoQ:** Quantities in BoQ must be derivable from the drawings.
- **Evaluation criteria:** Criteria stated in BDS/ITB must match those used in evaluation (no additions or removals).
- **Minimum bidding period:** Check against national/donor minimum requirements.

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1: Confirm Inputs
Verify that procurement method (Skill #47), contract form (Skill #42/#43), and funding source are confirmed. These determine the SBD selection.

### Step 2: Select SBD Template
Based on procurement method and funding source, identify the correct SBD. See country module for available SBD editions.

### Step 3: Complete Project-Specific Sections
Draft BDS entries, Particular Conditions, and any project-specific annexes. Cross-check for internal consistency.

### Step 4: Assemble Technical Package
Integrate technical specifications, BoQ (from Skill #51), and drawings list. Cross-check for completeness.

### Step 5: Quality Check
Run the document assembly checklist. Verify no contradictions, all cross-references correct, mandatory clauses included.

### Step 6: Approval
Submit assembled tender documents for Contracts Committee approval (or equivalent authority) before issue to bidders.

### Step 7: Produce Output
Format per Section 8. Provide the document checklist, SBD identification, and any quality issues found.
