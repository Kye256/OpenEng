### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads (Class A, B, C). KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads (Class D, E). KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**PPRA Standard Bidding Documents:** The PPRA issues authorised Standard Bidding Documents (SBDs) for each procurement type. Procuring entities must use these documents for GoK-funded procurement. Any deviation requires PPRA Authority approval. PPRA SBDs are available for different procurement methods (Open Tendering, Restricted Tendering, Request for Quotations) and contract types (works, supplies, services).

**World Bank Standard Bidding Documents:** For World Bank-funded projects, the World Bank issues its own SBDs. The WB SBD for Procurement of Works contains modified FIDIC conditions and mandatory World Bank clauses (fraud/corruption, inspection/audit, eligibility). KeNHA uses WB SBDs for all WB-funded road projects.

**Kenya Standard Specification:** The Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction is the primary technical specifications reference for Kenya road projects. It specifies materials, workmanship, testing requirements, and acceptance criteria. This is analogous to Uganda's MoWT General Specifications but specific to Kenya (per D-03).

**Document Approval:** The Tender Committee (or equivalent body within the procuring entity) must approve all bidding documents before issue. This is consistent with PPRA requirements for oversight of the procurement process.

### M.2.4 Technical Specifications References

For Kenya road projects, technical specifications typically reference:

1. **Kenya Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction** -- Primary reference for materials, workmanship, and testing requirements (per D-03)
2. **Kenya Road Design Manual (RDM)** -- Design standards for geometric, pavement, drainage, and structural design
3. **Project-specific amendments** -- Additional or modified specifications for the particular project
4. **Environmental and social management requirements** -- NEMA requirements and donor safeguards
5. **Safety specifications** -- Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) requirements

### M.2.5 Bill of Quantities Format

Kenya road projects use the **WSCM Part 3** standard BoQ format (see Skill #44 Kenya module for detailed structure):
- Series-based item numbering (Series 100-800 typical for road works)
- Standard item descriptions and measurement rules
- Consistent across all Kenya road procuring entities

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Kenya SBD Selection:**
```
Is the project GoK-funded or donor-funded?
  |-- GoK funded:
  |     Is it Open Tendering (above PPRA threshold)?
  |       |-- YES: PPRA SBD for Works (Open Tendering edition)
  |       +-- NO: PPRA Quotation Forms or Restricted Tendering SBD
  |
  +-- World Bank / AfDB funded:
        Is it ICB?
          |-- YES: Donor SBD for Procurement of Works
          +-- NO (NCB): Donor SBD or acceptable PPRA SBD
                         (Donor reviews PPRA SBD for acceptability)
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**PPRA SBDs are mandatory for GoK-funded procurement.** Using non-standard documents or modifying standard text (as opposed to using BDS/COPA entries) may be a procurement violation. Verify current edition of PPRA SBDs before use.

**For donor-funded projects, donor SBDs are usually used.** Even for NCB, the donor SBD is preferred because it includes mandatory donor clauses. If PPRA SBDs are used for NCB, they must include donor mandatory clauses.

**Cross-check BoQ items against Standard Specification.** The WSCM Part 3 BoQ item descriptions and measurement units should be consistent with the Kenya Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction. Inconsistencies between BoQ and specifications are a common tender document quality issue.

**Kenya Standard Specification, not MoWT General Specifications.** Kenya road projects reference the Kenya Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction. Do not reference Uganda's MoWT General Specifications in Kenya tender documents.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Using outdated PPRA SBDs.** PPRA updates SBDs periodically. Always verify the current edition.
2. **Omitting donor mandatory clauses from NCB documents.** Donor-funded NCB using PPRA SBDs must include mandatory donor clauses.
3. **Referencing wrong technical specifications.** Kenya road projects reference the Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction (Kenya), not MoWT General Specifications (Uganda).
4. **Inconsistent COPA entries.** COPA modifications that contradict BDS entries or create ambiguities.
5. **Using WSCM Part 3 item numbers inconsistent with Standard Specification sections.** BoQ items must align with both WSCM Part 3 format and Standard Specification requirements.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: Assemble Tender Documents for KeRRA Rural Road Construction (GoK Funded, Open Tendering)

**Project:** Construction of Class D rural road, 25 km, in Western Kenya.
**Procurement method:** Open National Tendering (from Skill #47, value KES 800 million, GoK funded).
**Contract form:** FIDIC Red Book (Skill #42).

**Solution:**

**Step 1: SBD Selection**
- GoK funded + Open National Tendering --> PPRA SBD for Works (Open Tendering edition)
- Verify current edition from PPRA Authority

**Step 2: Complete Bid Data Sheet**
- Employer: Kenya Rural Roads Authority (KeRRA)
- Procurement reference: KeRRA/WORKS/2026/[number]
- Bid validity: 90 days
- Bid security: 2% of estimated KES 800 million = KES 16 million, unconditional bank guarantee
- Submission deadline: Allow minimum 30 calendar days from first advertisement (ILLUSTRATIVE -- verify)
- Bid opening: same date, immediately after submission deadline
- Evaluation method: Technical compliance (pass/fail) then lowest evaluated price
- Currency: Kenya Shillings (KES)

**Step 3: Draft Conditions of Particular Application (COPA)**
- Sub-Clause 4.2: Performance Security = 10% of Contract Price
- Sub-Clause 8.2: Time for Completion = 18 months
- Sub-Clause 8.7: Delay damages = 0.05% of Contract Price per day (ILLUSTRATIVE)
- Sub-Clause 14.2: Advance Payment = 10% of Contract Price, bank guarantee required
- Sub-Clause 14.7: Payment within 56 days
- Sub-Clause 14.9: Retention = 10% per IPC, max 5% of Contract Price
- Sub-Clause 20.6: Arbitration per NCIA Rules, seat Nairobi
- Applicable law: Laws of Kenya
- Language: English

**Step 4: Technical Specifications**
- Reference: Kenya Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction (current edition)
- Project-specific amendments for local material sources and environmental conditions
- Environmental management plan requirements per NEMA
- Safety requirements per OSHA

**Step 5: Bill of Quantities**
- Prepared per WSCM Part 3 format (Skill #51 for BoQ preparation)
- Series 100 through Series 800 as applicable
- Items match Standard Specification sections
- Measurement units consistent with WSCM Part 3 rules

**Step 6: Drawings List**
- All design drawings listed by number and title per Kenya RDM standards
- Separate volume for full-size drawings

**Step 7: Quality Check**

| Check | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| SBD current edition | PASS | Verified with PPRA |
| BDS complete | PASS | All required entries completed |
| COPA consistency | PASS | No contradictions with BDS |
| BoQ matches Standard Specification | PASS | WSCM Part 3 format, items cross-checked |
| Bid security specified | PASS | KES 16M, bank guarantee |
| Evaluation criteria stated | PASS | Technical compliance + lowest price |
| Minimum bidding period | PASS | 30+ calendar days allowed (ILLUSTRATIVE) |

**Step 8: Approval**
- Submit to Tender Committee for approval before issue

**Result:** Complete PPRA SBD for Works with: BDS entries, FIDIC Red Book COPA, Standard Specification reference, WSCM Part 3 BoQ, drawings list, standard bid forms. Tender Committee approval required before issue.
