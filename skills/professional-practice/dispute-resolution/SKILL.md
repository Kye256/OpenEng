## Purpose

Guide the Engineer and contracting parties through the FIDIC dispute resolution process when the Engineer's determination under Sub-Clause 3.5 is not accepted. This skill covers the three-tier dispute resolution mechanism under FIDIC 1999: (1) DAB referral, (2) amicable settlement, and (3) arbitration. The Engineer is not a party to the dispute but may be required to provide records and attend as a witness.

## When to Use This Skill

- **Use when:** A party is dissatisfied with the Engineer's determination and wishes to challenge it, or when a dispute has arisen that cannot be resolved through the Engineer's determination process.
- **Do not use when:** The dispute can still be resolved through the Engineer's determination (Sub-Clause 3.5 -- use Skill #42/#45). For routine contract administration (use Skills #42-45).
- **Prerequisite skills:** Skill #42 (Red Book) or Skill #43 (Yellow Book) for contract context. Skill #45 (Variations & Claims) for understanding the determination that led to the dispute.

## 1. Knowledge (Universal)

### 1.1 Key Concepts

**Three-Tier Dispute Resolution (FIDIC 1999):**

**Tier 1 -- Dispute Adjudication Board (DAB) (Sub-Clause 20.2-20.4):**
- **Appointment (Sub-Clause 20.2):** Parties jointly appoint the DAB within 28 days after the first Party receives the Letter of Acceptance. DAB may comprise 1 or 3 members. For a 3-member DAB, each Party nominates one member, and the two nominated members agree on the chairman.
- **Standing DAB vs Ad Hoc DAB:** A standing DAB is appointed for the duration of the contract and visits the site periodically (proactive role). An ad hoc DAB is appointed only when a dispute arises (reactive role). Standing DABs are preferred by FIDIC.
- **DAB Decision (Sub-Clause 20.4):** Either Party may refer a dispute to the DAB. The DAB gives its decision within 84 days (or as agreed by the Parties and approved by the DAB). The decision is "binding on both Parties" -- meaning it must be complied with immediately, even if a Party disagrees.
- **Notice of Dissatisfaction:** If a Party is dissatisfied with the DAB's decision, it must give notice of dissatisfaction within 28 days. If no notice is given within 28 days, the decision becomes "final and binding" -- it can never be revised.
- **Compliance:** Both Parties must comply with the DAB decision immediately, regardless of whether they have given notice of dissatisfaction. Non-compliance is itself a ground for arbitration.

**Tier 2 -- Amicable Settlement (Sub-Clause 20.5):**
- After a notice of dissatisfaction is given, the Parties have 56 days to attempt amicable settlement.
- This is a mandatory step before arbitration can commence.
- No formal procedure is prescribed -- negotiation, mediation, or other forms of alternative dispute resolution may be used.
- If settlement is reached, it is typically recorded in a settlement agreement.

**Tier 3 -- Arbitration (Sub-Clause 20.6):**
- If amicable settlement fails (or after 56 days have elapsed since the notice of dissatisfaction), either Party may commence arbitration.
- Default rules: ICC (International Chamber of Commerce) Rules of Arbitration (1999 edition). Particular Conditions may specify different rules (e.g., UNCITRAL, LCIA).
- The arbitration tribunal may review and revise any DAB decision, Engineer's determination, or certificate.
- The arbitration award is final and binding.

**Sub-Clause 20.7 -- Failure to Comply with DAB Decision:**
If a Party fails to comply with a binding DAB decision, the other Party may (without prejudice to any other rights) refer the failure to arbitration under Sub-Clause 20.6. This is a separate arbitration from the underlying dispute.

**Sub-Clause 20.8 -- Expiry of DAB's Appointment:**
If the DAB's appointment has expired and no replacement is appointed, either Party may request that the dispute be decided by arbitration. This bypasses the DAB tier.

**2017 Edition Changes -- DAAB:**
The 2017 edition replaced the DAB with the DAAB (Dispute Avoidance/Adjudication Board). Key differences:
- **Dispute avoidance role:** The DAAB proactively assists the Parties in avoiding disputes (not just adjudicating them). The DAAB may issue informal advisory opinions.
- **Standing DAAB mandatory:** The 2017 edition requires a standing DAAB (not ad hoc).
- **Clause 21 (Disputes):** Claims (Clause 20) are separated from disputes (Clause 21) in the 2017 edition.
- **Note:** DAAB is emerging practice, not yet standard in East African contracts which generally use the 1999 edition.

**Engineer's Role in Disputes:**
- The Engineer is NOT a party to the dispute.
- The Engineer may be required to provide records, attend DAB hearings as a witness, and explain determinations.
- The Engineer's impartiality (Sub-Clause 3.5) may be questioned during disputes.
- The Engineer should maintain comprehensive contemporaneous records throughout the contract.

### 1.2 Key Relationships (No Formulas)

**Dispute Resolution Timeline (FIDIC 1999):**
```
Engineer's Determination (Sub-Clause 3.5)
  --> Party dissatisfied
    --> Refer to DAB (Sub-Clause 20.4)
      --> DAB decision within 84 days
        --> Decision is binding immediately (must comply)
          --> If dissatisfied: Notice of Dissatisfaction within 28 days
            |-- No notice within 28 days --> Decision is FINAL AND BINDING
            +-- Notice given within 28 days
              --> Amicable Settlement attempt (56 days) (Sub-Clause 20.5)
                --> If settled: agreement recorded
                --> If not settled (after 56 days): Arbitration (Sub-Clause 20.6)
                  --> Arbitral award: FINAL AND BINDING
```

**Critical Deadlines:**
```
DAB decision: 84 days from referral
Notice of dissatisfaction: 28 days from DAB decision
Amicable settlement period: 56 days from notice of dissatisfaction
Arbitration: may commence after amicable settlement period expires
```

## 2. Reasoning (Universal)

### 2.1 Decision Logic

```
Step 1: Confirm the dispute has passed through the Engineer's determination
  |-- Has the Engineer made a determination under Sub-Clause 3.5?
  |   |-- YES --> A party is dissatisfied. Proceed to DAB.
  |   +-- NO --> The Engineer should first make a determination.
  +-- Is there a DAB appointed?
      |-- YES --> Refer to DAB (Sub-Clause 20.4)
      +-- NO --> Can the Parties appoint a DAB? If not, Sub-Clause 20.8 applies.

Step 2: DAB Referral (Sub-Clause 20.4)
  --> Either Party submits referral to DAB
  --> DAB reviews submissions from both Parties
  --> DAB may request additional information, visit site, hold hearings
  --> DAB gives decision within 84 days

Step 3: Compliance with DAB Decision
  --> Both Parties MUST comply immediately
  --> Compliance is mandatory regardless of dissatisfaction
  --> Non-compliance is a separate ground for arbitration (20.7)

Step 4: Notice of Dissatisfaction
  --> Must be given within 28 days of DAB decision
  --> CRITICAL: If not given within 28 days, decision is FINAL AND BINDING
  --> Notice must state that it is a notice of dissatisfaction with the DAB decision

Step 5: Amicable Settlement (Sub-Clause 20.5)
  --> 56-day period for negotiation
  --> No prescribed procedure -- parties negotiate freely
  --> If settled: record agreement
  --> If not settled: proceed to arbitration after 56 days

Step 6: Arbitration (Sub-Clause 20.6)
  --> Commence after amicable settlement period expires
  --> ICC Rules (or as modified by Particular Conditions)
  --> Tribunal may revise any DAB decision, Engineer's determination, or certificate
  --> Award is final and binding
```

### 2.2 Professional Judgment (Universal)

**DAB decisions are immediately binding.** Parties must comply with DAB decisions even if they disagree. This is one of the most important principles in FIDIC dispute resolution. A party that refuses to comply with a DAB decision is in breach of the contract.

**The 28-day notice of dissatisfaction is critical.** Missing this deadline means the DAB decision becomes final and binding -- it can never be challenged, even in arbitration. Parties must diarise this deadline immediately upon receiving a DAB decision.

**Amicable settlement should be genuinely attempted.** While many parties treat the 56-day amicable settlement period as a formality before arbitration, FIDIC intends this as a genuine opportunity to resolve the dispute without the cost and time of arbitration. Mediation can be very effective during this period.

**Arbitration is expensive and time-consuming.** International arbitration under ICC Rules can cost millions of dollars and take years. For smaller disputes, this cost may exceed the disputed amount. The Engineer should encourage resolution at earlier stages.

**Engineer's records are crucial.** The Engineer's contemporaneous records (site diaries, measurement records, instructions, minutes) are often the most important evidence in arbitration. Maintaining comprehensive records throughout the contract is essential.

### 2.3 Common Errors

1. **Not complying with a binding DAB decision while pursuing arbitration.** The DAB decision is binding immediately -- compliance is mandatory.
2. **Missing the 28-day notice of dissatisfaction.** This deadline is absolute.
3. **Commencing arbitration before the 56-day amicable settlement period.** This is a procedural requirement.
4. **Treating DAB referral as an appeal of the Engineer's determination.** The DAB does its own assessment; it does not simply review the Engineer's decision.
5. **Engineer participating as a party.** The Engineer is a witness, not a party. The Engineer should not advocate for either the Employer or the Contractor.

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1: Confirm Dispute
Verify that the Engineer's determination has been made and a Party is dissatisfied.

### Step 2: Check DAB Status
Is a DAB appointed? If not, advise Parties to appoint per Sub-Clause 20.2. If appointment fails, Sub-Clause 20.8 may apply.

### Step 3: DAB Referral
Either Party refers the dispute to the DAB with supporting documentation.

### Step 4: DAB Process
DAB reviews submissions, may hold hearings, issues decision within 84 days.

### Step 5: Compliance
Both Parties must comply with the DAB decision immediately.

### Step 6: Notice of Dissatisfaction
If dissatisfied, give notice within 28 days. If no notice, the decision is final and binding.

### Step 7: Amicable Settlement
56-day period for negotiation. Encourage genuine settlement efforts.

### Step 8: Arbitration
If no settlement, commence arbitration under ICC Rules (or as modified by Particular Conditions).
