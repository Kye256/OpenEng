### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities as Employers:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads. KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads. KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**Supervision consultant (Resident Engineer team):** Kenya road projects typically appoint a supervision consulting firm as the Engineer under FIDIC. The firm deploys a site-based team led by the Resident Engineer (RE). The supervision team structure typically includes:

| Role | Responsibility | Typical Deployment |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Resident Engineer (RE) | Overall site supervision, contract administration, Engineer's Representative per Sub-Clause 3.2 | Full-time site-based |
| Deputy RE | Assists RE, acts during RE absence, coordinates specialist staff | Full-time site-based |
| Materials Engineer | Quality control, material testing oversight, source approval | Full-time site-based |
| Surveyor | Setting out verification, measurement, as-built surveys | Full-time site-based |
| Inspector of Works | Daily inspection of construction activities, site diary entries | Full-time site-based |
| Quantity Surveyor | Measurement, payment certification support, variation assessment | Full-time site-based |
| Environmental/Social Specialist | NEMA compliance, ESIA monitoring, community liaison | Full-time or part-time |
| Laboratory Technician(s) | Field and laboratory testing per Section 1700 | Full-time site-based |

**MoRT policy oversight:** The Ministry of Roads and Transport sets policy direction. KeNHA, KeRRA, KURA, and County Governments are responsible for project implementation within their respective mandates.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Kenya supervision practice is based on:
- Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction (WSCM Part 2, 2025) -- technical compliance requirements
- FIDIC Red Book 1999/2017 -- contract administration framework
- Supervision consultant's Terms of Reference -- scope, team composition, deliverables
- Professional practice and industry norms

The Standard Specification is comprehensive and prescriptive, covering Contractor's obligations (Section 1300), Engineer's establishment (Section 1400), testing requirements (Section 1700), and quality control procedures (Section 1800).

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Contractor's Obligations (Standard Specification Section 1300)

The Standard Specification Section 1300 sets out the Contractor's establishment and general obligations including:

- **Programme submission** (Clause 1301): Contractor to submit a detailed programme within the period stated in the contract. The programme must show the sequence and timing of all activities, resource allocation, and critical path.
- **Method statements** (Clause 1303): Contractor to submit method statements for key activities before commencing work. The RE reviews for compliance with the Standard Specification.
- **Insurance** (Clause 1306): As required by FIDIC and Particular Conditions.
- **Site facilities** (Clause 1307): Contractor to provide site offices, stores, workshops, and laboratory as specified in the contract.
- **Environmental management** (cross-reference Series 9000): NEMA compliance is mandatory. The Contractor must implement the approved Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP).

### M.2.2 Engineer's Supervision Responsibilities (Standard Specification)

The Engineer (through the RE team) is responsible for:

**(a) Quality assurance:**
- Monitoring compliance with the Standard Specification
- Independent testing per Section 1700 (Testing of Materials and Workmanship)
- Quality control assessment per Section 1800
- Hold points: Engineer's approval required before proceeding to next layer (Section 1730, Clause 1730(a)) -- "Work on layers shall in no circumstances commence until the preceding layer and been approved and accepted by the Engineer in writing"
- Witness points: Engineer to inspect formation, pavement layers, and structural concrete before covering

**(b) Progress monitoring:**
- S-curve comparison (planned vs actual)
- Monthly progress reports to the Employer (KeNHA/KeRRA/KURA/County)
- Programme recovery notices under Sub-Clause 8.3 when Contractor is behind programme

**(c) Safety and environmental compliance:**
- Section 9200: Occupational Health and Safety requirements
- Section 9100: Environmental Protection and Waste Disposal
- Section 9300: Road Safety Awareness during construction

**(d) Monthly reporting requirements:**
The RE compiles monthly progress reports covering:
- Physical progress (overall percentage, by activity)
- Financial progress (cumulative certified vs contract value)
- Quality status (NCRs open/closed, test results summary)
- Programme status (planned vs actual, critical path analysis)
- Safety statistics (incidents, near-misses, PPE compliance)
- Environmental compliance (ESMP monitoring, NEMA requirements)
- Community/social issues
- Decisions required from the Engineer or Employer

### M.2.3 Measurement and Certification Procedures

Payment certification follows the WSCM Part 3 BoQ format (linked to Skill #44 and Skill #51 Kenya modules):

1. **Measurement:** RE team measures quantities of completed work against the BoQ items per Standard Specification measurement rules.
2. **Interim Payment Certificate (IPC):** Contractor submits Statement per Sub-Clause 14.3. RE verifies measurements, Engineer issues IPC per Sub-Clause 14.6 within 28 days.
3. **Retention:** Typically 10% retention up to a limit of 5% of contract value (verify against Particular Conditions).
4. **WSCM Part 3 alignment:** All measurements and payments follow the Standard Specification series structure (Series 1000-9000) as detailed in the Skill #51 Kenya module.

### M.2.4 Non-Conformance Procedures

When work does not meet the Standard Specification requirements, the following options apply (same option range as established in Phase 05):

| Option | When Applied | Authority |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **(a) Additional testing** | Marginal result, need more data | RE (within delegation) |
| **(b) Accept with reduced payment** | Minor non-conformance, no structural concern | Engineer's determination |
| **(c) Rework** | Non-conformance can be corrected in place | RE instruction |
| **(d) Remove and replace** | Significant non-conformance, structural concern | Engineer's instruction |

Non-conformance reports (NCRs) must document: specification requirement, test result/observation, affected location and quantity, and proposed remedial action.

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Step 2a: Kenya project context**
```
Is the project donor-funded (World Bank, AfDB, JICA, EU)?
  |-- YES --> Additional reporting requirements (quarterly reports, safeguards monitoring)
  |         Donor supervision missions require specific data preparation
  |         Environmental and Social Framework (ESF) compliance if World Bank
  +-- NO (GoK funded) --> Standard KeNHA/KeRRA/KURA reporting
                          May have more flexible reporting requirements
                          But may also have cash flow delays (government budget)
```

**Step 3a: Laboratory and testing access**
```
Is a site laboratory available?
  |-- YES --> Routine testing on site per Section 1700
  |         (grading, Atterberg, compaction, density, concrete cubes)
  |         Send specialist tests to MTRD laboratory or accredited lab
  +-- NO --> Rely on field testing methods (DCP, nuclear gauge)
             Arrange mobile lab or send samples to nearest accredited laboratory
             Flag delays in test results as risk to progress
             NEMA-accredited laboratories for environmental monitoring
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Seasonal constraints:** Kenya has two rainy seasons -- the "long rains" (March-May) and "short rains" (October-December). Earthworks, subgrade preparation, and surfacing operations are significantly affected during these periods. The RE should ensure the programme accounts for seasonal constraints and monitor actual rainfall against assumptions.

**Materials Testing and Research Division (MTRD):** The State Department for Roads maintains the MTRD laboratory on Machakos Road, Nairobi (Industrial Area). MTRD provides verification testing and specialist tests. For projects distant from Nairobi, accredited private laboratories may be used with Engineer's approval.

**Contractor capacity in Kenya:** The NCA registration system (NCA 1-8) provides an indication of contractor capacity. Large international contractors (NCA 1-2) typically have strong QA systems. Smaller local contractors (NCA 5-8) may require more intensive supervision and guidance. The RE should assess contractor capacity early and adjust supervision intensity accordingly.

**County road projects:** County-managed road projects may have different supervision arrangements, potentially with less experienced supervision teams. The Standard Specification applies equally, but enforcement may require additional effort.

**Community engagement:** Road construction in Kenya often generates community expectations. Community access and land acquisition issues can cause delays. The RE should actively monitor the Contractor's community liaison activities and coordinate with the Employer's social team.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Not enforcing hold points per Section 1730:** The Standard Specification requires Engineer's written approval before proceeding to the next layer. Contractors sometimes proceed without approval -- the RE must enforce this consistently.
2. **Underestimating rainy season impact:** Both rainy seasons can significantly reduce earthworks productivity. Programme recovery often requires extended working hours in dry periods.
3. **Not using the Standard Specification Section 1800 QC framework:** Section 1800 provides a statistical quality control system with lot-based assessment. Some supervision teams revert to ad-hoc testing instead of applying the systematic approach.
4. **Inadequate documentation of verbal instructions:** Same risk as any FIDIC project -- all instructions must be in writing per Sub-Clause 3.3. Verbal comments during site walks can be misinterpreted as instructions.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Expected | Unit | Standard Reference | Action if Violated |
|-----------|----------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Monthly progress report | Submitted within 10 working days of month end | date | KeNHA practice | Late submission delays Employer review |
| IPC recommendation | Within 14 days of receiving Statement | days | RE processing time | Late recommendation delays Engineer's 28-day IPC deadline |
| NCR response | Contractor responds within 7 days | days | Good practice | Escalate if no response within 14 days |
| Hold point approval | Before next layer commences | -- | Std Spec Section 1730 | STOP work if proceeding without approval |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** RE delegation letter is in place before commencing supervision
- **Standard:** FIDIC Sub-Clause 3.2
- **Pass condition:** Written delegation received by both Employer and Contractor
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- No delegation letter. RE authority undefined.

- **Check:** Site laboratory is operational
- **Standard:** Standard Specification Section 1706
- **Pass condition:** Lab equipped, calibrated, staffed, and operational before construction starts
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Site lab not operational. Quality assurance compromised.

- **Check:** Contractor's Quality Control management system submitted
- **Standard:** Standard Specification Section 1701
- **Pass condition:** QC system approved by Engineer before construction starts
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- QC system not submitted. Request submission and approval before construction proceeds.

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

The Standard Specification states (Preface): "Users of the manual are expected to follow the standards set there-in and seek approval of the Ministry should any departures be warranted." Departures from the Standard Specification require Ministry approval through the Employer (KeNHA/KeRRA/KURA).

## M.6 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Suggested Action |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Payment > 90 days overdue | Government cash flow issue | SENIOR REVIEW -- IPC payment [X] days overdue. Contractor may exercise rights under Sub-Clause 16.1. Escalate to KeNHA/KeRRA/KURA Project Manager. |
| Contractor proceeding without hold point approval | Violation of Section 1730 | IMMEDIATE ACTION -- Stop work. Issue formal instruction. Placed material may need to be tested and assessed for acceptance. |
| No site laboratory on major project | Quality assurance compromised | SENIOR REVIEW -- Site laboratory required per Section 1706. All testing delayed. Flag to Employer. |
| Safety incident (reportable) | Occupational Safety per Section 9200 | SENIOR REVIEW -- Report incident per Section 9200 requirements. Notify Employer. Investigate root cause. |
| Community disruption or blockade | Programme and safety impact | SENIOR REVIEW -- Community issue at Km [X]. Coordinate with Employer social/community team. |
| NEMA non-compliance | Environmental regulatory risk | SENIOR REVIEW -- NEMA requirements not being met. Risk of stop order from NEMA. Require Contractor to comply immediately. |

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: Monthly Supervision Report for a KeNHA Road Construction Project

**Project:** Construction of 30 km Class B Road, Isiolo-Marsabit Corridor, KeNHA, AfDB funded, KES 6.5 billion.

**Reporting period:** Month 10 (March 2026)

**Report Summary:**

```
============================================================
MONTHLY PROGRESS REPORT -- ISIOLO-MARSABIT ROAD
Supervision Period: March 2026
Contract Time: 30 months (Month 10 of 30)
============================================================

PROGRESS SUMMARY
----------------
Overall physical progress: 28% (planned: 32%)
Programme status: Behind by approximately 4 weeks
Cause of slippage: Delayed borrow pit approval (NEMA EIA process)
                   and heavier than expected long rains start

Key activities this month:
- Earthworks: Km 5-12 fill placement 85% complete
- Sub-base: Km 0-5 placed and tested (passed Section 1730 approval)
- Base course: Km 0-3 ongoing (crushed aggregate from Nanyuki quarry)
- Drainage: 5 of 18 culverts completed
- Structures: Km 8 box culvert formwork in progress

QUALITY STATUS
--------------
NCRs open: 1
  NCR-008: Base course grading at Km 2+400 outside envelope
           (Section 4300 requirement). Additional testing ordered
           at 100m intervals. Results pending.
NCRs closed this period: 2
  NCR-006: Sub-base compaction at Km 4 -- reworked and retested.
           Field density now 96% MDD AASHTO T180 (spec 95%). CLOSED.
  NCR-007: Fill material PI at Km 9 -- material from approved
           source with PI=18, meets spec PI<=20. CLOSED.

Hold point approvals this period: 3
  - Sub-base Km 3-5: APPROVED (Section 1730 compliance confirmed)
  - Formation Km 10-12: APPROVED
  - Culvert backfill Km 6: APPROVED

Laboratory: Site lab operational. 42 tests completed this month
(CBR, PI, grading, MDD, field density). 4 samples sent to MTRD
(Marshall stability for AC mix design, binder tests).

PAYMENT STATUS
--------------
IPC #5 recommended amount: KES 285 million
Cumulative certified to date: KES 1,650 million (25% of Contract)
Contract Price: KES 6,500 million
Retention held: KES 82.5 million

SAFETY
------
Incidents: 0 this period
PPE compliance: 92% (improved from 85% after site safety briefing)
Safety induction: All new workers inducted before starting work

ENVIRONMENTAL (NEMA COMPLIANCE)
-------------------------------
ESMP monitoring: On track
Borrow pit Km 15: NEMA EIA approval received 28 March.
                  Exploitation can commence in April.
Dust management: Water bowser operational on active sections.
Waste disposal: Compliant -- designated disposal site in use.

COMMUNITY
---------
Community Access Road at Km 7: Maintained per contract requirement.
Grievances: 1 received (noise complaint from adjacent settlement
at Km 11). Night work suspended at this location.

DECISIONS REQUIRED FROM ENGINEER
---------------------------------
1. NCR-008: Approve additional testing protocol for base course
   grading at Km 2+400.
2. AC mix design: MTRD Marshall test results expected Week 2 of
   April -- approve trial section location and programme.
============================================================
```
