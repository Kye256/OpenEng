### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**UNRA (Uganda National Roads Authority):** The primary Employer for national trunk road projects. UNRA appoints a supervision consulting firm (the Engineer) under a separate services contract. The Engineer deploys a site-based team led by the Resident Engineer. UNRA has a Project Manager on their side who receives reports and liaises with the supervision team.

**Supervision Consultant:** Typically an international or regional consulting engineering firm, often in joint venture with a local firm. The firm is appointed as the Engineer under the works contract (FIDIC Red Book). The firm's Team Leader or Project Director acts as the Engineer, and the site-based RE acts as the Engineer's Representative per Sub-Clause 3.2.

**MoWT (Ministry of Works and Transport):** Provides technical standards (Road Design Manual, General Specifications). MoWT does not have a separate supervision manual -- supervision practice is based on FIDIC contract administration requirements, the General Specifications, and the Terms of Reference for the supervision consultant.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

There is no codified Uganda supervision standard. Practice is based on:
- FIDIC Red Book 1999 (contract administration framework)
- UNRA Standard Terms of Reference for supervision consultants (scope and deliverables)
- MoWT General Specifications (technical compliance requirements)
- Professional practice and industry norms

The supervision approach is pragmatic -- formal procedures coexist with practical challenges including remote site locations, limited laboratory access, and variable contractor capacity.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Supervision Team Composition (Typical UNRA Project)

A typical UNRA road project supervision team includes:

| Role | Responsibility | Typical Deployment |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Resident Engineer (RE) | Overall site supervision, contract administration | Full-time site-based |
| Deputy RE | Assists RE, acts during RE absence | Full-time site-based |
| Materials Engineer | Quality control, material testing oversight | Full-time site-based |
| Survey Engineer | Setting out verification, measurement | Full-time site-based |
| Environmental Officer | ESMP monitoring, community liaison | Full-time or part-time |
| Quantity Surveyor | Measurement, payment certification support | Full-time site-based |
| Safety Officer | Site safety monitoring | Part-time (for smaller projects) |
| Laboratory Technician(s) | Field and laboratory testing | Full-time site-based (at site lab) |

For World Bank-funded projects, the Terms of Reference may also require a Social Safeguards Specialist and a Gender-Based Violence (GBV) Specialist.

### M.2.2 Reporting Structure

```
UNRA Project Manager
  |-- Receives monthly progress reports
  |-- Approves IPCs (after Engineer certification)
  |-- Approves variations (above RE authority)
  +-- Liaison with World Bank (if donor-funded)

Engineer (consulting firm HQ)
  |-- Makes determinations under Sub-Clause 3.5
  |-- Issues IPCs (Sub-Clause 14.6)
  |-- Approves variations within authority
  +-- Reviews RE's monthly reports

Resident Engineer (site-based)
  |-- Daily supervision (site diary)
  |-- Weekly technical meetings (chairs)
  |-- Monthly progress reports (compiles)
  |-- Measurement verification (for IPC)
  +-- Quality assurance oversight (with Materials Engineer)
```

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Step 2a: Uganda project context**
```
Is the project World Bank / donor funded?
  |-- YES --> Additional reporting requirements (quarterly reports, safeguards monitoring)
  |         World Bank supervision missions (twice yearly) require specific data
  |         Environmental and social compliance reporting more stringent
  +-- NO (GoU funded) --> Standard UNRA reporting
                          May have less stringent safeguards monitoring
                          BUT may have more cash flow delays (government budget)
```

**Step 3a: Laboratory and testing access**
```
Is a site laboratory available?
  |-- YES --> Routine testing on site (field density, compaction, grading, concrete cubes)
  |         Send samples to UNRA central lab or accredited lab for specialist tests
  +-- NO --> Rely on field testing methods (DCP, sand replacement, nuclear gauge)
             Arrange mobile lab or send all samples to nearest laboratory
             Flag delays in test results as risk to progress
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Remote site challenges:** Many Uganda road projects are in remote locations with limited infrastructure. The RE must plan for: accommodation for supervision team, reliable communication (mobile network coverage is variable), access during rainy season (some roads become impassable), and power supply for site laboratory equipment.

**Contractor capacity varies significantly.** International contractors on large UNRA projects generally have strong QA systems. Local contractors on smaller projects may need more guidance on documentation, testing procedures, and programme management. The RE should assess contractor capacity early and adjust supervision intensity accordingly.

**Seasonal constraints:** Uganda has two rainy seasons (March-May and September-November). Earthworks, subgrade preparation, and surfacing operations are significantly affected. The RE should ensure the programme accounts for seasonal constraints and monitor actual weather against the assumptions in the programme.

**Community engagement is critical.** Road construction in Uganda often generates community expectations and concerns (land acquisition, employment, access disruption, environmental impacts). The RE should actively monitor the Contractor's community liaison activities and escalate unresolved grievances.

**UNRA central laboratory:** Located in Kampala. Used for verification testing and specialist tests not available at site laboratories. Turn-around time is typically 2-4 weeks, which can delay material source approvals.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Underestimating rainy season impact.** Earthworks progress during the rainy season can drop to near zero. Programme recovery often requires double-shift operations in dry periods.
2. **Relying solely on field testing without laboratory verification.** DCP and nuclear gauge results should be periodically verified against laboratory CBR and sand replacement density tests.
3. **Not adapting supervision intensity to contractor capacity.** A weak contractor needs more frequent inspections and closer monitoring, not less.
4. **Ignoring community grievances.** Unresolved community issues can escalate to road blockages and security incidents. Early engagement prevents escalation.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Expected | Unit | Standard Reference | Action if Violated |
|-----------|----------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Monthly progress report | Submitted by 10th of following month | date | UNRA ToR | Late submission delays UNRA review and payment |
| IPC recommendation | Within 14 days of receiving Statement | days | RE processing time | Late recommendation delays Engineer's 28-day IPC deadline |
| NCR response | Contractor responds within 7 days | days | Good practice | Escalate if no response within 14 days |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** RE delegation letter is in place before commencing supervision
- **Standard:** FIDIC Sub-Clause 3.2
- **Pass condition:** Written delegation received by both Employer and Contractor
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- No delegation letter. RE authority undefined.

- **Check:** Site laboratory is operational (for projects requiring one)
- **Standard:** UNRA ToR
- **Pass condition:** Lab equipped, calibrated, staffed, and operational before construction starts
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Site lab not operational. Quality assurance compromised.

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

No formal departures process for supervision procedures. The supervision consultant's ToR defines the scope and intensity of supervision. Any changes to supervision arrangements should be agreed between the Engineer, UNRA, and (for World Bank projects) the World Bank through implementation support missions.

## M.6 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Suggested Action |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Payment > 90 days overdue | Government cash flow issue | SENIOR REVIEW -- IPC payment [X] days overdue. Contractor may exercise rights under Sub-Clause 16.1. Escalate to UNRA Project Manager. |
| Contractor workforce below 50% of mobilised | Possible demobilisation or financial distress | SENIOR REVIEW -- Contractor workforce reduced significantly. Assess whether Contractor is abandoning works. |
| No laboratory facilities available | Quality assurance compromised | NOTE -- No laboratory available for [test type]. All testing delayed by [estimated time] for samples sent to [laboratory location]. |
| Land acquisition incomplete at site | Employer obligation (Sub-Clause 2.1) | NOTE -- Land acquisition outstanding at Km [X]-[Y]. Contractor cannot access. Potential claim under Sub-Clause 8.4(e). |
| Community blockade of works | Security and programme impact | SENIOR REVIEW -- Community blockade at Km [X]. Immediate liaison required. Report to UNRA and (if World Bank) to safeguards team. |

## M.7 Worked Examples -- Uganda

### Example 1: Monthly Supervision Report for UNRA Road Project

**Project:** Rehabilitation of Trunk Road Masaka-Bukakata (45 km), UNRA, World Bank funded, UGX 85 billion.

**Reporting period:** Month 14 (February 2026)

**Report Summary:**

```
============================================================
MONTHLY PROGRESS REPORT -- MASAKA-BUKAKATA ROAD
Supervision Period: February 2026
Contract Time: 24 months (Month 14 of 24)
============================================================

PROGRESS SUMMARY
----------------
Overall physical progress: 65% (planned: 70%)
Programme status: Behind by approximately 3 weeks
Cause of slippage: Equipment breakdown (grader out of service for 2 weeks)
                   and delayed material source approval (Km 22 borrow pit)

Key activities this month:
- Earthworks: Km 28-32 completed (fill placement and compaction)
- Sub-base: Km 15-22 placed and tested (passed field density)
- Base course: Km 8-15 ongoing (crusher production adequate)
- Culverts: 3 of 5 remaining culverts completed
- Bridge approaches: Km 18 bridge -- approach embankment 80% complete

QUALITY STATUS
--------------
NCRs open: 2
  NCR-014: Base course CBR test at Km 12 returned 72% (specification
           requires 80%). Additional testing at closer spacing ordered.
           Contractor to rework 200m section if confirmed below spec.
  NCR-015: Culvert at Km 29 -- concrete cube strength at 7 days:
           16 N/mm2 for Class 25 concrete (expected 17-18 N/mm2).
           Marginal -- monitoring 28-day result before decision.
NCRs closed this period: 1
  NCR-013: Subgrade compaction at Km 30 -- reworked and retested.
           Field density now 96% MDD (spec 95%). CLOSED.

Material source approvals pending: 1 (Km 22 borrow pit -- samples
sent to UNRA central lab, results expected in 2 weeks)

PAYMENT STATUS
--------------
IPC #7 recommended amount: UGX 2.1 billion
Cumulative certified to date: UGX 52.3 billion (61% of Contract Price)
Contract Price: UGX 85 billion
Retention held: UGX 2.6 billion

SAFETY
------
Incidents: 1 minor (worker struck by falling timber during formwork
removal -- first aid treatment, no lost time)
PPE compliance: 85% (improvement from 75% last month after written
instruction to Contractor)

ENVIRONMENTAL
-------------
Erosion control: Adequate at most locations. Km 25 cut slope needs
additional erosion protection -- instruction issued to Contractor.
Dust management: Water bowser operational. No complaints this period.
Community: 2 grievances received (noise during night works at Km 16,
access disruption at Km 20). Both addressed through community meeting.

DECISIONS REQUIRED FROM ENGINEER
---------------------------------
1. NCR-014: If additional testing confirms base course failure at
   Km 12, approve removal and replacement vs acceptance with
   reduced payment?
2. Km 22 borrow pit: Approve interim use of existing approved
   source (longer haul, higher cost to Contractor) while awaiting
   lab results?
============================================================
```

### Example 2: Contractor Behind Programme -- RE Assessment and Action

**Project:** Any UNRA road project, Month 10 of 18-month contract.

**Scenario:** Contractor's actual progress is 45% against planned 52% -- behind by approximately 3 weeks on the critical path (earthworks and pavement layers). The RE assesses the cause and determines appropriate action.

**Assessment:**

Step 1: Review S-curve -- actual vs planned divergence started in Month 8.

Step 2: Assess cause:
- Contractor's grader broke down in Month 8 (2 weeks out of service) -- this is within the Contractor's control (equipment maintenance is the Contractor's responsibility).
- Replacement grader brought from another project in Week 3 of Month 8.
- Crusher output dropped in Month 9 due to worn liners (2-week delay in replacement parts) -- also within Contractor's control.
- No Employer-caused delays identified. No exceptional weather.

Step 3: Determine action:
- The delay is within the Contractor's control. There is no entitlement to extension of time under Sub-Clause 8.4.
- Issue written notice to Contractor per Sub-Clause 8.3 requiring submission of an updated programme showing how the Contractor intends to recover the delay.
- Include in the notice a reminder that delay damages under Sub-Clause 8.7 may apply if the Time for Completion is not met.
- Document all findings in the site diary and monthly progress report.

**RE's Letter to Contractor (summary):**
```
Subject: Programme Recovery -- Notice under Sub-Clause 8.3

Dear [Contractor's Representative],

The current physical progress of 45% is approximately 3 weeks behind
the approved programme (planned: 52%). The delay is attributable to
equipment breakdowns (grader, crusher) which are within your
responsibility.

In accordance with Sub-Clause 8.3, you are requested to submit a
revised programme within 14 days showing:
(a) the measures you intend to take to recover the delay
(b) the revised sequence and timing of activities
(c) additional resources to be deployed

Please note that failure to complete the Works within the Time for
Completion may result in delay damages under Sub-Clause 8.7.

[RE signature]
```

### Example 3: Unapproved Borrow Material -- Edge Case

**Project:** UNRA road project, earthworks phase.

**Scenario:** During a routine site inspection, the RE discovers that the Contractor is placing fill material from a borrow pit that has not been submitted for approval. The material appears to be red laterite but no test results are available. Approximately 2,000 m3 has already been placed and compacted over a 500m section.

**RE's Response:**

Step 1: Immediate action
- Issue stop work instruction for fill placement at this location.
- Record discovery in site diary with photographs, chainage, and estimated quantity.
- Issue formal instruction (numbered) requiring the Contractor to:
  (a) immediately cease using material from the unapproved source
  (b) submit source approval documentation including test results
  (c) provide samples of the placed material for testing

Step 2: Issue NCR (NCR-016)
- Non-conformance: Material placed from unapproved source in contravention of Specification Section [X] (material source approval requirement).
- Specification requirement: All material sources must be approved by the Engineer before use.
- Quantity affected: approximately 2,000 m3, Km 18+200 to Km 18+700.

Step 3: Testing (reference Skill #55)
- Take samples of the placed material and the source.
- Test for: CBR (soaked), grading, Atterberg limits (PI, LL), compaction characteristics (Modified AASHTO), field density of placed material.
- Compare results against specification for selected fill (MoWT General Specifications).

Step 4: Decision based on test results
```
Test results received:
  |-- Material PASSES specification
  |   --> Approve source retrospectively
  |   --> Accept placed material
  |   --> Issue formal warning to Contractor (repeat offence = more serious consequences)
  |   --> Close NCR with corrective action: "Approved retrospectively. Contractor warned."
  |
  |-- Material FAILS specification
  |   --> Require removal and replacement with approved material
  |   --> Cost of removal and replacement borne by Contractor
  |   --> NCR closed only after replacement material tested and approved
  |   --> Consider impact on programme (delay is Contractor's responsibility)
  |
  +-- Material MARGINAL (close to specification limits)
      --> Assess risk: subgrade fill is less critical than base course
      --> Consult Engineer: accept with reduced payment or require replacement?
      --> This decision requires Engineer's determination -- may exceed RE authority
```
