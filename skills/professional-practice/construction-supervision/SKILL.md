## Purpose

Guide the Resident Engineer (RE) through day-to-day construction supervision duties on a road project administered under a FIDIC contract. This skill covers site diary management, site meeting procedures, progress monitoring, quality assurance oversight, payment certification workflow, safety and environmental compliance, and contractor performance assessment. It is a procedural skill based on professional practice rather than a specific design standard.

## When to Use This Skill

- **Use when:** Advising the RE or supervision team on daily, weekly, or monthly supervision duties during road construction. Applicable during active construction phase under a FIDIC contract.
- **Do not use when:** Administering contract clauses (use Skill #42 FIDIC Red Book). Not for detailed material testing procedures (use Skill #55 Material Testing & QC). Not for measurement and payment certification details (use Skill #44 Measurement & Payment).
- **Prerequisite skills:** Skill #42 (FIDIC Red Book) for contract administration framework. Skill #44 (Measurement & Payment) for IPC procedures. Skill #55 (Material Testing & QC) for quality testing requirements.

## 1. Knowledge (Universal)

### 1.1 Key Concepts

**The Resident Engineer (RE) Role:** The RE is the Engineer's Representative on site, appointed per Sub-Clause 3.2 of FIDIC Red Book 1999. The Engineer delegates specific duties and authority to the RE through a formal delegation letter. The RE acts within the scope of this delegated authority -- actions beyond the delegation require the Engineer's direct involvement. The RE is the primary point of contact between the supervision team and the Contractor on site.

**Delegated Authority (Sub-Clause 3.1 / 3.2):** The Engineer may delegate authority to the RE to carry out duties including: inspecting works, issuing instructions (within authority), reviewing Contractor submissions, measuring completed work, and certifying quality. The delegation must be in writing, and both the Employer and Contractor receive copies. Critically, the RE may NOT have authority to: issue variation orders above a threshold, determine claims, or make Sub-Clause 3.5 determinations unless specifically delegated.

**Key Supervision Duties:**

**(1) Site Diary:** A daily record maintained by the RE documenting: weather conditions (rainfall, temperature), Contractor's workforce numbers by trade, equipment deployed and working status, work activities and progress, visitors to site, instructions issued, incidents or accidents, material deliveries, and any other significant events. The site diary is a critical contractual document -- it provides contemporaneous evidence in the event of claims or disputes. It should be factual, objective, and recorded on the day.

**(2) Site Meetings:** Two principal types:
- **Weekly technical meetings** (sometimes called progress meetings): Attended by RE, Contractor's site agent, and key technical staff. Agenda covers: review of previous minutes, progress against programme, technical issues and solutions, quality matters, safety matters, upcoming work, any other business. Minutes recorded and distributed within 48 hours.
- **Monthly management meetings:** Attended by Engineer (or RE), senior Contractor management, and sometimes Employer representatives. Agenda covers: overall programme status, financial status (payments, variations, claims), major issues, decisions required. More strategic than weekly meetings.

**(3) Progress Monitoring:** The RE monitors construction progress against the Contractor's approved programme (Sub-Clause 8.3). Key tools:
- **S-curve:** Plots cumulative physical progress (percentage) and/or financial progress against time. Planned vs actual curves show whether the project is ahead, on schedule, or behind.
- **Critical path review:** Identifies which activities are on the critical path and whether delays to those activities will affect the overall completion date.
- **Early warning:** The RE should provide early warning when progress falls behind programme, before formal contractual notices become necessary.
- **Monthly progress reports:** Compiled by the RE summarising physical progress (by work item), financial progress (cumulative payments vs budget), quality status, safety record, environmental compliance, weather data, and issues/decisions required.

**(4) Quality Assurance Oversight:** The RE ensures that the Contractor's quality management system is functioning and that materials and workmanship meet specification requirements. This includes:
- **Material source approval:** Reviewing and approving material sources before use (reference Skill #55 for testing requirements).
- **Inspection and testing plan (ITP):** Reviewing the Contractor's ITP and verifying compliance.
- **Hold points:** Activities where work cannot proceed until the RE has inspected and approved (e.g., subgrade approval before placing sub-base).
- **Witness points:** Activities where the RE is invited to observe (testing, concrete pours) but work can proceed if the RE does not attend.
- **Non-conformance reporting (NCR):** When work or materials fail to meet specifications, a formal NCR is issued documenting: the non-conformance, the specification requirement, the proposed remedial action, and the deadline for correction.

**(5) Payment Certification:** The RE verifies site measurements that form the basis of interim payment certificates (IPCs). This involves:
- Joint measurement with the Contractor (wherever practical).
- Cross-checking claimed quantities against survey records and site diary.
- Verifying that work claimed for payment has passed quality requirements.
- Feeding verified measurements into the IPC process (see Skill #44 for detailed procedures).

**(6) Safety:** The RE monitors the Contractor's compliance with safety requirements including:
- Contractor's site-specific safety plan (submitted and approved before works commence).
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) compliance.
- Traffic management for works on or near live roads.
- Incident reporting and investigation.
- The RE is not responsible for the Contractor's safety management (the Contractor has this obligation under FIDIC Sub-Clause 4.8), but the RE must report serious deficiencies.

**(7) Environmental Compliance:** The RE monitors the Contractor's compliance with the Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP), including:
- Erosion and sediment control at the works.
- Dust and noise management near sensitive receptors.
- Waste disposal and materials storage.
- Protection of water courses, vegetation, and cultural heritage sites.
- Community liaison and grievance management.

### 1.2 Universal Formulas

No formulas -- this is a procedural/judgment skill.

### 1.3 Figures & Diagrams

**S-Curve:** A plot with time (months) on the x-axis and cumulative progress (%) on the y-axis. The planned progress line is typically an S-shape (slow start, acceleration, plateau at completion). The actual progress line is plotted alongside. The gap between planned and actual at any point in time shows whether the project is ahead or behind programme.

**Progress Monitoring Dashboard:** Conceptual layout showing: overall % complete, S-curve chart, key milestones (achieved/remaining), critical issues, and next month's planned activities.

## 2. Reasoning (Universal)

### 2.1 Decision Logic

```
Step 1: Determine contract form and RE's delegated authority
  --> Load country module for institutional context
  --> Review delegation letter for authority limits
  --> If unclear: seek clarification from Engineer before acting

Step 2: Identify supervision scenario
  |-- (A) Contractor behind programme --> Decision Tree A
  |-- (B) Quality issue identified --> Decision Tree B
  |-- (C) Monthly payment processing --> Decision Tree C
  |-- (D) Safety concern --> Decision Tree D
  |-- (E) Environmental non-compliance --> Decision Tree E
  +-- (F) Routine daily supervision --> Follow Section 7 Procedure
```

**Decision Tree A -- Contractor Behind Programme:**
```
A1: Assess programme status
  --> Compare actual progress against approved programme
  --> Is the Contractor behind on critical path activities?
    |-- NO (behind on non-critical activities) --> Monitor, note in progress report
    +-- YES (behind on critical path) --> Proceed to A2

A2: Assess cause of delay
  --> Is the cause within the Contractor's control?
    |-- YES (e.g., equipment breakdown, insufficient resources, poor planning)
    |   --> Issue written notice requiring updated programme per Sub-Clause 8.3
    |   --> If persistent: warn of potential delay damages (Sub-Clause 8.7)
    |   --> Document in site diary and progress report
    +-- NO (e.g., Employer's delayed access, design changes, force majeure)
        --> Document the cause thoroughly (contemporaneous records critical)
        --> Advise Contractor to submit notice under Sub-Clause 20.1 within 28 days
        --> Assess time extension entitlement per Sub-Clause 8.4
        --> Refer to Skill #42 Decision Tree B for time extension assessment
        --> Refer to Skill #45 for claims procedure
```

**Decision Tree B -- Quality Issue Identified:**
```
B1: Nature of quality issue
  |-- Material fails specification --> proceed to B2
  |-- Workmanship deficient --> proceed to B2
  +-- Unapproved material source --> STOP work for that material, proceed to B3

B2: Issue Non-Conformance Report (NCR)
  --> Document: what failed, which specification requirement, test results (if any)
  --> Require Contractor to propose remedial action within specified timeframe
  --> Assess proposed remedy:
    |-- (a) Accept remedy --> monitor correction, verify upon completion
    |-- (b) Reject remedy --> require revised proposal
    +-- (c) If material: test to determine extent of non-conformance
        --> Reference Skill #55 for testing requirements and acceptance criteria

B3: Unapproved material source
  --> Issue stop work instruction for that activity
  --> Require Contractor to submit source approval documentation
  --> Require testing of material already placed (reference Skill #55)
  --> Options based on test results:
    |-- Material passes specification --> approve source retrospectively, issue warning
    |-- Material fails specification --> require removal and replacement
    +-- Material marginal --> assess risk, may accept with reduced payment (with Engineer approval)
```

**Decision Tree C -- Monthly Payment Processing:**
```
C1: Contractor submits monthly Statement
  --> Verify Statement completeness (Sub-Clause 14.3)
  --> Cross-check claimed quantities against:
    |-- Site measurement records (joint measurements where done)
    |-- Survey data and as-built records
    |-- Site diary (work activities recorded)
    +-- Quality records (only pay for work that passes quality)

C2: Prepare measurement verification
  --> Document adjustments with reasons
  --> Deduct for defective work pending correction
  --> Apply retention percentage per contract
  --> Deduct advance payment repayment instalment

C3: Recommend for IPC (feed into Skill #44 process)
  --> Submit verified measurements and recommended certified amount to Engineer
  --> Engineer issues IPC within 28 days of receiving Contractor's Statement (Sub-Clause 14.6)
```

**Decision Tree D -- Safety Concern:**
```
D1: Severity assessment
  |-- Imminent danger to life --> immediate stop work instruction for affected area
  |-- Serious concern (PPE, traffic management) --> written instruction to Contractor to rectify
  +-- Minor observation --> note in site diary, raise at next site meeting

D2: Incident occurred
  --> Ensure immediate response (first aid, secure area)
  --> Record in site diary with full details
  --> Require Contractor's incident report within 24 hours
  --> Report to Engineer and (if required) to authorities
  --> Review Contractor's root cause analysis and corrective actions
```

**Decision Tree E -- Environmental Non-Compliance:**
```
E1: Severity assessment
  |-- Pollution event (oil spill, sediment to watercourse) --> immediate action required
  |-- Ongoing non-compliance (dust, noise, erosion) --> written instruction to rectify
  +-- Minor observation --> note in site diary

E2: Action
  --> Issue written instruction referencing ESMP requirement
  --> Require Contractor to submit corrective action plan
  --> Monitor implementation
  --> Report in monthly progress report
  --> If persistent: escalate to Engineer and Employer
```

### 2.2 Professional Judgment (Universal)

**The site diary is your most important tool.** In the event of a claim or dispute, the site diary is often the most relied-upon contemporaneous record. Write it as though a tribunal or arbitrator will read it years later -- factual, specific, dated, and clear.

**Written instructions, always.** Verbal instructions on site are inevitable, but they must be confirmed in writing as soon as practicable. An unconfirmed verbal instruction is contractually unenforceable and creates risk for both the Contractor and the Engineer. Use a standard instruction form with sequential numbering.

**Balance quality with programme.** Pressure to accept defective work to maintain programme is the most common ethical challenge in construction supervision. The RE must resist this pressure -- accepting defective work creates long-term liability and undermines the project's intended service life.

**The RE is not the Contractor's site manager.** The RE supervises and monitors; the Contractor manages. Over-involvement in the Contractor's operations blurs responsibility and creates liability risks for the Engineer.

### 2.3 Common Errors

1. **Not maintaining a daily site diary.** Missing a single day's record creates a gap in the contemporaneous record that cannot be reconstructed later. This is the most damaging omission in supervision.
2. **Issuing verbal instructions without written confirmation.** Verbal instructions are common on site but are contractually unenforceable. Confirm in writing within 24 hours.
3. **Accepting defective work under programme pressure.** Once accepted, the defective work becomes the Engineer's responsibility. The RE must document non-conformances and require correction.
4. **Exceeding delegated authority.** The RE must know the limits of the delegation letter. Acting beyond delegated authority (e.g., approving a large variation) can expose the Engineer and Employer to liability.
5. **Failing to provide early warning of delays.** Late identification of programme slippage reduces the options for recovery. The S-curve and critical path should be reviewed weekly.

### Default Behaviour Rules

1. **Contract documents take precedence** -- if the specification or contract defines supervision procedures, follow those.
2. **Always document assumptions** about supervision frequency, reporting requirements, or hold points.
3. **Conservative approach:** When in doubt about authority, refer to the Engineer.

## 4. Validation (Universal)

### 4.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Expected | Unit | Action if Violated |
|-----------|----------|------|--------------------|
| Site diary entries | 1 per working day | days | Missing entries are a supervision failure -- flag immediately |
| Site meeting minutes | Distributed within 48 hours | hours | Late distribution reduces effectiveness -- note and rectify |
| Progress report | Submitted within 10 days of month end | days | Late submission delays payment certification and issue resolution |
| NCR response time | Contractor responds within 7-14 days | days | Escalate if Contractor does not respond within specified timeframe |

### 4.2 Consistency Checks

- **Claimed progress vs site diary:** Work claimed for payment should be consistent with activities recorded in the site diary.
- **Quality records vs payment:** Work should not be certified for payment until quality tests have been completed and passed.
- **Programme vs actual:** S-curve deviations greater than 10% should be explained in the progress report.

The agent shall run all validation checks from the loaded country module's Section M.5 in addition to the universal checks above.

## 5. Escalation (Universal)

### 5.1 Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Reason | Suggested Action |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Programme slippage > 15% | Risk of delay to completion date | SENIOR REVIEW -- Programme slippage exceeds 15%. Assess recovery options and consider contractual notices. |
| Multiple NCRs for same issue | Systemic quality problem | SENIOR REVIEW -- Repeat non-conformance for [issue]. Contractor's QA system may be inadequate. |
| Safety incident (serious injury/fatality) | Immediate response required | SENIOR REVIEW -- Serious safety incident. Immediate reporting and investigation required. |
| RE authority unclear | Risk of acting beyond delegation | SENIOR REVIEW -- RE delegated authority unclear for [action]. Refer to Engineer. |
| Contractor claims RE instruction is a variation | Contractual and financial implications | SENIOR REVIEW -- Contractor claims instruction is a variation. Review authority and variation procedure (Skill #45). |

### 5.2 Escalation Behaviour

- **Never suppress an escalation flag.** Document and report.
- **Continue working** where possible while awaiting senior guidance.
- **Safety incidents** require immediate escalation -- do not wait for the next progress report.

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1: Establish Supervision Framework
Review the contract to identify: RE's delegated authority (Sub-Clause 3.2), specification requirements for hold points and witness points, reporting requirements, and any project-specific supervision provisions.

### Step 2: Load Country Module
Identify the jurisdiction and load the appropriate country module for institutional context (e.g., UNRA reporting requirements for Uganda).

### Step 3: Daily Supervision Routine
- Begin day with site walk-over to observe work in progress.
- Record in site diary: weather, workforce, equipment, activities, visitors, instructions, incidents.
- Inspect active work fronts for specification compliance.
- Attend hold points when called by Contractor.
- Issue written instructions as needed (sequential numbering).

### Step 4: Weekly Activities
- Chair weekly technical meeting with Contractor.
- Review programme progress (S-curve update).
- Review quality status (pending NCRs, test results).
- Review safety observations.
- Prepare and distribute meeting minutes within 48 hours.

### Step 5: Monthly Activities
- Compile monthly progress report (physical, financial, quality, safety, environmental).
- Verify Contractor's monthly Statement (quantities and values) for IPC processing.
- Attend monthly management meeting.
- Review S-curve for early warning of programme slippage.
- Update risk register.

### Step 6: Quality Assurance Activities (Ongoing)
- Review material source approval requests (reference Skill #55).
- Attend witness points for material testing.
- Review test results against acceptance criteria (reference Skill #55).
- Issue NCRs for non-conforming work or materials.
- Verify NCR closeout.

### Step 7: Produce Output
Compile reporting outputs per Section 8. Include all escalation flags and assumptions.
