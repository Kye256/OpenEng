### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads. KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads. KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**KEBS (Kenya Bureau of Standards):** The national standards body. KEBS adopts and publishes Kenya Standards (KS) which increasingly align with European Norms (EN). Kenya is transitioning from BS to KS/EN standards for construction materials. The Standard Specification (2025) references both BS and KS/EN standards, with KS/EN being the preferred standard where available.

**Standards transition note:** The Standard Specification Section 1703 states that materials shall comply with "the current edition of Specifications issued by the Kenya Bureau of Standards or other specifications relevant which may be applicable. These shall be British Standards (BS or BS EN) or American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials (AASHTO) or American Society for Testing Materials (ASTM)." This multi-standard acceptance reflects Kenya's transition from BS to KS/EN while maintaining AASHTO as an accepted alternative.

**Materials Testing and Research Division (MTRD):** The State Department for Roads maintains the MTRD laboratory on Machakos Road, Industrial Area, Nairobi. MTRD provides verification testing, specialist tests (Marshall stability, binder tests, steel tests), and arbitration testing when site results are disputed.

**Laboratory accreditation:** Kenya National Accreditation Service (KENAS) accredits testing laboratories. For construction QC, the Standard Specification (Section 1706) requires the Contractor to "provide, use and maintain on the site a suitable laboratory and adequate equipment operated by competent staff."

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The Standard Specification Section 1709 emphasises the Atterberg limits "shall be measured according to the Kenyan (KS) and British Standard (BS) procedures and utilise BS equipment." This is significant -- it mandates KS/BS procedures for Atterberg limits specifically, while allowing AASHTO for other tests. CBR testing uses the "three-point method" or other approved method (Section 1710). Compaction testing uses AASHTO T99 (2.5 kg rammer) or T180 (4.5 kg rammer) as explicitly stated in Section 1710(b).

For field density, the Sand Replacement Method is the reference (Section 1710(b)(v)), with nuclear methods accepted where approved by the Engineer. Nuclear gauge calibration requires at least 50 parallel tests with sand replacement at project start (Section 1730(c)).

### M.2.4 Kenya Test Standards Mapping

The Standard Specification references the following test standards (from Sections 1709-1733):

**Soils and Gravels (Section 1710):**
- Sieve analysis: BS 1377 - Test 7(A) with ISO sieves
- Atterberg limits: KS and BS procedures with BS equipment (Section 1709 emphasis)
- CBR: Three-point method, compacted to MDD at AASHTO T99 or T180
- Compaction: AASHTO T99 (2.5 kg rammer) or T180 (4.5 kg rammer)

**Stone, Aggregate, Sand and Filler (Section 1711):**
- Referenced standards include BS EN 933 series (sieve analysis, particle shape), BS EN 1097 series (mechanical and physical properties), and BS 812 (various parts)

**Concrete (Section 1715):**
- Cube strength testing per applicable BS/KS EN standards
- Cubes cast and cured per Section 1804(b)
- Core testing per Concrete Society Technical Report No.11 if non-compliance suspected

**Bituminous Binders (Section 1716):**
- Referenced standards include BS EN 12591 (penetration grade bitumens), BS EN 13808 (cationic emulsions)

**Bituminous Mixes (Section 1717):**
- Marshall test / Gyratory compaction test per applicable standard
- Extraction and grading per applicable standard

**Field Density (Section 1730):**
- Sand Replacement Method as primary reference (Section 1710(b)(v))
- Nuclear method: requires minimum 50 calibration tests against sand replacement for each material type

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Step 3a: Determine available testing facilities**
```
What laboratory facilities are available?
  |-- Full site laboratory (CBR press, Marshall, grading, Atterberg, compaction)
  |   --> All routine tests on site per Section 1706
  |   --> Send specialist tests (binder, steel) to MTRD or accredited lab
  |
  |-- Basic site laboratory (grading, Atterberg, compaction, cubes only)
  |   --> CBR, Marshall, and specialist tests to MTRD or accredited lab
  |   --> Plan for 2-3 week turnaround on sent-away samples
  |   --> Use DCP for interim subgrade assessment
  |
  +-- No site laboratory
      --> All testing at nearest accredited laboratory
      --> Rely on DCP and nuclear gauge for field checks
      --> Flag: quality assurance significantly compromised
      --> Escalate to Engineer/Employer per Section 1706
```

**Step 4a: Kenya-specific acceptance assessment**
```
Test result vs Standard Specification requirement:
  |-- PASS (meets or exceeds specification) --> Record, proceed to Section 1730 approval
  |
  |-- FAIL by small margin (within 5% of specification value)
  |   --> Additional testing at closer spacing per Section 1800
  |   --> If isolated: consider acceptance with reduced payment (Engineer determination)
  |   --> Consult relevant material specification section for exact requirements
  |
  +-- FAIL by significant margin (> 5% below specification)
      --> NCR and remedial action required per Section 1800
      --> For base course: likely removal and replacement
      --> For subgrade: reworking and re-compaction may suffice
      --> Engineer's determination per Section 1805
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Kenya's transition from BS to KS/EN standards:** The Standard Specification references both BS and KS/EN standards. Engineers should be aware that test results from BS and KS/EN methods may not be directly comparable in all cases. The Standard Specification specifically notes this for Atterberg limits (Section 1709) -- KS and BS procedures should be used with BS equipment, and other methods should not be used "unless proper correlation to BS has been carried out to the satisfaction of the Engineer."

**MTRD laboratory access:** Located in Nairobi's Industrial Area. Turn-around time is typically 2-3 weeks for standard tests. For projects distant from Nairobi (e.g., North Eastern Kenya), consider using KENAS-accredited private laboratories to reduce turnaround times.

**Nuclear gauge licensing:** Nuclear density gauges require licensing from the Kenya Nuclear Regulatory Authority (KNRA, formerly Radiation Protection Board). The Contractor must hold a valid licence for nuclear gauge operation. Transport between sites must follow KNRA regulations.

**Laterite gravels in Kenya:** Common pavement material, particularly for sub-base and improved subgrade. Quality varies widely -- laterite from the same borrow pit can have significantly different properties at different depths. Regular verification testing is essential, not just source approval testing.

**Highland altitude effects on concrete curing:** For projects above 2,000m (Central Highlands, Rift Valley escarpment), lower ambient temperatures may affect concrete curing rates. Cube strength results at 7 days may be lower than expected. Allow for this in the 7-day to 28-day strength estimation.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Using BS test methods when KS/EN required:** The Standard Specification is transitioning to KS/EN. For newer projects, verify which test method the Special Specification requires before testing begins.
2. **Not calibrating nuclear gauge with 50 parallel sand replacement tests:** Section 1730(c) requires at least 50 parallel tests for initial calibration. This is more onerous than some other countries' requirements. Plan calibration time into the programme.
3. **Accepting laterite gravel based on appearance alone:** Laterite properties vary widely. Always test -- visual assessment is insufficient for compliance.
4. **Not applying Section 1800 statistical QC procedures:** The Standard Specification provides a systematic lot-based quality control framework. Use it rather than ad-hoc testing and acceptance.
5. **Sending samples to MTRD without clear labelling:** Same as any laboratory -- samples must be clearly identified with project, chainage, layer, depth, date, and required tests.

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Correct compaction standard used for CBR testing
- **Standard:** Std Spec Section 1710(b)
- **Pass condition:** Base/subbase CBR at AASHTO T-180; subgrade CBR at AASHTO T-99
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- CBR tested at wrong compaction standard. Results not valid for compliance. Retest required.

- **Check:** Nuclear gauge calibrated with 50+ parallel sand replacement tests
- **Standard:** Std Spec Section 1730(c)
- **Pass condition:** 50+ calibration tests completed per material type at project start
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Nuclear gauge calibration insufficient. Complete calibration before relying on nuclear gauge results.

- **Check:** Atterberg limits tested using KS/BS procedure with BS equipment
- **Standard:** Std Spec Section 1709
- **Pass condition:** KS/BS procedure documented in test report
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Non-standard Atterberg test method. Results may not be comparable. Retest if required.

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

Acceptance of non-compliant work follows the Section 1800 Quality Control procedures. The Engineer assesses non-compliance using statistical methods (lot-based assessment) and may accept, reject, or accept with reduced payment based on the severity and extent of the non-conformance. Formal departures from the Standard Specification require Ministry approval per the Preface.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: QC Testing Programme for Granular Base Course on a Kenya Road Project

**Project:** KeNHA road construction, base course placement at Km 4+000 to Km 4+500, using graded crushed stone (Section 4300 of Standard Specification).

**Material specification (from Standard Specification / Skill #24 Kenya module):**
- CBR >= 80% (soaked, AASHTO T-180)
- PI <= 6
- LAA <= 30% (or ACV <= 21%)
- Grading: within specified envelope per Section 4300
- Field density: 98% of vibratory Hammer (crushed aggregate) per Table 1730/2

**Step 1: Source approval testing (before placement)**

Samples taken from Nanyuki quarry (40 km from site). Tests submitted to site laboratory and MTRD:

| Test | Standard | Result | Specification | Status |
|------|----------|--------|--------------|--------|
| CBR (soaked, 4-day, AASHTO T-180) | BS 1377 / AASHTO | 115% | >= 80% | PASS |
| Grading (sieve analysis) | BS EN 933-1 | Within envelope | Section 4300 envelope | PASS |
| Plasticity Index | KS/BS procedure | 3 | <= 6 | PASS |
| Liquid Limit | KS/BS procedure | 25 | -- | Recorded |
| Los Angeles Abrasion | BS EN 1097-2 | 22% | <= 30% | PASS |
| Flakiness Index | BS EN 933-3 | 18% | <= 25% | PASS |
| MDD (vib. Hammer) | BS 1377 | 2,180 kg/m3 | Reference value | Recorded |

**Decision:** Source APPROVED for base course (Section 4300).

**Step 2: Construction control testing (during placement)**

Testing frequency per Table 1708/1 (base course using crushed aggregate):
- PI, SG, Vib. Hammer, grading, flakiness: 1 sample per 5,000 m2
- MDD, ACV: 1 sample per 10,000 m2

For a 500m section at 8.5m road width = 4,250 m2:
- Minimum 1 set of PI, grading, flakiness, MDD tests required

Field density testing per Table 1730/1:
- Base course: 1 test per 500 m2
- For 4,250 m2: minimum 8.5, say **9 field density tests** required
- Absolute minimum: 6 per section

**Step 3: Field density results (9 tests at approximately 50m intervals)**

| Test # | Location | Dry Density (kg/m3) | % of vib. Hammer MDD | Spec (>= 98%) | Status |
|--------|----------|--------------------|-----------------------|---------------|--------|
| 1 | Km 4+050 | 2,145 | 98.4% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 2 | Km 4+100 | 2,158 | 99.0% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 3 | Km 4+150 | 2,136 | 98.0% | >= 98% | PASS (borderline) |
| 4 | Km 4+200 | 2,167 | 99.4% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 5 | Km 4+250 | 2,120 | 97.2% | >= 98% | **FAIL** |
| 6 | Km 4+300 | 2,152 | 98.7% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 7 | Km 4+350 | 2,163 | 99.2% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 8 | Km 4+400 | 2,149 | 98.6% | >= 98% | PASS |
| 9 | Km 4+450 | 2,171 | 99.6% | >= 98% | PASS |

**Step 4: Assessment per Section 1800**

8 of 9 tests pass (89%). 1 test fails at 97.2% (shortfall of 0.8 percentage points).

Per Section 1800 Quality Control statistical assessment:
- Assess whether the failure is isolated or indicative of a broader problem
- Test 5 at Km 4+250 is an isolated failure -- adjacent tests (Test 4 at 99.4%, Test 6 at 98.7%) pass comfortably

**Decision:** Issue NCR for Km 4+250 area. Require Contractor to:
1. Scarify and re-compact approximately 50m section (Km 4+225 to Km 4+275)
2. Re-test at 25m intervals (3 additional tests)
3. Results must achieve >= 98% of vibratory Hammer MDD before proceeding

**After reworking -- re-test results:**
- Km 4+225: 98.5% -- PASS
- Km 4+250: 99.1% -- PASS
- Km 4+275: 98.8% -- PASS

**NCR closed.** Section approved per Section 1730 -- Engineer's written approval to proceed to surfacing.
