### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**PPDA (Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority):** The regulatory body established under Section 5 of the PPDA Act 2003. Sets procurement standards, issues guidelines (including thresholds), monitors compliance, and audits procuring and disposing entities (PDEs). Issues Standard Bidding Documents that PDEs must use.

**UNRA (Uganda National Roads Authority):** A Procuring and Disposing Entity (PDE) under the PPDA Act. UNRA procures road construction, rehabilitation, and maintenance contracts. For GoU-funded projects, UNRA follows PPDA Act procedures. For donor-funded projects, UNRA follows the donor's procurement rules (with PPDA Act applying for non-procurement aspects).

**Contracts Committee:** Established under Section 27 of the PPDA Act within each PDE. Responsible for: authorising the choice of procurement method (Section 29(a)(i)), approving bidding documents, approving evaluation reports, and awarding contracts. Members are nominated by the Accounting Officer and approved by the Secretary to the Treasury.

**Procurement and Disposal Unit (PDU):** The operational unit within each PDE (Section 30-32). Manages all procurement activities except adjudication and award. Prepares bidding documents, manages bid processes, evaluates bids (through Evaluation Committees), and makes recommendations to the Contracts Committee.

**Evaluation Committee:** Appointed for each procurement (Section 37). Minimum 3 members with relevant technical skills. Must not include Contracts Committee members. Reports to the PDU.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The PPDA Act 2003 adopts a "rules-based" approach: specific methods are prescribed for specific value ranges, with open bidding as the preferred method (Section 51). Deviation from prescribed methods requires justification and, in some cases, PPDA Authority approval. The Act emphasises transparency, fairness, and value for money (Sections 44-52).

**Key principle (Section 4):** Where the PPDA Act conflicts with an international agreement (including donor financing agreements), the provisions of the international agreement prevail. This is the legal basis for donor procurement rules overriding PPDA procedures on donor-funded projects.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 PPDA Method Thresholds (GoU-Funded Works)

**Table: PPDA Thresholds for Works** --> `tables/ppda_thresholds.json`

From PPDA Guideline 1/2014 (Thresholds for Procurement Methods), Section 1 -- Thresholds for Procurement of Works:

| Method | Min (UGX) | Max (UGX) | Reference |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Open Domestic Bidding or Open International Bidding | > 500,000,000 | No limit | Guideline 1/2014, 1.1 |
| Restricted Domestic Bidding or Restricted International Bidding | > 200,000,000 | 500,000,000 | Guideline 1/2014, 1.2 |
| Quotation Method | > 10,000,000 | 200,000,000 | Guideline 1/2014, 1.3 |
| Micro Procurement | 0 | 10,000,000 | Guideline 1/2014, 1.4 |

**CRITICAL DISCLAIMER:** These thresholds are from Guideline 1/2014, effective 3 March 2014. The PPDA Authority periodically updates thresholds. **Always verify current thresholds from PPDA Authority before use.** Thresholds shown are ILLUSTRATIVE of the regulatory framework.

**Notes on threshold application:**
- Open International Bidding is used when the value exceeds UGX 500 million AND international competition is needed (insufficient domestic capacity). Open Domestic Bidding is the default for values above UGX 500 million with adequate domestic competition.
- Restricted bidding requires justification beyond just the value range -- it must be based on limited providers, specialisation, or prequalification.
- Micro procurement is for unforeseen requirements only (Regulation 7) -- not for planned procurement below the threshold.

### M.2.2 World Bank Thresholds (Donor-Funded Works)

**Table: World Bank Thresholds** --> `tables/wb_procurement_thresholds.json`

World Bank thresholds are not fixed by regulation but are agreed in each project's Procurement Plan. Typical thresholds for Uganda road projects:

| Method | Min (USD) | Max (USD) | Prior Review | Reference |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| International Competitive Bidding (ICB) | > 15,000,000 | No limit | Yes | WB Procurement Regulations, Annex III |
| National Competitive Bidding (NCB) | > 200,000 | 15,000,000 | Above USD 5M | Project-specific |
| Shopping | 0 | 200,000 | No | Project-specific |

**CRITICAL DISCLAIMER:** World Bank thresholds are project-specific, agreed in the Procurement Plan for each project. The values shown are typical for Uganda infrastructure projects but may vary. **Always check the project Procurement Plan.**

**World Bank Terminology:**
- "Open International" (WB) = ICB (traditional term)
- "Open National" (WB) = NCB (traditional term)
- "Request for Quotations" (WB) = Shopping (traditional term)
- The 2016 Procurement Regulations introduced the concept of "fit-for-purpose" procurement, giving more flexibility in method selection through the Procurement Plan.

### M.2.3 AfDB Thresholds (Donor-Funded Works)

AfDB procurement framework is similar to World Bank. Key differences:
- AfDB uses "International Open Tender" terminology
- Threshold levels may differ slightly from World Bank (project-specific)
- AfDB Procurement Policy Framework (2015, revised 2023) governs
- Prior review thresholds agreed in each project's Procurement Plan

For Uganda road projects, AfDB is less common than World Bank. When AfDB-funded, obtain the specific project Procurement Plan for applicable thresholds.

### M.2.4 Method Rules from PPDA Regulations 2014

**Open Domestic Bidding (Regulation 12):**
- Open to all bidders by public advertisement in at least one newspaper of wide national circulation
- Prequalification may be used to obtain a shortlist
- Standard method for works above UGX 500 million with adequate domestic competition

**Open International Bidding (Regulation 13):**
- Open to all bidders by public advertisement in at least one publication of wide international circulation
- Prequalification may be used
- PDU makes submission to Contracts Committee for approval

**Restricted Bidding (Regulation 14):**
- Selection from a shortlist
- Grounds: limited number of providers (Regulation 9), compatibility/continuity (Regulation 10)
- Must include all potential providers on shortlist
- Must publish notice on PPDA website indicating restricted bidding and grounds

**Quotation Method (Regulation 15):**
- Selection from shortlist of bidders
- Minimum 3 quotations required
- No public bid opening required
- PDU makes submission to Contracts Committee

**Micro Procurement (Regulation 16):**
- For unforeseen requirements below threshold
- No bidding document, bid submission, public opening, or evaluation committee required
- May be delegated to User Department

**Direct Procurement (Regulation 17):**
- Emergency situations (Regulation 8): urgent, unforeseeable, not caused by dilatory conduct
- Limited providers (Regulation 9): sole source, patent, monopoly
- Compatibility/continuity (Regulation 10)
- Must use standard documentation with modifications
- Requires Contracts Committee approval
- **Even in emergencies, consider competitive methods first** (Regulation 8(5)-(9)):
  - If value requires open bidding: first consider restricted bidding, then quotation, then any competitive method with modifications, before direct procurement
  - Modifications to competitive methods may include shorter bidding period, shortlist of 2+ bidders, simplified documentation

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Step 2a: PPDA Method Selection (GoU Funded)**
```
Estimated value of works:
  |-- > UGX 500,000,000 --> Open Domestic Bidding (default)
  |                         OR Open International Bidding (if insufficient domestic competition)
  |                         Approval: Contracts Committee
  |
  |-- > UGX 200,000,000 to UGX 500,000,000 --> Restricted Bidding
  |                                              (only if grounds per Regulation 9/10 exist)
  |                                              OR Quotation Method (if > 3 providers available)
  |                                              Approval: Contracts Committee
  |
  |-- > UGX 10,000,000 to UGX 200,000,000 --> Quotation Method
  |                                             Minimum 3 quotations
  |                                             Approval: Contracts Committee (via PDU submission)
  |
  +-- <= UGX 10,000,000 --> Micro Procurement
                             Unforeseen requirements only
                             May be delegated to User Department

Note: Most UNRA road projects exceed UGX 500 million.
Typical UNRA road contracts range from UGX 5 billion to UGX 200+ billion.
Open Domestic Bidding or Open International Bidding is the norm.
```

**Step 2b: World Bank Method Selection (WB Funded)**
```
Estimated value of works:
  |-- > USD 15,000,000 --> International Competitive Bidding (ICB)
  |                        Prior review required
  |                        World Bank no-objection for: bidding documents, evaluation report, award
  |
  |-- > USD 200,000 to USD 15,000,000 --> National Competitive Bidding (NCB)
  |                                        Prior review above USD 5,000,000
  |                                        Must use acceptable NCB procedures
  |                                        (WB reviews NCB procedures for acceptability)
  |
  +-- <= USD 200,000 --> Shopping
                          Minimum 3 quotations
                          Post review (unless flagged in Procurement Plan)

Note: Most UNRA trunk road projects (World Bank funded) exceed USD 15M.
ICB with prequalification is standard for large trunk road contracts.
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Most large UNRA road projects are donor-funded.** Projects above UGX 50 billion are typically funded by World Bank IDA, AfDB, or other development partners. This means donor procurement rules apply for method selection, even though the PPDA Act remains the national framework. Engineers working on UNRA projects must be familiar with both frameworks.

**Prequalification is common for large road contracts.** Both PPDA (Regulation 18-22 of Rules & Methods Regulations 2014) and World Bank encourage prequalification for large, complex works contracts. For UNRA trunk road projects, prequalification reduces the number of bidders to those with demonstrated capacity. Prequalification criteria include: experience on similar contracts, performance record, equipment capacity, financial capability, and personnel qualifications.

**Contract splitting is actively monitored.** PPDA Authority conducts procurement audits and performance audits (Section 7(j) of the Act). Splitting contracts to avoid thresholds is an offence. PPDA has the power to investigate and recommend suspension of officers involved.

**Emergency procurement after flooding/landslides is the most common legitimate use of direct procurement for road works.** Uganda experiences periodic flood damage to road infrastructure. The justification must demonstrate: (a) the event was unforeseeable, (b) urgent action is needed to restore road access, (c) the procuring entity's own delays did not cause the urgency. Even then, competition should be sought where feasible (Regulation 8(5)-(9)).

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Using PPDA thresholds for World Bank-funded projects.** PPDA Guideline 1/2014 thresholds apply to GoU-funded procurement only. World Bank projects follow WB Procurement Regulations with project-specific thresholds.
2. **Assuming current thresholds.** PPDA thresholds are updated periodically. The 2014 thresholds shown here may have been revised. Always verify from PPDA Authority.
3. **Treating restricted bidding as a default alternative to open bidding.** Restricted bidding in the UGX 200M-500M range requires specific grounds (limited providers, compatibility). It is not simply a "simpler" open bidding.
4. **Using micro procurement for planned small works.** Regulation 7 limits micro procurement to "unforeseen requirements." Planned works below UGX 10M should use quotation method.
5. **Failing to obtain Contracts Committee approval.** Even quotation method requires PDU submission to Contracts Committee for approval of the procurement method (Section 29(a)(i)).

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Funding source correctly identified
- **Standard:** PPDA Act Section 4 (international obligations)
- **Pass condition:** Framework matches funding source
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Possible framework mismatch. Verify funding source and applicable procurement rules.

- **Check:** Contracts Committee approval obtained for method selection
- **Standard:** PPDA Act Section 29(a)(i)
- **Pass condition:** Evidence of Contracts Committee authorisation
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Contracts Committee must authorise the choice of procurement method.

- **Check:** No contract splitting
- **Standard:** PPDA Act Section 48 (economy and efficiency)
- **Pass condition:** No related contracts that aggregate above a threshold
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Possible contract splitting. Aggregate value UGX [X] requires [method].

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

The PPDA Act does not have a formal "departures" process. However:
- Deviation from Standard Bidding Documents requires PPDA Authority approval (Regulation 10 of PPDA Regulations 2014)
- Use of direct procurement in emergencies requires comprehensive justification and Contracts Committee approval
- Any deviation from prescribed methods may be investigated by PPDA Authority during procurement audits

## M.7 Worked Examples -- Uganda

### Example 1: GoU-Funded District Road Upgrade (UGX 8 Billion)

**Project:** Upgrade of District Road from gravel to paved standard, 25 km length.

**Given:**
- Funding source: Government of Uganda (GoU) budget
- Estimated value: UGX 8,000,000,000
- Multiple domestic contractors available with relevant experience
- No urgency or special circumstances

**Solution:**

Step 1: Funding source = GoU --> PPDA Act framework applies.

Step 2: Compare value against PPDA thresholds (Guideline 1/2014):
- UGX 8 billion > UGX 500 million threshold --> Open Domestic Bidding or Open International Bidding (1.1)

Step 3: Assess market competition:
- Domestic market has sufficient qualified contractors for a UGX 8 billion district road upgrade
- International bidding not required
- **Method: Open Domestic Bidding**

Step 4: Check special circumstances: None.

Step 5: Contract form:
- Employer provides design (specifications and drawings)
- Traditional construction contract
- **Contract form: FIDIC Red Book (Skill #42)**

Step 6: Approval requirements:
- Contracts Committee authorises procurement method
- Contracts Committee approves bidding documents
- Contracts Committee approves evaluation report and awards contract
- No donor no-objection required (GoU funded)

**Result:** Open Domestic Bidding, PPDA Standard Bidding Documents, FIDIC Red Book contract, Contracts Committee approval.

### Example 2: World Bank-Funded Trunk Road Rehabilitation (UGX 120 Billion)

**Project:** Rehabilitation of National Trunk Road, 80 km length, UNRA project.

**Given:**
- Funding source: World Bank IDA Credit
- Estimated value: UGX 120,000,000,000 (approximately USD 32 million at UGX 3,750/USD)
- Project has an agreed Procurement Plan with World Bank
- Large, complex contract requiring prequalification

**Solution:**

Step 1: Funding source = World Bank IDA --> World Bank Procurement Regulations apply (PPDA Act Section 4).

Step 2: Compare value against World Bank thresholds:
- USD 32 million > USD 15 million ICB threshold --> International Competitive Bidding (ICB)
- Prior review required (above threshold)

Step 3: Additional World Bank requirements:
- No-objection required for: prequalification documents, bidding documents, evaluation report, contract award
- World Bank Standard Bidding Documents for Procurement of Works
- Two-envelope system (technical + financial) if QCBS applies; single envelope for ICB works (lowest evaluated price)
- Prequalification recommended for this contract size and complexity

Step 4: Contract form:
- Employer provides design
- **Contract form: FIDIC Red Book (Skill #42)** with World Bank modified conditions (MDB Harmonised Edition or WB SBD conditions)

Step 5: PPDA Act applicability:
- PPDA Act applies for: registration requirements, tax treatment, administrative review (Section 89-91)
- PPDA Act does NOT override WB procurement method selection
- Contracts Committee still reviews and awards, but WB no-objection is required before award

**Result:** ICB with prequalification, World Bank SBDs, FIDIC Red Book (WB modified), World Bank prior review and no-objection required at key stages.

### Example 3: Emergency Culvert Replacement After Flooding (UGX 150 Million, GoU Funded)

**Project:** Emergency replacement of washed-out culvert on access road after severe flooding event.

**Given:**
- Funding source: GoU budget (emergency allocation)
- Estimated value: UGX 150,000,000
- Road is impassable; communities are cut off
- Event occurred 3 days ago; flooding was sudden and unforeseeable
- 3-4 local contractors with capacity to undertake the work

**Solution:**

Step 1: Funding source = GoU --> PPDA Act framework applies.

Step 2: Compare value against PPDA thresholds:
- UGX 150 million is in the Quotation Method range (UGX 10M - 200M)
- Normal procedure: obtain minimum 3 quotations

Step 3: Assess emergency provisions (Regulation 8):
- Is it genuinely urgent? YES -- road impassable, communities cut off.
- Is it unforeseeable? YES -- sudden flooding event.
- Caused by dilatory conduct? NO -- not the result of PDE's own delays.
- Emergency criteria are met.

Step 4: Consider competitive methods first (Regulation 8(5)-(9)):
- Value requires quotation method (not open bidding)
- Before using direct procurement, consider quotation method with modifications:
  - Shortened response period (e.g., 3-5 working days instead of standard)
  - Shortlist of 3 available local contractors
  - Simplified documentation
- **Recommended: Quotation Method with shortened timeline** (not direct procurement, since competitive option is feasible)
- If only 1 contractor can mobilise immediately: Direct Procurement with full justification

Step 5: Documentation requirements:
- Comprehensive justification documenting emergency circumstances
- PDU recommendation to Contracts Committee
- Contracts Committee approval
- PDU to maintain full records per Section 41 of PPDA Act

**Result:** Quotation Method with emergency modifications (shortened timeline) is preferred. Direct Procurement only if competition genuinely impossible. Comprehensive justification required in either case. Contracts Committee approval mandatory.
