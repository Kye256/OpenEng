### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads (Class A, B, C). KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads (Class D, E). KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads under devolution. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**PPRA (Public Procurement Regulatory Authority):** The procurement regulator established under the Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act 2015. PPRA sets procurement standards, issues regulations and guidelines, monitors compliance, and audits procuring entities. All road procuring entities (KeNHA, KeRRA, KURA, County Governments) must comply with PPRA regulations.

**Procuring Entities:** Each road authority (KeNHA, KeRRA, KURA) and each County Government is a procuring entity under the PPRA framework. Each has:
- **Accounting Officer:** Head of the procuring entity, responsible for procurement decisions
- **Procurement Function:** The procurement unit within each entity, manages all procurement activities
- **Tender Committee (or equivalent):** Reviews and approves evaluation reports and awards contracts

**National Treasury:** Oversees public financial management. National Treasury circulars may supplement PPRA regulations on procurement thresholds and procedures.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The PPRA framework under the 2015 Act emphasises transparency, fairness, and value for money. Open Tendering is the default procurement method (Section 91). Deviation from Open Tendering requires specific justification per the Act. The Act provides for several procurement methods, each with specific conditions for use.

**Key principle:** Where an international agreement (including donor financing agreements) conflicts with the PPRA framework, the international agreement provisions may prevail (similar to the Uganda PPDA Act Section 4 pattern). This provides the legal basis for donor procurement rules on donor-funded projects.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 PPRA Procurement Methods for Works

The PPRA framework provides the following procurement methods for works contracts:

| Method | Description | Conditions for Use | Reference |
|--------|-------------|--------------------|-----------|
| Open Tendering | Default method; open to all qualified bidders by public advertisement | Above prescribed threshold for Open Tendering. Default for all works above threshold. | Section 91 |
| Restricted Tendering | Selection from a shortlist of pre-identified bidders | Limited providers (Section 91(a)), specialised nature (Section 91(b)), or emergency (Section 91(c)). Requires justification and Accounting Officer approval. | Section 91 |
| Direct Procurement | Procurement from a single source without competition | Emergency/urgent (Section 93), sole source/monopoly (Section 94), standardisation/compatibility. Requires comprehensive justification. | Sections 93-94 |
| Request for Quotations | Solicitation of price quotations from multiple suppliers | Below prescribed threshold. Minimum 3 quotations. For readily available works of low value. | Section 95 |
| Request for Proposals | Competitive proposals with technical and financial components | Primarily for consultancy services. May be used for complex design-build works. | Section 96 |
| Low Value Procurement | Simplified procurement for very low-value items | Below the prescribed low-value threshold. Simplified documentation. | Regulations |

### M.2.4 Method Rules under PPRA

**Open Tendering (Section 91 -- Default):**
- Open to all bidders by public advertisement
- Advertisement in the Kenya Gazette, at least two newspapers of wide national circulation, and the PPRA website
- Prequalification may be used for large contracts
- Minimum bidding period as prescribed in regulations

**Restricted Tendering (Section 91):**
- Requires justification: limited providers, specialised nature, or emergency
- Must be approved by the Accounting Officer with documented justification
- Shortlist of qualified bidders invited to tender
- PPRA Authority may review the justification

**Direct Procurement (Sections 93-94):**
- **Emergency (Section 93):** Urgent and unforeseeable circumstances not caused by the procuring entity's own delays. Must demonstrate that competitive methods are impractical.
- **Sole Source (Section 94):** Monopoly supplier, proprietary technology, compatibility with existing equipment/systems.
- Requires documented justification and Accounting Officer approval.
- Even in emergencies, competition should be sought where feasible (similar to the Uganda PPDA pattern).

**Request for Quotations (Section 95):**
- For readily available works below the prescribed threshold
- Minimum 3 quotations from qualified providers
- Simplified evaluation (lowest responsive quotation)

### M.2.5 Kenya vs Uganda Procurement Framework Comparison

| Aspect | Kenya (PPRA 2015) | Uganda (PPDA 2003) |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Regulator | PPRA (Public Procurement Regulatory Authority) | PPDA (Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority) |
| Primary Act | Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act 2015 | PPDA Act 2003 (as amended) |
| Default method | Open Tendering | Open Domestic/International Bidding |
| Emergency procurement | Section 93 | Regulation 8 |
| Threshold updates | PPRA/National Treasury | PPDA Guidelines |
| Donor override | International agreement provisions | Section 4, PPDA Act |

*Note: While the frameworks are parallel in structure and purpose, specific threshold values, section numbers, and procedural details differ. Do not apply Uganda PPDA thresholds or procedures to Kenya projects.*

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Most large KeNHA projects involve donor funding.** World Bank, AfDB, EU, JICA, and other development partners fund a significant share of Kenya's national road programme. This means donor procurement rules apply frequently. Engineers working on KeNHA projects must be familiar with both PPRA and donor frameworks.

**Prequalification is common for large contracts.** Both PPRA and donor frameworks encourage prequalification for large, complex works contracts. For KeNHA trunk road projects, prequalification reduces the number of bidders to those with demonstrated capacity.

**County Government procurement follows the same PPRA framework.** Under devolution, County Governments are procuring entities and must comply with PPRA regulations. However, county procurement capacity may be less developed than national agencies (KeNHA, KeRRA). This is a practical consideration for county road projects.

**Contract splitting is actively monitored.** PPRA monitors compliance and audits procuring entities. Splitting contracts to avoid thresholds is a procurement offence. PPRA has powers to investigate and recommend sanctions.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Using PPRA thresholds for donor-funded projects.** PPRA thresholds apply to GoK-funded procurement. Donor-funded projects follow donor procurement rules with project-specific thresholds.
2. **Assuming current thresholds.** PPRA and National Treasury update thresholds periodically. Always verify current values.
3. **Treating Restricted Tendering as a default alternative.** Restricted Tendering requires specific justification (limited providers, specialisation, emergency) regardless of contract value.
4. **Applying Uganda PPDA framework to Kenya projects.** Despite structural similarities, the specific legislation, thresholds, and procedures differ. Use PPRA (Kenya), not PPDA (Uganda).
5. **Failing to obtain Accounting Officer approval for method deviation.** Deviation from Open Tendering requires documented justification and approval.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example 1: KeNHA GoK-Funded Class B Road Rehabilitation (KES 3 Billion)

**Project:** Rehabilitation of Class B trunk road, 40 km length.

**Given:**
- Funding source: Government of Kenya (GoK) budget through KeNHA
- Estimated value: KES 3,000,000,000
- Multiple domestic contractors available with relevant experience
- No urgency or special circumstances
- Detailed design complete (Employer provides design)

**Solution:**

Step 1: Funding source = GoK --> PPRA Act 2015 framework applies.

Step 2: Compare value against PPRA thresholds:
- KES 3 billion is well above the Open Tendering threshold (ILLUSTRATIVE: KES 6 million).
- Open Tendering is the default method.
- Assess whether international competition is needed: domestic market has sufficient qualified contractors for this contract size.
- **Method: Open National Tendering**

Step 3: Prequalification assessment:
- KES 3 billion is a substantial contract. Consider prequalification to ensure only qualified contractors bid.
- Prequalification criteria: experience on similar road rehabilitation contracts, equipment capacity, financial capability, personnel qualifications.

Step 4: Contract form:
- Employer provides design --> FIDIC Red Book (Skill #42)
- KeNHA COPA apply

Step 5: Approval requirements:
- Accounting Officer approves procurement method
- Tender Committee reviews and approves bidding documents
- Tender Committee approves evaluation report and awards contract
- No donor no-objection required (GoK funded)

**Result:** Open National Tendering with prequalification, PPRA Standard Bidding Documents, FIDIC Red Book contract with KeNHA COPA.

### Example 2: KeNHA World Bank-Funded Trunk Road Construction (KES 15 Billion)

**Project:** Construction of Class A trunk road, 80 km length, World Bank IDA funded.

**Given:**
- Funding source: World Bank IDA Credit
- Estimated value: KES 15,000,000,000 (approximately USD 115 million at KES 130/USD)
- Large complex contract requiring prequalification
- Project has agreed Procurement Plan with World Bank

**Solution:**

Step 1: Funding source = World Bank --> World Bank Procurement Regulations apply.

Step 2: Compare value against World Bank thresholds:
- USD 115 million far exceeds ICB threshold (ILLUSTRATIVE: USD 15 million).
- **Method: International Competitive Bidding (ICB)**
- Prior review required (above threshold).

Step 3: Additional World Bank requirements:
- No-objection required for: prequalification documents, bidding documents, evaluation report, contract award
- World Bank Standard Bidding Documents for Procurement of Works
- Prequalification mandatory for this contract size
- Fraud and corruption clause mandatory

Step 4: Contract form:
- Employer provides design --> FIDIC Red Book with World Bank SBD conditions (MDB Harmonised Edition)

Step 5: PPRA applicability:
- PPRA Act applies for: registration requirements, tax treatment
- PPRA Act does NOT override World Bank procurement method selection
- Tender Committee still reviews and awards, but World Bank no-objection required before award

**Result:** ICB with prequalification, World Bank SBDs, FIDIC Red Book (WB modified). World Bank prior review and no-objection required at key stages.
