# Variations & Claims

> **Skill ID:** 45

## Purpose

Guide the Engineer through variation procedures and claims assessment under FIDIC contracts. This skill covers: instructing and valuing variations (Sub-Clause 13.x), assessing Contractor's claims for additional time and/or cost (Sub-Clause 20.1), determining time extensions (Sub-Clause 8.4), and the Engineer's determination process (Sub-Clause 3.5). Applicable to both Red Book (Skill #42) and Yellow Book (Skill #43) contracts, with branches where the two forms differ.

## When to Use This Skill

- **Use when:** Instructing a variation, valuing varied work, assessing a claim for time extension or additional payment, checking claim notice compliance, or making an Engineer's determination on a variation or claim.
- **Do not use when:** Processing routine payments without variations or claims (use Skill #44). Handling disputes (use Skill #46). Contract form selection (use Skill #42/#43).
- **Prerequisite skills:** Skill #42 (FIDIC Red Book) or Skill #43 (FIDIC Yellow Book) for contract context.

## 1. Knowledge (Universal)

### 1.1 Key Concepts

**Variations (Sub-Clause 13.x):**

- **Sub-Clause 13.1 -- Right to Vary:** The Engineer may instruct a Variation at any time before the Taking-Over Certificate. A Variation may include: changes to quantities, quality, levels, dimensions, sequence, timing, or methods. The Contractor shall not make any alteration or modification unless and until instructed.
- **Sub-Clause 13.2 -- Value Engineering:** The Contractor may propose changes that reduce cost or improve value. If accepted, savings are typically shared (per Particular Conditions or 50/50 default).
- **Sub-Clause 13.3 -- Variation Procedure:** The Engineer may request a proposal before instructing. The Contractor's proposal should include: description of work, programme impact, and proposed valuation. The Engineer then instructs, modifies, or withdraws the request.

**Variation Valuation (Sub-Clause 12.3):**
- **Contract rates apply** where the varied work is of similar character and executed under similar conditions.
- **New rates** are agreed or determined where: (a) no applicable contract rate exists, or (b) the varied work is of fundamentally different character or conditions.
- **Day rates** (Sub-Clause 13.5/Appendix to Tender) may be used as an alternative valuation method.
- For Yellow Book: Variations change the Employer's Requirements; the Contractor designs the solution and values accordingly. The lump sum Contract Price is adjusted.

**Claims (Sub-Clause 20.1):**

**The 28-Day Notice Bar -- the most critical procedural requirement:**
1. The Contractor must give notice within 28 days after becoming aware (or should have become aware) of the event or circumstance.
2. If notice is late, the Contractor shall not be entitled to any additional payment, and the Employer is discharged from all liability.
3. The notice must describe the event and state that it is a claim under Sub-Clause 20.1.
4. Within 42 days of the notice, the Contractor must submit detailed particulars (or interim particulars for ongoing events).
5. The Engineer responds within 42 days with approval, disapproval (with reasons), or additional information requests.

**Common Entitlement Clauses (Claims):**

| Sub-Clause | Event | Entitlement | Time? | Cost? | Profit? |
|------------|-------|-------------|-------|-------|---------|
| 1.9 | Delayed drawings/instructions | Yes | Cost + profit | Yes | Yes |
| 2.1 | Late access to site | Yes | Cost + profit | Yes | Yes |
| 4.7 | Errors in Employer's setting-out data | Yes | Cost + profit | Yes | Yes |
| 4.12 | Unforeseeable physical conditions | Yes | Cost | Yes | No |
| 4.24 | Fossils/antiquities | Yes | Cost + profit | Yes | Yes |
| 8.4 | Extension of time (various causes) | Yes | See source clause | -- | -- |
| 8.9 | Employer's suspension | Yes | Cost + profit | Yes | Yes |
| 13.7 | Changes in legislation | Yes | Cost | Yes | No |
| 17.4 | Employer's risks consequences | Yes | Cost + profit (some) | Yes | Some |
| 19.4 | Force majeure consequences | Yes | Cost (limited) | Yes | No |

**Note:** "Cost" is defined in Sub-Clause 1.1.6.4 as all expenditure reasonably incurred, excluding profit. "Cost + profit" means Cost plus a reasonable percentage for profit.

**Time Extension (Sub-Clause 8.4):**

Extension is available if the Contractor is delayed by:
- (a) A Variation or substantial change in quantity
- (b) A cause of delay giving entitlement under any Sub-Clause
- (c) Exceptionally adverse climatic conditions
- (d) Unforeseeable shortages (epidemic or government action)
- (e) Any delay, impediment, or prevention caused by the Employer

The Contractor must comply with the Sub-Clause 20.1 notice procedure to claim time extension.

**Red Book vs Yellow Book Differences for Variations and Claims:**
- **Red Book:** Variations change the design (drawings, specifications, quantities). Valued by re-measurement at contract rates or new rates.
- **Yellow Book:** Variations change the Employer's Requirements. The Contractor designs the changed work. Valued as a change to the lump sum. Design-related issues are generally the Contractor's risk (not claimable).

### 1.2 Key Relationships (No Formulas)

**Variation Valuation Hierarchy:**
```
Is there an applicable contract rate?
  |-- YES: Is the varied work of similar character and conditions?
  |    |-- YES --> Use contract rate
  |    +-- NO --> Derive new rate from contract rate (adjusted)
  +-- NO: --> Agree new rate
              |-- Agreement reached --> Use agreed rate
              +-- No agreement --> Engineer determines per Sub-Clause 3.5
```

**Claims Assessment Flowchart:**
```
Step 1: Notice check (28 days from awareness) -- TIME BAR
Step 2: Identify the entitlement clause
Step 3: Check detailed particulars (42 days from notice)
Step 4: Assess entitlement (time and/or cost)
Step 5: Determine amount per Sub-Clause 3.5
Step 6: If parties dissatisfied --> Skill #46 (Dispute Resolution)
```

## 2. Reasoning (Universal)

### 2.1 Decision Logic

**Decision Tree H -- Variation Instruction:**
```
H1: Need for change identified
  --> Is this within the scope of the Contract?
    |-- NO (fundamental change to nature of Works) --> Cannot be a Variation.
    |   Consider re-procurement or contract amendment.
    +-- YES --> proceed

H2: Determine valuation approach
  --> Red Book: is there an applicable contract rate?
    |-- YES and similar character --> use contract rate
    |-- YES but different character --> derive adjusted rate
    +-- NO --> agree or determine new rate (Sub-Clause 12.3 / 3.5)
  --> Yellow Book: assess impact on lump sum
    |-- Change to Employer's Requirements
    |-- Contractor designs solution and proposes valuation
    +-- Adjust Contract Price

H3: Procedure options (Sub-Clause 13.1 / 13.3)
  |-- Engineer instructs directly --> Contractor executes, values later
  +-- Engineer requests proposal first (Sub-Clause 13.3)
      --> Contractor submits: description, programme, valuation
      --> Engineer: accepts, modifies, or withdraws
      --> If accepted: Engineer instructs the Variation

H4: Check authority
  --> Is the variation value within the Engineer's authority?
    |-- YES --> Engineer instructs
    +-- NO --> Seek Employer approval (and World Bank no-objection if applicable)
```

**Decision Tree I -- Claims Assessment:**
```
I1: Receive notice of claim (Sub-Clause 20.1)
  --> Was notice given within 28 days of awareness?
    |-- NO --> Claim is time-barred. Inform Contractor.
    |         Check: do Particular Conditions modify the time bar?
    +-- YES --> proceed to I2

I2: Identify the entitlement clause
  --> Which Sub-Clause gives the entitlement?
  --> Is the entitlement to time, cost, or both?
  --> Is it "Cost" or "Cost + reasonable profit"?

I3: Receive detailed particulars (within 42 days of notice)
  --> Are particulars adequate?
    |-- NO --> Request additional information
    +-- YES --> proceed to assessment

I4: Assess time extension (if claimed, Sub-Clause 8.4)
  --> Which cause applies (a)-(e)?
  --> Assess actual delay to critical path
  --> Consider concurrent delays
  --> Determine fair extension

I5: Assess cost (if entitled)
  --> Verify claimed costs against records
  --> Apply the correct basis (Cost or Cost + profit)
  --> Determine reasonable cost per Sub-Clause 3.5

I6: Engineer's determination (Sub-Clause 3.5)
  --> Consult both Parties
  --> Endeavour to reach agreement
  --> If no agreement: make fair determination
  --> Issue determination with reasons

I7: If either Party dissatisfied --> refer to Skill #46 (Dispute Resolution)
```

### 2.2 Professional Judgment (Universal)

**The 28-day notice bar is strict but must be applied carefully.** The clock starts from the Contractor becoming "aware" of the event -- not from the event itself. In some cases, the full impact of an event is not immediately apparent. The Engineer should consider when a reasonable contractor would have become aware.

**Concurrent delay requires careful analysis.** When Contractor's own delays overlap with Employer-caused delays, the time extension assessment is complex. FIDIC does not prescribe a method. Common approaches include: (a) but-for analysis, (b) critical path analysis, (c) time impact analysis. The Engineer should select an appropriate method and document the reasoning.

**Variation scope limits.** A Variation cannot fundamentally change the nature of the Works. If the cumulative effect of variations significantly alters the project scope, this may affect the contract's validity. The threshold is typically 15-25% of Contract Price (depends on jurisdiction and procurement rules).

**Engineer's determination must be fair.** Under Sub-Clause 3.5, the Engineer's determination must be fair -- not favouring either party. This is especially important for claims where the Engineer may have been involved in the events giving rise to the claim.

### 2.3 Common Errors

1. **Not checking the 28-day notice before assessing a claim.** This is the first step in every claim assessment.
2. **Using contract rates for fundamentally different work.** If the character or conditions of the varied work differ significantly from the original item, a new rate must be determined.
3. **Confusing Cost with Cost + profit.** Different entitlement clauses provide different bases. Getting this wrong affects the claim quantum significantly.
4. **Not considering programme impact of variations.** Every variation should be assessed for its impact on the programme and completion date.
5. **Instructing variations verbally without written confirmation.** All variations must be instructed in writing per Sub-Clause 13.1. Verbal instructions create disputes about scope and value.

## 4. Validation (Universal)

### 4.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Valid Values | Unit | Action if Violated |
|-----------|-------------|------|--------------------|
| Notice period | 28 | days | Must be 28 days (or as modified by Particular Conditions) |
| Particulars period | 42 | days | Detailed particulars due within 42 days of notice |
| Engineer response | 42 | days | Engineer determines within 42 days |
| Cumulative variation value | 0-15% of CP | % | Flag if > 15% -- procurement implications |

### 4.2 Consistency Checks

- **Entitlement clause vs basis:** Verify the correct basis (Cost or Cost + profit) for the identified entitlement clause.
- **Notice date vs event date:** Verify that the 28-day period is calculated from the correct date (awareness, not event).
- **Variation authority:** Verify that the Engineer has authority for the variation value, or that Employer approval has been obtained.
- **Red Book vs Yellow Book:** Verify the correct variation procedure is applied for the contract form.

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1: Classify the Issue
Is this a variation (Engineer-initiated change) or a claim (Contractor-initiated request for additional time/cost)?

### Step 2: For Variations -- Follow Decision Tree H
Identify the change, check scope, determine valuation approach, check authority, instruct.

### Step 3: For Claims -- Follow Decision Tree I
Check 28-day notice, identify entitlement clause, assess time and/or cost, make determination.

### Step 4: Determine Under Sub-Clause 3.5
Consult both Parties, endeavour to reach agreement, make fair determination if no agreement.

### Step 5: Document and Include in IPC
Communicate the determination to both Parties. Include determined amounts in the next IPC (Skill #44).
