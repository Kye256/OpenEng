### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads (Class A, B, C). KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads (Class D, E). KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**Evaluation Committee:** The PPRA framework requires procuring entities to constitute an Evaluation Committee for each procurement. Key requirements:
- Minimum members with relevant technical skills and experience
- Must include technical expertise relevant to the works being procured
- Members must declare any conflicts of interest
- Evaluation conducted jointly during committee sessions
- Committee produces an evaluation report with recommendations

**Tender Committee (or equivalent):** Reviews and approves the evaluation report. Awards the contract based on the approved evaluation. May return the report for further review if it disagrees with the recommendation.

**PPRA Oversight:** PPRA monitors compliance with evaluation procedures. Procuring entities must maintain complete evaluation records for audit purposes. PPRA has authority to investigate complaints and review procurement processes.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The PPRA evaluation framework prescribes a staged evaluation process. For works contracts, the standard approach is technical compliance evaluation (pass/fail technical assessment) followed by financial comparison (lowest evaluated cost). This is similar in structure to the Uganda PPDA evaluation framework, though the specific regulations and procedures differ.

**Fundamental principle:** The evaluation committee shall use ONLY the criteria stated in the bidding documents. No additional criteria may be introduced during evaluation. This is consistent with international best practice and is a key compliance requirement.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Evaluation Process under PPRA (Works Contracts)

The standard evaluation for works contracts follows a staged approach:

**Stage 1: Preliminary Examination**
- Determine eligibility of bidders per PPRA requirements
- Check administrative compliance:
  - Bid security submitted (correct form, amount, and validity)
  - Bid in required format with all mandatory documents
  - Authorisation and signature correct
  - Bid validity meets requirements
  - Required certificates/licences/registrations submitted
  - Tax compliance certificate (from Kenya Revenue Authority -- KRA)
- Pass/fail: Non-eligible or non-compliant bids rejected at this stage

**Stage 2: Technical Evaluation**
- Assess responsiveness to technical specifications and requirements
- For works specifically:
  - Adherence to Standard Specification for Road and Bridge Construction
  - Adequacy of work plan and construction programme
  - Adequacy of construction methods and equipment schedule
  - Quality assurance proposals
  - Environmental and social management plans
  - Safety and health plans per OSHA requirements
- Pass/fail for standard works contracts (technical compliance method)
- Bids not substantially responsive are rejected

**Stage 3: Financial Evaluation**
- Correct arithmetic errors:
  - Unit price x quantity discrepancy: unit price prevails, total corrected
  - Subtotal addition errors: corrected
  - Bidder notified and asked to agree to corrections
- Check for completeness of pricing (all WSCM Part 3 items priced)
- Cost and add any missing items for comparison purposes
- Apply unconditional discounts
- Convert to single evaluation currency if multi-currency bids
- Apply margin of preference if applicable under PPRA regulations
- Determine total evaluated price
- Rank by total evaluated price
- Determine lowest evaluated cost (for works: lowest evaluated price among technically responsive bids)

**Stage 4: Post-Qualification**
- Verify that the best evaluated bidder has the capacity and resources to perform
- Assess: experience on similar contracts, financial capability, key personnel, equipment capacity, current workload
- If best evaluated bidder fails post-qualification, evaluate the next-ranked bidder

### M.2.2 Evaluation Methods under PPRA

| Method | Application | Technical Assessment | Financial Assessment | Award Basis |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Technical Compliance + Lowest Price | Standard for works contracts | Pass/fail | Lowest evaluated price | Lowest price among technically compliant bids |
| Quality and Cost Based | Design-build, complex projects | Scored (merit points) | Scored | Highest combined score |

### M.2.3 Arithmetic Correction Rules

Per PPRA regulations (similar to international FIDIC practice):
- Unit price prevails over total price (unit price x quantity = corrected total)
- Subtotal errors: corrected by re-addition
- Words vs figures: words prevail (except where arithmetic relationship determines the correct value)
- Bidder notified of corrections and asked to agree
- If bidder does not agree: bid may be rejected (bid security forfeited if applicable)

### M.2.4 Material and Non-Material Deviations

**Material deviations** result in bid rejection. A deviation is material if it:
- Affects scope, quality, or performance substantially
- Limits the procuring entity's rights or the bidder's obligations substantially
- If corrected, would unfairly advantage the bidder over compliant bidders

**Non-material deviations** may be:
- Waived as minor
- Clarified with the bidder (without changing the substance of the bid)
- Quantified in monetary terms for evaluation purposes

### M.2.5 Single Bid or Limited Competition

If only 1 bid or very few bids are received, the evaluation committee must assess whether:
- The bidding process was adequate (sufficient advertisement, reasonable terms, adequate bidding period)
- Market conditions explain the low response
- No collusion is suspected
- The procurement method was appropriate

If these conditions are satisfied, evaluation may proceed. Otherwise, re-tendering should be considered.

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Kenya Evaluation Flow:**
```
Is the project GoK-funded or donor-funded?
  |-- GoK funded:
  |     Apply PPRA evaluation procedures
  |     Standard for works: technical compliance (pass/fail) + lowest evaluated price
  |     Evaluation Committee constituted per PPRA requirements
  |     Evaluation report to Tender Committee
  |
  +-- Donor funded (World Bank / AfDB):
        Apply donor evaluation methodology
        Similar staged process but with donor-specific requirements:
        - Prior review of evaluation report (if above threshold)
        - Donor no-objection before award
        - Donor evaluation forms and reporting format
        - Evaluation report submitted to Tender Committee AND donor
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Evaluation timeline must be managed.** For complex road projects with many bidders, thorough evaluation takes time. If extension is needed, it must be documented and approved per the procuring entity's procedures.

**Abnormally low bids are a significant risk in Kenya road construction.** Some bidders submit unrealistically low prices. The evaluation committee should:
- Compare key unit rates against the engineer's estimate and known market rates
- Flag bids significantly below the estimate (e.g., >15-20% below)
- Request detailed cost justification from abnormally low bidders
- Assess whether the justification demonstrates genuine cost advantages or unsustainable pricing

**Tax compliance is mandatory.** Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) tax compliance certificates are standard requirements. Bids without valid tax compliance certificates are non-responsive.

**PPRA complaint mechanisms.** Unsuccessful bidders may lodge complaints through PPRA's review mechanisms. The evaluation report must therefore be thorough, well-documented, and based solely on criteria stated in the bidding documents.

**For donor-funded projects, the evaluation report format follows donor requirements.** World Bank and AfDB have specific formats. In practice, the procuring entity prepares one report meeting both PPRA and donor requirements.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Applying evaluation criteria not stated in the bidding documents.** This is the most common ground for procurement challenges. Only bidding document criteria may be used.
2. **Not requesting cost justification for abnormally low bids.** Accepting the lowest bid without investigating unrealistic pricing exposes the procuring entity to performance risk.
3. **Not verifying tax compliance certificates.** Invalid or expired KRA certificates should be identified during preliminary examination.
4. **Incomplete evaluation reports.** PPRA and donors require thorough documentation of the evaluation process and rationale for recommendations.
5. **Confusion between Kenya PPRA and Uganda PPDA procedures.** While structurally similar, the specific regulations, forms, and requirements differ.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Only bidding document criteria used in evaluation
- **Standard:** PPRA Act 2015 evaluation provisions
- **Pass condition:** Every criterion traceable to bidding document section
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Evaluation criterion [X] not found in bidding documents. This is a violation and grounds for procurement challenge.

- **Check:** Tax compliance certificates verified
- **Standard:** Kenya Revenue Authority requirements
- **Pass condition:** Valid KRA tax compliance certificate submitted by all bidders progressing past preliminary examination
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Tax compliance certificate missing or invalid. Bidder may be non-responsive.

- **Check:** Arithmetic corrections properly applied
- **Standard:** PPRA regulations
- **Pass condition:** Corrections follow prescribed rules (unit price prevails)
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Verify arithmetic correction methodology against PPRA regulations.

- **Check:** Abnormally low bids investigated
- **Standard:** Best evaluation practice
- **Pass condition:** Bids significantly below estimate have documented cost justification
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Bid from [bidder] is [X]% below estimate. Request detailed cost justification before recommending.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: Evaluating 5 Bids for a KeNHA Road Construction Contract (GoK Funded)

**Project:** Rehabilitation of Class B trunk road, 35 km, estimated value KES 2.5 billion. GoK funded. Open National Tendering. 5 bids received.

**Given:**

| Bidder | Read-Out Price (KES million) | Bid Security | Tax Compliance | Key Notes |
|--------|------------------------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Aberdare Construction | 2,350 | Provided (valid) | Valid KRA cert | Domestic |
| Baringo Roads Ltd | 2,180 | Provided (valid) | Valid KRA cert | Domestic |
| Central Engineering | 1,950 | **Missing bank guarantee** | Valid KRA cert | Domestic |
| Diani Contractors | 2,480 | Provided (valid) | Valid KRA cert | Domestic |
| Eldoret Works | 2,220 | Provided (valid) | **Expired KRA cert** | Domestic |

Engineer's estimate: KES 2,400 million.

**Solution:**

**Stage 1: Preliminary Examination**

| Bidder | Eligible | Admin Compliant | Tax Compliant | Status | Reason |
|--------|----------|----------------|--------------|--------|--------|
| Aberdare | Yes | Yes | Yes | PASS | -- |
| Baringo | Yes | Yes | Yes | PASS | -- |
| Central | Yes | **NO** | Yes | REJECTED | Bid security (bank guarantee) missing -- material deviation |
| Diani | Yes | Yes | Yes | PASS | -- |
| Eldoret | Yes | Yes | **NO** | REJECTED | Expired KRA tax compliance certificate -- non-responsive |

Result: 3 bids proceed to technical evaluation. Central rejected for missing bid security. Eldoret rejected for expired tax compliance certificate.

**Stage 2: Technical Evaluation**

| Bidder | Specs Compliance | Work Plan | Methods & Equipment | Safety/Environment | Status |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|--------|
| Aberdare | Pass | Pass | Pass | Pass | RESPONSIVE |
| Baringo | Pass | Pass | Pass | Pass | RESPONSIVE |
| Diani | Pass | Pass | Pass | Pass | RESPONSIVE |

Result: All 3 remaining bids are technically responsive.

**Stage 3: Financial Evaluation**

| Bidder | Read-Out (KES M) | Arithmetic Correction | Evaluated Price (KES M) | % of Estimate | Rank |
|--------|------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|--------------|------|
| Baringo | 2,180 | -15 (unit price correction) | 2,165 | 90.2% | 1 |
| Aberdare | 2,350 | None | 2,350 | 97.9% | 2 |
| Diani | 2,480 | None | 2,480 | 103.3% | 3 |

Baringo's bid at 90.2% of estimate is within reasonable range (not abnormally low). No cost justification investigation required.

**Stage 4: Post-Qualification of Baringo Roads Ltd**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Similar contracts completed | 2 road rehabilitation contracts of similar size in last 5 years -- PASS |
| Key personnel | Project Manager with 12 years experience, Resident Engineer qualified -- PASS |
| Equipment | Owns major earthworks and paving equipment -- PASS |
| Financial capacity | Annual turnover 2x contract value, credit facilities confirmed -- PASS |
| Current workload | One ongoing project (60% complete), capacity available -- PASS |

Result: Post-qualification PASSED.

**Award Recommendation:**
- Recommended bidder: Baringo Roads Ltd
- Evaluated price: KES 2,165,000,000
- Basis: Lowest evaluated cost among technically responsive bidders
- Next steps: Submit evaluation report to Tender Committee for approval and award
