### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Kenya Road Authorities:** KeNHA (Kenya National Highways Authority) manages national and international trunk roads. KeRRA (Kenya Rural Roads Authority) manages rural and small town roads. KURA (Kenya Urban Roads Authority) manages urban roads. County Governments manage county roads. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoRT) provides policy oversight.

**WSCM as mandatory BoQ format:** The Works and Services Contracts Manual (WSCM) Part 3 provides the standard BoQ format for all Kenya national road projects. The BoQ item numbering aligns with the Standard Specification (WSCM Part 2) series numbering. All KeNHA, KeRRA, and KURA projects are expected to follow this format.

**FIDIC contract framework:** Kenya road contracts predominantly use FIDIC Red Book (1999) with Particular Conditions. The BoQ is a measured-work document -- the Contractor is paid for actual quantities executed at tendered rates, with variations managed per FIDIC Clause 13.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The WSCM Part 2 (Standard Specification) follows a series-based structure where each series covers a category of work. BoQ item numbers align with specification series numbers. This differs from Uganda's specification structure, which uses a different numbering convention. The Kenya series numbering system is:

- **Series 1000:** General items (Items 11.xx through 19.xx)
- **Series 2000:** Drainage items (Items 21.xx through 27.xx)
- **Series 3000:** Earthworks items (Items 31.xx through 36.xx)
- **Series 4000:** Pavement layers items (Items 41.xx through 47.xx)
- **Series 5000:** Bituminous items (Items 51.xx through 59.xx)
- **Series 6000:** Concrete pavement items (Items 61.xx through 62.xx)
- **Series 7000:** Structures items (Items 71.xx through 79.xx)
- **Series 8000:** Ancillary roadworks items (Items 81.xx through 87.xx)
- **Series 9000:** Environmental/safety items (Items 91.xx through 93.xx)

**Note:** Item numbering above is derived from the Standard Specification table of contents structure. Verify exact item numbers against the WSCM Part 3 Excel BoQ template for the specific project.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 WSCM Part 3 BoQ Structure

The WSCM Part 3 BoQ is organised by series, with each series corresponding to a chapter of the Standard Specification (WSCM Part 2). The key series and their typical content:

**Series 1000 -- General**

| Section | Content | Typical BoQ Items |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 1100 | Introduction | Not separately measured |
| 1200 | General Requirements and Provisions | Insurance, bonds, general obligations |
| 1300 | Contractor's Establishment | Site offices, camps, equipment, laboratories |
| 1400 | Engineer's Site Establishment | RE offices, vehicles, survey equipment |
| 1500 | Accommodation of Traffic | Traffic control, temporary deviations, temporary culverts |
| 1600 | Overhaul | Material hauled beyond free-haul distance (1.0 km per Table 1602/1) |
| 1700 | Testing of Materials | Special tests requested by Engineer |
| 1800 | Quality Control | QC system and procedures |
| 1900 | Setting Out and Tolerances | Survey setting out |

**Series 2000 -- Drainage**

| Section | Content | Typical Measurement |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 2100 | Drains | Linear metres (side drains), cubic metres (excavation) |
| 2200 | Prefabricated Culverts | Metres (pipe length by diameter) |
| 2300 | Concrete Channelling and Linings | Square metres, linear metres |
| 2400 | In-Situ Concrete Culverts | Cubic metres (concrete), kg (reinforcement) |
| 2500 | Pitching, Stonework and Erosion Protection | Square metres, cubic metres |
| 2600 | Gabions | Cubic metres |
| 2700 | Filter Fabric and Drainage Composite | Square metres |

**Series 3000 -- Earthworks**

| Section | Content | Typical Measurement |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 3100 | Clearing, Grubbing and Topsoil Removal | Hectares (clearing), cubic metres (topsoil) |
| 3200 | Removal of Existing Structures | Lump sum or number |
| 3300 | Breaking Up Existing Pavement | Square metres |
| 3400 | Borrow Pit Acquisition and Exploitation | Provisional sum, hectares |
| 3500 | Earthworks, Fill and Subgrade Treatment | Cubic metres (compacted) |
| 3600 | Rectification of Earthworks | Cubic metres |

**Series 4000 -- Pavement Layers**

| Section | Content | Typical Measurement |

| 4500 | Bound and Stabilised Materials | Cubic metres (compacted) |
| 4600 | Handpacked Stone | Cubic metres |
| 4700 | Rectification of Pavement Layers | Cubic metres |

**Series 5000 -- Bituminous**

| Section | Content | Typical Measurement |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 5100 | General Requirements for Bituminous | Not separately measured |
| 5200 | Prime Coat and Tack Coat | Square metres |
| 5300 | Surface Dressing | Square metres |

**Series 6000-9000** cover concrete pavements, structures, ancillary roadworks, and environmental/safety -- following the same section-aligned item numbering pattern.

### M.2.2 Measurement Rules

Key measurement conventions from the Standard Specification:

- **Earthworks fill** (Section 3500): Measured as cubic metres of compacted material in the completed embankment.
- **Overhaul** (Section 1600): Free haul distance is 1.0 km for all materials (Table 1602/1). Overhaul measured in cubic metre-kilometres (m3km) for material hauled beyond the free-haul distance.
- **Pavement layers** (Series 4000): Measured as cubic metres of compacted material.
- **Bituminous surfacing** (Series 5000): Measured as square metres at specified thickness.
- **Culverts** (Series 2000): Prefabricated culverts measured in metres of pipe; in-situ concrete culverts measured in cubic metres of concrete and kg of reinforcement.
- **Clearing and grubbing** (Section 3100): Measured in hectares.

### M.2.3 Preamble Requirements

The WSCM Part 3 BoQ includes a preamble section that defines:
- Method of measurement and payment
- General directions applicable to all items
- Definitions of provisional sums and prime cost items
- Pricing instructions for tenderers
- Treatment of VAT and duties

The preamble is an essential part of the BoQ document. Omitting the preamble is a common error.

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Kenya BoQ Practice:**

1. **Item numbering:** Kenya uses the WSCM Part 2/Part 3 series-aligned item numbering. Item numbers follow the pattern SS.II where SS = section number and II = item number within the section (e.g., 35.01 for Earthworks fill, 52.01 for Prime coat). Verify exact numbering against the WSCM Part 3 template.

2. **Provisional sums:** Kenya contracts typically include:
   - Dayworks (labour, equipment, materials at scheduled rates)
   - Price escalation (per FIDIC Sub-Clause 13.8, using Kenya CPI or construction cost indices)
   - Contingencies (physical contingency as percentage -- see Skill #50)
   - NEMA environmental compliance costs (where required)

3. **FIDIC measurement rules:** Same as universal -- quantities in the BoQ are estimated quantities, payment is for actual measured quantities, with rate review provisions per Sub-Clause 12.3 if quantities differ significantly.

4. **Currency:** BoQ priced in KES (Kenya Shillings). For donor-funded projects, foreign currency component may be allowed.

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

- **Overhaul is significant in Kenya:** The free haul distance is 1.0 km (Table 1602/1 of the Standard Specification). For projects in areas with limited local material sources (North Eastern, Upper Eastern), overhaul quantities can be very large. Include overhaul items prominently in the BoQ.
- **Borrow pit acquisition:** Section 3400 of the Standard Specification covers borrow pit acquisition and exploitation. In Kenya, NEMA requires Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for borrow pit exploitation. Include borrow pit restoration as a separate BoQ item.
- **Traffic accommodation:** Section 1500 includes detailed items for traffic control during construction (flagmen, signs, temporary deviations, half-width construction). Kenya traffic volumes on trunk roads can be high -- adequate provision for traffic management is essential.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Using Uganda item numbering instead of WSCM series numbering:** The Kenya and Uganda BoQ structures are different. Kenya uses Series 1000-9000; Uganda uses a different section-based numbering.
2. **Omitting the BoQ preamble:** The WSCM Part 3 preamble defines measurement rules and pricing instructions. Omitting it creates ambiguity.
3. **Wrong measurement units:** Common errors include measuring bituminous surfacing in m3 instead of m2, or pavement layers in m2 instead of m3.
4. **Missing environmental items (Series 9000):** Kenya requires environmental management provisions (NEMA compliance). Series 9000 items for environmental protection, waste disposal, and road safety awareness are mandatory.
5. **Not separating overhaul from material costs:** Overhaul (Section 1600) is measured separately from material production/placement. Including transport in the material rate AND claiming overhaul is double-counting.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Minimum | Maximum | Unit | Standard Reference | Action if Violated |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Number of Series with items | 4 | 9 | count | WSCM practice | Review BoQ structure |
| Dayworks provision | 2 | 8 | % of total | Practice | Flag if outside 3-5% |
| Preliminary items (Series 1000) | 5 | 25 | count | Practice | Review if <5 (may be missing items) |
| Environmental items (Series 9000) | 1 | 10 | count | NEMA requirement | Flag if zero (likely mandatory) |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** All earthworks items reference Standard Specification Series 3000
- **Standard:** WSCM Part 2 Series 3000
- **Pass condition:** Each earthworks item references the correct specification section
- **Fail action:** Add specification reference

- **Check:** Overhaul items included where haul distance exceeds 1.0 km
- **Standard:** Standard Specification Section 1600, Table 1602/1
- **Pass condition:** Overhaul items present for fill, pavement, and gravel wearing course
- **Fail action:** Add overhaul items per Section 1600

- **Check:** BoQ preamble present
- **Standard:** WSCM Part 3 format
- **Pass condition:** Preamble section with measurement rules and pricing instructions
- **Fail action:** Add preamble per WSCM Part 3 template

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

No formal departures process for BoQ format. However, the Particular Conditions or Special Specification may require additional items not in the standard WSCM Part 3 template. Non-standard items should be clearly identified in the BoQ with full descriptions and measurement rules.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: Partial BoQ for 15 km Class C Road Rehabilitation Using WSCM Part 3 Format

**Source:** Illustrative Kenya road project (all quantities approximate for methodology demonstration)

**Given:**
- Road: 15 km Class C road rehabilitation, Nakuru-Eldoret corridor
- Pavement: Overlay -- 40mm AC wearing course on existing surface, patch repairs, base course repairs where failed
- Earthworks: Minimal -- formation repair and shoulder re-gravelling
- Drainage: Cleaning existing drains, 8 culvert extensions (600mm pipe), 2 new culverts (900mm pipe)
- Road width: 6.5m carriageway + 1.0m shoulders each side = 8.5m
- WSCM Part 3 format with Standard Specification series numbering

**Solution:**
