### M.1.2 Institutional Context

Traffic surveys in Uganda are conducted by or on behalf of road agencies:

- **UNRA (Uganda National Roads Authority):** Manages national trunk roads (~20,000 km). UNRA maintains some permanent count stations on major routes and commissions project-specific surveys for design. UNRA projects typically require comprehensive traffic data including classified counts and, for major projects, axle load surveys.
- **District Local Governments:** Manage district roads (~30,000 km). Traffic data on district roads is often limited or absent. Surveys for district road projects are typically short-duration manual counts commissioned during feasibility or design.
- **KCCA (Kampala Capital City Authority):** Manages Kampala roads. Urban traffic surveys require junction turning counts and may include OD surveys for major infrastructure projects.
- **Development Partners (World Bank, AfDB, EU, JICA):** Donor-funded projects typically specify traffic survey requirements in the Terms of Reference, often requiring more comprehensive data than minimum MoWT guidance.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

The MoWT Road Design Manual provides guidance on traffic characteristics and their use in design (Section 5.4) but does not prescribe a detailed traffic survey methodology. The manual focuses on how traffic data is used for design (AADT, ADT, DHV, composition, PCU factors) rather than how to collect it. Survey methodology is left to the engineer's professional judgment, guided by the data requirements for the design stage.

In practice, traffic survey standards for Uganda road projects are often set by the project Terms of Reference rather than by the MoWT manual directly. The manual establishes what data is needed; the ToR establishes how to collect it.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Traffic Data Requirements (MoWT Vol. 1, Section 5.4)

The MoWT manual defines the following traffic parameters needed for road design:

- **AADT (Section 5.4.1.2):** Total traffic volume for the year divided by 365. For two-lane rural roads, total traffic in both directions is taken. AADT in the design year is the primary design control for low-volume roads.
- **ADT (Section 5.4.1.3):** Average daily traffic during a given time period (greater than one day, less than one year). ADT is useful but does not indicate variation by month, day, or hour.
- **DHV / 30th HV (Section 5.4.1.4):** The design hourly volume should be the 30th highest hourly volume of the year. For rural roads with average fluctuation, the 30th HV approximates 15% of ADT.
- **Composition (Section 5.4.1.5):** The composition of traffic by vehicle type and the percentage of truck traffic during peak hours must be known. Vehicles in the truck class are those with GVM >= 4,000 kg and dual tires on the rear axle.

**Table 5-2: Traffic Condition and 30th HV as a Fraction of ADT (Verified)**

| Traffic Condition | 30th HV as a fraction of ADT |
|-------------------|------------------------------|
| Rural Arterial (average value) | 0.15 |
| Rural Arterial (maximum value) | 0.25 |
| Heavily trafficked road under congested urban conditions | 0.08 -- 0.12 |
| Normal urban conditions | 0.10 -- 0.15 |
| Road catering for recreational or other traffic of seasonal nature | 0.20 -- 0.30 |

### M.2.2 Survey Methods for Uganda

The following survey methods are standard practice in Uganda road projects:

**Manual Classified Count (MCC):**
- Most common method for project-specific surveys in Uganda.
- Enumerators stationed at survey points with tally sheets or electronic counters.
- Standard classification for Uganda: passenger cars, pickups/4x4, minibuses (matatus/taxis), buses, medium goods vehicles, heavy goods vehicles, truck-trailers/articulated, motorcycles (boda-bodas), bicycles, pedestrians.
- Typical duration: 12 hours (06:00--18:00) for rural roads; 16 hours (06:00--22:00) for urban and truck corridor roads.
- Recording interval: 15-minute or 1-hour periods.
- Minimum count period: 3 days (including at least one market day and one non-market day). 7 days preferred where budget allows.

**Automatic Traffic Counter (ATC):**
- Pneumatic tube counters are the most commonly available type in Uganda.
- Deployed for 7-day continuous counts on trunk road projects.
- UNRA maintains some permanent ATC stations on major corridors, but coverage is limited.
- ATC data provides total volume and simple axle-based classification (2-axle, 3-axle, etc.) but not the detailed vehicle type classification needed for design.
- ATC should always be supplemented with at least one day of MCC to establish vehicle classification proportions.

**Video Survey:**
- Used primarily for junction turning movement counts in urban areas (Kampala, Jinja, Mbarara).
- Cameras mounted on roadside poles or bridges.
- Post-processing is labour-intensive; typically outsourced.

**Origin-Destination (OD) Survey:**
- Roadside interview method is standard in Uganda.
- Requires police cooperation for traffic stopping.
- Typically conducted at cordons or screenlines.
- Used for major corridor studies, bypass feasibility, and transport planning.

### M.2.3 Seasonal Considerations for Uganda

Uganda has two rainy seasons and two dry seasons:

| Season | Months | Traffic Implications |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Dry season 1 | December -- February | Generally stable traffic; good survey period |
| Rainy season 1 | March -- May | Traffic may decrease on unpaved roads due to poor conditions; paved road traffic relatively stable |
| Dry season 2 | June -- August | Generally stable traffic; good survey period |
| Rainy season 2 | September -- November | Similar to rainy season 1 |

**Key timing considerations:**
- **Avoid:** Christmas/New Year period (mid-December to early January), Easter week, Independence Day (9 October), major Muslim holidays (dates vary), election periods.
- **Agricultural peaks:** Harvest seasons vary by region and crop. In coffee-growing areas (Central, Western), harvest peaks may increase truck traffic significantly.
- **School terms:** School term start/end dates cause temporary traffic surges on inter-urban routes.
- **Market days:** In rural areas, weekly market days can increase traffic 2-3x on access roads. Survey plans should capture at least one market day and one non-market day.

**Recommended survey timing:** June-August (dry season 2) or January-February (dry season 1) are generally the most "neutral" periods for traffic surveys in Uganda, avoiding major holidays and agricultural peaks. However, local conditions should always be checked.

### M.2.4 Station Spacing Guidance

For Uganda trunk road projects, the following station spacing is typical practice:

| Project Type | Station Spacing | Notes |
|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Trunk road upgrade (> 50 km) | Every 15-25 km + at major junctions | Capture volume changes at towns and junction turnoffs |
| Urban road improvement | Every 1-2 km + at key junctions | Higher density due to volume variation |
| District road upgrade | 2-3 stations for entire project | Budget-constrained; focus on representative locations |
| Bridge/ferry crossing | 1 station each side of crossing | Capture directional variation |

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Uganda Branch)

```
Step 2a (Uganda): Check project ToR for specific survey requirements
  |- If ToR specifies survey requirements -> Follow ToR (may exceed MoWT minimum)
  |- If donor-funded project -> Check donor requirements (World Bank, AfDB often have specific data requirements)
  \- If no ToR guidance -> Apply standard practice below

Step 4a (Uganda): Select survey type
  |- UNRA trunk road project -> 7-day ATC + minimum 2-day MCC + axle load survey (if pavement design)
  |- District road project -> Minimum 3-day MCC (12-hour)
  |- Urban road project -> MCC + junction turning counts + possibly OD survey
  |- Low-volume road (< 200 vpd estimated) -> Minimum 3-day MCC; consider community traffic assessment
  \- If existing UNRA ATC data available nearby -> Request from UNRA; supplement with project-specific MCC
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Uganda-Specific)

**Traffic data on Uganda's district road network is generally poor.** Many district roads have never had a formal traffic count. For district road projects, any data is better than no data, and even a short 3-day count provides a basis for design. However, the designer should flag the limitations clearly.

**Boda-boda (motorcycle) traffic is significant and growing.** On many district and urban roads, motorcycles constitute 30-50% of all vehicles. They are important for cross-section design (NMT provision) but less relevant for pavement design (low axle load). Ensure MCC enumerators are specifically trained to count motorcycles.

**Overloaded trucks are common.** The traffic composition recorded in a classified count does not reflect the actual loading. For pavement design, a classified count must be supplemented with axle load survey data (see Skill #4) to determine actual vehicle damage factors.

**UNRA may have historical count data.** Before commissioning new surveys, check with UNRA's Planning Department for existing data on the project corridor. This can supplement (though rarely replace) project-specific counts.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Uganda-Specific)

1. **Not counting boda-bodas.** Enumerators unfamiliar with traffic counting may focus on motor vehicles and undercount motorcycles. Provide specific boda-boda tally columns.
2. **Counting during market days only.** Rural traffic on market days can be 2-3x normal. Ensure the count period includes both market and non-market days.
3. **Using urban survey methods on rural roads.** Rural Uganda roads often have very low nighttime traffic. A 24-hour count may waste resources; 12-hour counts with a nighttime factor are more cost-effective.
4. **Ignoring pedestrian and NMT traffic.** On community and district roads, pedestrians and cyclists may outnumber motor vehicles. Count them if cross-section design is part of the project scope.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Minimum | Maximum | Unit | Standard Reference | Action if Violated |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| MCC duration | 6 | 24 | hours | Practice standard | Warn if < 12 hours |
| MCC count days | 1 | 14 | days | Practice standard | Warn if < 3 days |
| ATC duration | 3 | 30 | days | Practice standard | Warn if < 7 days |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Survey classification scheme includes motorcycle/boda-boda category.
- **Standard:** Uganda practice standard
- **Pass condition:** Boda-boda is a separate count category
- **Fail action:** Warn -- "Classification scheme does not include boda-boda/motorcycle category. Add it -- motorcycles are a significant traffic component on most Uganda roads."

- **Check:** Survey period avoids major Ugandan holidays.
- **Standard:** Practice standard
- **Pass condition:** Survey dates do not fall on Christmas/New Year, Easter, Independence Day, or major Muslim holidays
- **Fail action:** Warn -- "Survey dates coincide with [holiday]. Data may not be representative."

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

Uganda MoWT does not have a formal departures process for traffic surveys. Survey methodology is at the engineer's professional judgment, subject to project ToR requirements. For donor-funded projects, departure from ToR survey requirements must be approved by the supervising engineer and the funding agency.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Uganda

### Example: Planning Traffic Surveys for a UNRA Trunk Road Upgrade

**Source:** Hypothetical, based on typical Uganda practice.

**Given:**
- Road: Lira--Kamdini Road (50 km)
- Country: Uganda
- Authority: UNRA
- Project stage: Preliminary design
- Road function: National trunk road (Functional Class B)
- Existing data: 3-day MCC from 2021 (both directions combined: ~2,800 vpd)
- Survey date: Planning for June 2026
- Budget: Adequate for comprehensive survey

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Load Uganda module.** Done.

**Step 2: Check project stage.** Preliminary design -- reliable AADT and classified data required. Pavement design data also needed (classified counts + axle load survey for Skill #4).

**Step 3: Review existing data.** 2021 MCC (3-day, 12-hour) exists. Data is 5 years old -- on a national trunk road with typical growth, traffic may have increased 25-30%. Data is partially adequate for screening but not for design. Additional surveys needed.

**Step 4: Select survey types.**
- 7-day ATC at 3 locations (continuous volume, daily patterns)
- 3-day MCC (12-hour, 06:00-18:00) at 3 locations (vehicle classification)
- Axle load survey at 1 location (for pavement design -- see Skill #4)

**Step 5: Locate survey stations.**
- Station 1: km 5 (south of Lira, on open road between Lira and first major junction)
- Station 2: km 25 (midpoint, near Aduku -- captures rural trunk road traffic)
- Station 3: km 45 (north of Kamdini junction approach, before junction influence)
- Axle load survey: Station 2 (representative midpoint location)

**Step 6: Determine duration and timing.**
- ATC: 7 consecutive days (Monday to Sunday)
- MCC: 3 consecutive days (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday -- avoiding market day effects)
- Period: June 2026 (dry season 2 -- neutral period)
- Check: June has no major Ugandan holidays. Acceptable.

**Step 7: Prepare survey plan.**

**Result:**

| Station | Chainage | Location | Survey Type | Duration |
|---------|----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | km 5 | South of Lira | ATC + MCC | 7 days ATC / 3 days MCC |
| 2 | km 25 | Near Aduku | ATC + MCC + Axle Load | 7 days ATC / 3 days MCC / 3 days axle load |
| 3 | km 45 | North of Kamdini | ATC + MCC | 7 days ATC / 3 days MCC |

**Equipment:** 3 pneumatic tube ATC units. 9 enumerators (3 per station, 2 shifts for 12-hour MCC). 1 portable weighbridge or weighpad for axle load survey.

**Raw data format:** Classified hourly volumes by direction, 15-minute intervals, using Uganda standard classification: cars, pickups/4x4, minibuses, buses, medium trucks, heavy trucks, truck-trailers, boda-bodas, bicycles.

**Escalation:** None -- survey plan meets requirements for preliminary design of a UNRA trunk road.
