### M.1.2 Institutional Context

Survey requirements for road projects in Kenya follow RDM 1.1 for all road agencies (KeNHA, KeRRA, KURA, 47 County Governments). The RDM 1.1 states that all survey work shall be performed and presented in accordance with the requirements of the manual to ensure uniformity and consistency of detail, format, quality, accuracy, and procedure (Section 1.2). Topographic surveys shall be conducted by qualified and registered surveyors with the Institution of Surveyors of Kenya (ISK) or any other recognised establishment (Section 2.1.2). The Director of Survey of Kenya is the overarching host of topographic survey databases (Section 2.1.10).

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Kenya Survey Requirements

From Kenya RDM 1.1:

**Units and measurements (Section 2.1.1):**
- All data for survey work shall be measured and recorded in metric units
- The use of metric system is predicated on the common use in surveying and mapping in Kenya

**Mapping guidelines (Table 2.1):**

| Purpose of Maps | Map Scales | Contour Interval (m) |
|----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Reconnaissance studies (mountainous, rolling, flat terrain) | 1:20,000 / 1:10,000-1:15,000 | 10 / 5 |
| Location studies (mountainous, rolling, flat terrain) | 1:5,000 Max / 1:5,000 | 5 / 5 |
| Rural road design (mountainous, rolling, flat terrain) | 1:500-1:2,000 / 1:1,000-1:2,000 | 2 / 1 |
| Urban road design | 1:500 | 0.5 |
| Selected site design | 1:100-1:200 | 0.2 |
| Selected site design (structures < 40 m long) | 1:100 | 0.2 |

**Source:** Kenya RDM 1.1, Table 2.1 (Verified from PDF)

**Road reserve widths (Section 2.1.5, Table 2.3):**
Survey corridor shall accommodate the road reserve widths specified in RDM 1.3. Road reserve widths range from 110 m (desirable) for Class A to 9 m (reduced) for specialist rural classes. The surveyor must verify the gazetted or cadastral surveyed road reserve width for the specific project corridor.

**Coordinate system (Section 5.6.1):**
- Surveys shall be based on UTM coordinates system based ARC1960 Datum
- Survey controls shall be tied to the National Survey Grid and National Geodetic Benchmarks (NGBM)
- Horizontal and vertical datum controls can be obtained from Survey of Kenya
- Kenya National Spatial Data Infrastructure (KNSDI) is the overarching geospatial framework

**Accuracy requirements (Table 2.4, Section 2.1.7-2.1.8):**
- Allowable loop/section mis-closure: First Order Class I: +/-0.5 mm sqrt(K); First Order Class II: +/-0.7 mm sqrt(K); Second Order Class I: +/-1.0 mm sqrt(K); Second Order Class II: +/-1.3 mm sqrt(K); Third Order: +/-2.0 mm sqrt(K)
- Where K = total length levelled between benchmarks in kilometres

**Survey stages (Section 5.2):**
Engineering surveys comprise five stages from design to completion of construction:
1. **Reconnaissance survey** -- early scoping, examining corridors, evaluating feasible alternatives
2. **Preliminary survey** -- environmental planning and concept study phase (aerial surveys, satellite imagery, ground control, topographic surveys, bridge site surveys, cadastral surveys, drone/UAV surveys)
3. **Location (setting out) survey** -- establishing designed highway elements on the ground
4. **Detailed (construction) survey** -- slope stakes, grade stakes, culvert and bridge control stakes
5. **As-built survey** -- showing physical development as built at completion for record purposes

**Scope of investigations by project stage (Table 3.2):**
- **Identification stage:** Desk study only -- historic records, road reserve status, social assessment, environmental assessment, cost estimation based on terrain and structures
- **Pre-feasibility stage:** Engineering surveys broadly specified for alignment, geometric, and pavement design. Limited geotechnical surveys. Drainage and bridge site identification. Social and environmental assessments. Cost estimation to 25% accuracy.
- **Feasibility stage:** Most favourable bridge sites identified. Detailed geotechnical surveys in unstable terrain. All culverts and water crossings identified. Materials availability confirmed. Data sufficient for preferred option selection and specification.
- **Final engineering design:** All major issues identified. All additional data requirements specified. Complete detailed survey per Section 5.6.

### M.2.2 Kenya-Specific Survey Considerations

- **Kenya datum note:** Kenya uses UTM coordinates based on ARC1960 Datum (Section 5.6.1). This is the same datum as Uganda (Arc 1960), simplifying cross-border project survey coordination. However, projects should always verify the specific datum transformation parameters as these can vary locally.
- **Survey of Kenya:** The official government agency for land surveys and mapping. Maintains the National Triangulation Network (NTN) and National Geodetic Benchmarks (NGBM). The surveyor should obtain control point information from Survey of Kenya before commencing field work (Section 5.3.1).
- **Aerial photography and UAVs:** RDM 1.1 recognises UAV/drone surveys as an efficient and cost-effective technology that should be adopted where possible for topographic surveying (Section 5.4). Aerial photography mapping scales range from 1:5,000 (detailed design) to 1:30,000 (route corridor identification) per Table 2.2.
- **Vegetation challenges:** In areas with dense non-arboreal vegetation or arable crops, aerial surveying may not offer required accuracy (Section 5.2.2). Ground survey or LiDAR may be necessary in Kenya's highland agricultural regions and forested areas.
