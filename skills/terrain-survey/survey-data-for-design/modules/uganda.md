## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 MoWT Survey Requirements

From MoWT Vol. 1 Section 3:

**Units and measurements:**
- All distances and heights in metres (Section 3.1.2)
- Angular measurement in degrees, minutes, seconds (360-degree circle) (Section 3.1.2)
- Coordinates based on the National Trigonometric System unless otherwise authorised (Section 3.1.3)
- Levels referred to mean sea level and related to a Local National Bench Mark (Section 3.1.3)
- All staked distances must be horizontal (Section 3.1.3)

**Benchmark requirements (Section 3.1.4):**
- Bench marks at maximum 700 m intervals, depending on terrain
- Bench marks required along the line, close to the right of way, and at all major structures (bridges and box culverts)
- Bench marks must be inter-visible

**Methods of data collection (Section 3.2):**
- Photogrammetry (Section 3.2.1): aerial photography with stereo processing. Advantages include rapid coverage and DTM generation. Limitations: vegetation cover obscures ground, weather dependent.
- Ground survey (Section 3.2.2): total station and GPS methods. Required where aerial methods cannot penetrate vegetation or where higher accuracy is needed.

**Survey procedures (Section 3.3):**
- Location surveys include flagging, monumenting, and centreline marking (Section 3.3.1)
- GPS survey for control network establishment (Section 3.3.2)
- Levelling for benchmark establishment and profile surveys (Section 3.3.3)
- Detail survey for features within the survey corridor (Section 3.3.4)
- Specific requirements for bridge sites (Section 3.3.5) and town sections (Section 3.3.6)

**Data collection and transfer (Section 3.4):**
- Data to be collected systematically and transferred in standard formats for design software compatibility

**Uganda coordinate system note:** Uganda is transitioning from the Arc 1960 datum to WGS 84 (used by GPS). Projects may encounter data in either datum. The transformation between Arc 1960 and WGS 84 involves both horizontal shifts and potential vertical datum differences. Always verify which datum survey data is referenced to.

### M.2.2 Uganda-Specific Survey Considerations

- **Dense vegetation:** Much of Uganda, particularly western and central regions, has dense tropical vegetation that limits aerial survey effectiveness. Ground survey or LiDAR may be necessary.
- **Access constraints:** Some regions (particularly Karamoja in the northeast and parts of northern Uganda) may have access constraints affecting survey logistics.
- **Seasonal timing:** Dry season (December-February, June-August) is preferred for survey work -- better ground visibility and site access.
- **Common survey methods in Uganda:** Total station with GPS control is the predominant method for road design surveys. LiDAR and drone surveys are emerging but not yet standard practice.
