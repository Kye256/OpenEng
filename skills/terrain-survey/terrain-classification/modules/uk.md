### M.1.2 Institutional Context

UK road design under DMRB does not use a formal terrain classification system in the way that MoWT, AASHTO, or ERA standards do. In the DMRB framework, design speed is determined by the **speed limit** and **alignment constraints** (bendiness, visibility) rather than by a prior terrain classification.

This represents a fundamental philosophical difference:
- **MoWT/AASHTO approach:** Classify terrain first -> terrain determines design speed -> design speed determines geometric parameters.
- **DMRB approach:** Set design speed from speed limit and desired standard -> design geometric parameters to achieve that speed -> accept alignment constraints imposed by terrain as departures or relaxations if needed.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 UK Approach to Terrain

DMRB CD 109 does not define terrain categories (flat/rolling/mountainous). Instead, it uses:

- **Design speed** set by the road's speed limit and desired standard (typically matching the national speed limit for the road type).
- **Relaxations and departures** where terrain constraints prevent achieving desirable geometric parameters.
- **Alignment constraint** measured by the bendiness of the route (degrees of curvature per km), which indirectly reflects terrain difficulty.

For the purposes of this skill, when working in the UK context:
1. Terrain classification is not formally required by DMRB.
2. If a terrain classification is requested (e.g., for comparison with international standards or for project reports), use the universal thresholds from the core skill Section 2.1.
3. Note that UK terrain is predominantly flat to rolling. Mountainous terrain exists in parts of Scotland (Highlands), Wales (Snowdonia), and northern England (Lake District, Pennines), but escarpment terrain in the MoWT sense is rare on UK road corridors.
