## M.1 Standards & Institutional Context

### M.1.1 Applicable Standards

| Standard | Edition/Year | Sections Referenced | Status |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| ERA Site Investigation Manual (SIM) | 2013 | Investigation planning, field investigation procedures, geotechnical reporting | Current -- primary source for investigation planning |
| ERA Geotechnical Design Manual (GDM) | 2013 | Chapter 2 (Pavement Subgrade), Section 2.2 (Subgrade characterization), Section 2.3 (Special soils) | Current -- design analysis for subgrade |
| ERA Flexible Pavement Design Manual Vol I | 2013 | Chapter 3 (Subgrade), Section 3.3 (Design CBR determination) | Current -- subgrade strength requirements |
| AASHTO | Various | T-99, T-180, T-193, T-89, T-90, T-88 (test standards referenced by ERA) | Reference test standards |
| ASTM | Various | Supplementary test methods as referenced by ERA manuals | Reference test standards |

**Note:** ERA has two separate geotechnical manuals. The Site Investigation Manual covers field investigation procedures and planning. The Geotechnical Design Manual covers design analysis. Do not confuse them.

### M.1.2 Institutional Context

The Ethiopian Roads Authority (ERA) is the custodian of national road design standards. ERA manages the federal road network (trunk and link roads). Regional Roads Authorities manage regional roads (main access, collector, feeder). Woreda (district) and Kebele administrations manage community roads. All road authorities use the ERA design manuals as the national standard.

> Cross-reference: See Skill #13 Ethiopia module (M.1.2) for full institutional context, road classification (DC1-DC8), and design period by road class.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

ERA manuals use mandatory "shall" language for investigation requirements. The ERA Site Investigation Manual establishes a phased investigation approach: desk study, reconnaissance, preliminary investigation, and detailed investigation. The scope and intensity of investigation is determined by the road's design class (DC1-DC8) and terrain classification. ERA manuals were developed with DFID/AFCAP/Crown Agents support and reference AASHTO/ASTM test methods throughout.

ERA's departure from standards process requires written approval submitted through the regional directorate to the Quality Assurance, Road Inspection and Safety Directorate (ERA GDM Section 2.3).

### M.2.1 Design Tables

**Table E.7.1: ERA Investigation Stages and Scope (From ERA SIM 2013 and ERA GDM 2013, values to be verified)**

| Investigation Stage | Purpose | Typical Activities |
|--------------------|---------|--------------------|

### M.2.3 ERA Special Soil Investigation Requirements

ERA GDM Section 2.3 identifies four problematic soil types requiring expanded investigation:

| Soil Type | ERA GDM Section | Field Indicators | Additional Investigation Required |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Expansive soils | 2.3.1 (8 pages) | Black cotton soil areas, seasonal cracking, heaving structures | Swell tests, shrinkage limit, free swell index, Atterberg limits at natural moisture |
| Compressible soils | 2.3.2 | Soft ground, alluvial deposits, high water table | Consolidation tests, vane shear, settlement analysis |
| Collapsible soils | 2.3.3 | Latosols, aeolian deposits, porous structure | Collapse potential test (double oedometer), saturation testing |
| Dispersive soils | 2.3.4 | Piping erosion, tunnel erosion, sodium-rich soils | Pinhole test, double hydrometer test, ESP (exchangeable sodium percentage) |

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic

**Step 1: Determine road design class (DC1-DC8)**
> Cross-reference: See Skill #13 Ethiopia module (M.3) for design class selection from traffic and terrain.

**Step 2: Determine terrain classification**
> Cross-reference: See Skill #11 Ethiopia module (M.2) for ERA terrain classification (flat, rolling, mountainous, escarpment).

**Step 3: Establish investigation scope from design class and terrain**
- Higher design class (DC1-DC3) requires closer spacing and more comprehensive testing.
- Terrain class modifies baseline spacing: mountainous and escarpment terrain require closer investigation spacing due to geological variability.

**Step 4: Check for special soil conditions**
- If black cotton soil (expansive) suspected: add swell testing programme per ERA GDM Section 2.3.1.
- If latosols or aeolian deposits (collapsible) suspected: add collapse potential testing per ERA GDM Section 2.3.3.
- If soft ground or high water table (compressible): add consolidation testing per ERA GDM Section 2.3.2.
- If dispersive soils suspected: add pinhole and double hydrometer tests per ERA GDM Section 2.3.4.

**Step 5: Plan laboratory programme**
- Minimum for all projects: classification (AASHTO), Atterberg limits, CBR (soaked), compaction (Proctor).
- Additional for pavement design: DCP correlation, soaking CBR at design moisture content.

> Next in pipeline: See Skill #8 Ethiopia module for AASHTO soil classification of collected samples.

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment

- Ethiopian highland soils frequently include residual volcanic soils with unusual properties (high plasticity but relatively high strength). Do not assume standard correlations apply without testing.
- The Rift Valley corridor contains variable soils over short distances -- reduce investigation spacing below the standard for this geological zone.
- Rural roads in western Ethiopia may traverse thick laterite deposits -- these can be excellent road material sources but require verification of durability (Los Angeles abrasion, aggregate crushing value).

### M.3.3 Common Errors

1. **Using the ERA Geotechnical Design Manual for investigation planning instead of the ERA Site Investigation Manual.** The GDM covers design analysis; the SIM covers investigation procedures. Both are needed but for different purposes.
2. **Assuming uniform soils in the Ethiopian Highlands.** Residual volcanic soils can change character over short distances due to variable weathering depth.
3. **Not testing for expansive soil properties in black cotton soil areas.** Standard CBR testing alone is insufficient for expansive soils -- swell potential and shrinkage limit are required.
4. **Applying Kenya RDM investigation spacing directly to Ethiopian projects.** While similar in structure, ERA spacing requirements differ from Kenya RDM 3.1 Table 6.1.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Valid Range | Flag If Outside |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| Test pit depth | 1.0-3.0 m (typical) | Depths >3.0 m may indicate deep problem soils -- verify need |
| DCP depth | 0.8-2.0 m (typical) | Refusal at <0.8 m may indicate rock or cemented layer |
| Investigation spacing (detailed) | 100-1000 m | Spacing >1000 m may be insufficient for pavement design |
| CBR values | 1-100% | CBR <2% triggers subgrade improvement; CBR >80% verify test procedure |
| Number of test pits per km | 1-10 | Fewer than 2 per km may be insufficient for trunk roads |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance

- All test methods shall reference AASHTO or ASTM designations per ERA convention (not BS standards).
- CBR shall be tested at Modified AASHTO compaction (T-180) for base and subbase materials, Standard Proctor (T-99) for subgrade.
- Classification shall use AASHTO system (A-1 through A-7), not ESCS.

### M.5.3 Departures Process

ERA departures from standard investigation requirements require:
1. Written justification documenting the departure and its reason.
2. Submission through the regional directorate.
3. Approval by the Quality Assurance, Road Inspection and Safety Directorate.
4. Documentation in the geotechnical report (ERA GDM Chapter 5).

## M.6 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Expansive soils (black cotton) detected | Volume change causes pavement heaving and cracking; most common problematic soil in Ethiopia | Expand investigation per ERA GDM 2.3.1; add swell/shrinkage testing; escalate to senior geotechnical engineer |
| Collapsible soils (latosols) detected | Sudden volume reduction when wetted; common in Ethiopian latosols and aeolian deposits | Collapse potential testing per ERA GDM 2.3.3; escalate to senior geotechnical engineer |
| Karst terrain encountered | Underground voids cause unpredictable ground conditions | Geophysical survey required; escalate to specialist geotechnical investigation |
| High water table (<1.5 m below formation) | Weakens subgrade, affects compaction, may require subgrade improvement | Table 3-2 water table assessment; drainage investigation; escalate to senior engineer |
| Variable geology crossing | Rapid changes in soil type over short distances | Reduce investigation spacing to half the standard minimum; additional DCP profiling |
| Rift Valley crossing | Complex geology with faulting, volcanic soils, variable conditions | Full geotechnical investigation with geophysical survey; escalate to specialist |

## M.7 Worked Example

### Investigation Planning for Modjo-Hawassa Corridor Upgrade

**Project:** Modjo-Hawassa corridor, approximately 275 km, trunk road upgrade from DC5 to DC3.

**Step 1: Design class and terrain**
- Design class: DC3 (Trunk Road) per Skill #13 Ethiopia module.
- Terrain: Predominantly rolling with mountainous sections through the Rift Valley escarpment (Skill #11 Ethiopia module).

**Step 2: Establish investigation scope**
- Rolling sections (approximately 200 km): Test pits at 300 m spacing, DCP at 150 m spacing.
- Mountainous/escarpment sections (approximately 75 km): Test pits at 200 m spacing, DCP at 100 m spacing.
- Structure sites: Boreholes to at least 2x expected foundation depth.

**Step 3: Check for special soils**
- Modjo area: Known black cotton soil (expansive). Add swell testing programme per ERA GDM 2.3.1.
- Rift Valley floor: Potential collapsible soils (aeolian deposits). Add collapse potential testing per ERA GDM 2.3.3.
- Lake region approaches: High water table zones. Consolidation testing per ERA GDM 2.3.2.

**Step 4: Laboratory programme**
- All samples: AASHTO classification, Atterberg limits, soaked CBR at Standard Proctor (T-99).
- Expansive soil zones: Free swell index, shrinkage limit, swell pressure.
- Potential borrow material: CBR at Modified AASHTO (T-180), Los Angeles abrasion, aggregate crushing value.

**Step 5: Estimated quantities**
- Total test pits: approximately 275,000 m / average 270 m = approximately 1,020 pits.
- Total DCP: approximately 275,000 m / average 135 m = approximately 2,040 DCP tests.
- Special soil testing: approximately 150 additional swell/collapse tests.

> **All outputs are preliminary/screening level.** Verify investigation scope against ERA Site Investigation Manual and project-specific terms of reference.
