### M.1.2 Institutional Context

Mass haul analysis in Ethiopia uses universal methods but is governed by ERA-specific contract conditions for payment. The ERA Standard Technical Specifications define free haul distances and overhaul payment categories. The ERA Standard Bidding Documents define the BoQ structure for earthworks items.

- **ERA (Ethiopian Roads Authority):** Federal trunk roads (DC5-DC8). Large-scale projects with potentially long haul distances through challenging terrain; multiple material sources typical.
- **Regional Roads Authorities:** Regional roads (DC3-DC4). Borrow pit availability varies significantly by region; haul economics are a major cost driver.
- **Woreda Administrations:** Local access roads (DC1-DC2). Emphasis on local material use to minimise haul cost; borrow pit management oversight may be limited.

All agencies follow the same ERA Standard Technical Specifications for haul payment definitions. Donor-funded projects (World Bank, AfDB, EU) may specify different free haul distances in Particular Conditions.

> Cross-reference: See Skill #13 Ethiopia module (M.1.2) for full institutional context and design class system.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Ethiopia road contracts follow FIDIC-based conditions with ERA-specific free haul distances and overhaul measurement methods defined in the Standard Technical Specifications and Bills of Quantities. Mass haul optimisation in Ethiopia must balance material quality (GC/GS code requirements from ERA GDM and Pavement Vol I) against haul distance economics, challenging terrain (highland escarpments, Rift Valley), and environmental management requirements (ERA Environmental Procedures Manual). The ERA Standard Technical Specifications classify excavation into three categories (common, hard material, rock) which affect unit costs and influence haul economics.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 ERA Haul Payment Categories

**Table EM.1: ERA Standard Technical Specification Haul Payment Categories**

| Category | Distance | Payment Basis | Description |
|----------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Free haul | Per ERA Standard Technical Specifications (typically 500 m, verify contract) | Included in earthworks unit rate | Material moved within free haul distance of source -- no separate haul payment |
| Overhaul | Beyond free haul distance | Per m3-station (100 m) or per m3-km beyond free haul | Material moved beyond the free haul distance; payment per unit volume multiplied by overhaul distance |

**Notes:**
- Free haul distance is specified in the ERA Standard Technical Specifications and may be modified by Particular Conditions for specific projects.
- Donor-funded projects (World Bank, AfDB) often specify their own free haul distances in the Particular Conditions.
- Overhaul is measured from the centre of gravity of the cut zone to the centre of gravity of the fill zone, minus the free haul distance.
- Payment is per m3 of compacted fill multiplied by the overhaul distance.

### M.2.2 ERA Excavation Classification

**Table EM.2: ERA Excavation Classification (From ERA Standard Technical Specifications)**

| Classification | Description | Typical Unit Cost Relative | Excavation Method |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Common excavation | All materials that can be excavated by ripping or digging without blasting | 1.0x (baseline) | Excavator, loader, dozer |
| Hard material excavation | Requires pneumatic tools or light blasting | 1.5-2.5x | Ripper-equipped dozer, pneumatic breaker, light blasting |
| Rock excavation | Requires drilling and blasting; UCS > 10 MPa or cannot be ripped by D8-class bulldozer | 2.5-4.0x | Drill and blast, hydraulic breaker |

**Excavation classification affects haul economics:** Rock excavation produces material suitable for rock fill (steeper slopes, lower shrinkage) but at higher unit cost. Common excavation of red latosol is typically the most cost-effective fill source.

### M.2.4 Environmental Considerations for Borrow Pit Management

**ERA Environmental Procedures Manual Requirements:**
- **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA):** Required for borrow pits per ERA Environmental Procedures Manual. Environmental management plan must be approved before excavation commences.
- **Borrow pit restoration:** Contractual requirement on all ERA-funded projects. Includes re-shaping slopes, topsoil replacement, and re-vegetation with indigenous species. Restoration cost typically 5-15% of borrow pit excavation cost.
- **Water management:** Borrow pits must not intercept or contaminate water courses. Drainage management plan required.
- **Community consultation:** Required for borrow pits near settlements. Land compensation is governed by Ethiopian land law (all land is state-owned, but compensation for crops and improvements applies).
- **Quarry permits:** Rock quarries require permits from the regional mining authority in addition to environmental clearance.

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Ethiopia-Specific)

**Ethiopia's highland terrain creates unique haul challenges.** Many trunk roads traverse the central highlands at elevations of 2,000-3,000 m with steep escarpments. Haul routes between borrow pits and fill locations frequently involve significant gradients, which reduce truck productivity by 20-40% compared to flat terrain. Mass haul optimisation for highland projects must account for gradient-adjusted haul cycle times, not just horizontal distance.

**Excavation classification significantly affects haul economics.** In the highlands, road cuttings through weathered basalt produce excellent fill material (CBR 30-70%, low shrinkage) but are classified as hard material or rock excavation with 1.5-4x the unit cost of common excavation. Conversely, common excavation of red latosol produces acceptable fill (CBR 15-30%) at lower cost but with higher shrinkage (1.20-1.35 vs 1.10-1.20). The economic balance depends on relative volumes, haul distances, and excavation classification ratios.

**Expansive soil zones create extreme haul situations.** When the alignment traverses black cotton soil (vertisols), the local material is unsuitable for any fill zone. All fill must be imported from borrow sources. Mass haul diagrams for projects crossing the Rift Valley floor or highland valley bottoms often show persistent deficit with no local balance possible. This is analogous to the Kenya Rift Valley black cotton soil situation.

**The Ethiopian rainy season (kiremt, June-September) severely impacts haul productivity.** The central highlands receive 1,000-1,800 mm annually, with 70-80% falling in kiremt. Unpaved haul routes become impassable during this period. Construction scheduling should concentrate earthworks in the dry season (October-May) where possible. Haul productivity can drop by 40-60% during kiremt.

**Material from road cuttings is usually the most economical fill source.** For roads through rolling terrain, optimising the vertical alignment to balance cut and fill volumes is the primary mass haul strategy. ERA formation widths and side slopes define the cross-section geometry; vertical alignment design (Skill #15) directly controls the cut/fill balance. A haul-balanced alignment may accept slightly steeper gradients or non-ideal K-values in exchange for significant earthworks cost savings.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Ethiopia-Specific)

1. **Not accounting for excavation classification when comparing borrow options.** A closer basalt quarry may appear optimal by distance but rock excavation costs 3-4x common excavation. A more distant latosol borrow pit may be cheaper overall.
2. **Ignoring terrain gradient effects on haul cycle time.** Highland haul routes with 8-12% gradients significantly increase loaded truck cycle times. Flat-terrain productivity rates underestimate actual haul cost.
3. **Using a single material source without checking suitability for all embankment zones.** ERA requires different CBR minimums for selected fill (GC, CBR >= 15%) vs general fill (CBR >= 5%). A single source may not serve all zones.
4. **Scheduling major earthworks during kiremt.** Haul productivity drops 40-60% during the rainy season; unpaved haul routes become impassable.
5. **Not checking for expansive or collapsible soils at borrow source.** ERA GDM Section 2.3 identifies several problematic soil types common in Ethiopia. Test borrow material before committing to a source.

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Borrow material meets ERA requirements for designated fill zone
- **Standard:** ERA GDM Chapter 3, ERA Pavement Vol I Chapter 4
- **Pass condition:** Material CBR at source >= minimum for fill zone (15% for GC/selected fill, 5% for general fill, 30% for GS/subbase)
- **Fail action:** REJECT -- Borrow material does not meet minimum strength for [zone]. Source alternative material or designate for lower zone only.

- **Check:** Free haul distance consistent with contract
- **Standard:** ERA Standard Technical Specifications, Contract Particular Conditions
- **Pass condition:** Free haul distance from contract used
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Free haul distance assumed. Verify with contract documents. If donor-funded, check Particular Conditions.

- **Check:** Borrow material free from problematic soils
- **Standard:** ERA GDM Section 2.3
- **Pass condition:** PI <= 25, free swell <= 50%, collapse potential < 1%, pinhole test not D1/D2
- **Fail action:** REJECT -- Borrow material contains [expansive/collapsible/dispersive] soil. Not suitable for fill. Source alternative material per Skill #28 Ethiopia module.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Ethiopia

### Example: Mass Haul Optimisation for an ERA Trunk Road with Multiple Material Sources

**Source:** Typical Ethiopia mass haul calculation.

**Given:**
- Road: DC5 Paved (federal trunk road, Central Highlands)
- Free haul distance: 500 m (ERA Standard Technical Specification default)
- Alignment length: 2.0 km (100 stations at 20 m intervals)
- Total cut volume (in-situ): 14,000 m3 (from Skill #26)
  - 60% common excavation (latosol, CBR 20%), 40% hard material/rock (weathered basalt, CBR 45%)
- Total fill volume (shrinkage-adjusted): 19,500 m3 (blended shrinkage factor 1.21)
- Net earthworks balance: -5,500 m3 (borrow needed)
- Three potential borrow pit sources available:

| Borrow Pit | Distance from Fill Centroid | Material | CBR | Excavation Class | Estimated Quantity Available |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------|-----|-----------------|----------------------------|
| BP-1 | 4 km (flat terrain) | Red latosol | 18% | Common | 10,000 m3 |
| BP-2 | 8 km (steep gradient, 10%) | Weathered basalt | 50% | Hard material | 20,000 m3 |
| BP-3 | 2 km (moderate gradient, 5%) | Red latosol (high PI) | 12% | Common | 8,000 m3 |

**Solution:**

**Step 1 -- Assess material suitability for each borrow source (Skill #28 cross-reference):**

| Borrow Pit | CBR | PI | Suitable for GC/Selected Fill (CBR >= 15%)? | Suitable for General Fill (CBR >= 5%)? | Problematic soil check |
|-----------|-----|----|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|----------------------|
| BP-1 (latosol, CBR 18%) | 18% | 15 | YES | YES | No issues |
| BP-2 (basalt, CBR 50%) | 50% | 8 | YES | YES | No issues; check slake durability |
| BP-3 (latosol high PI, CBR 12%) | 12% | 28 | NO | YES | WARNING: PI = 28 > 25 -- potential expansive. Check free swell per ERA GDM Section 2.3.1 |

**Step 2 -- Determine fill zone requirements:**

Assuming average fill height 3.5 m with 8.6 m formation width:
- GC / selected fill (upper 600 mm): approximately 30% of total fill = 5,850 m3 (requires CBR >= 15%)
- General fill / core: approximately 70% of total fill = 13,650 m3 (requires CBR >= 5%)

The 5,500 m3 borrow deficit supplements fill in sections where cut is insufficient. The cut material (60% latosol CBR 20%, 40% basalt CBR 45%) is suitable for all zones.

**Step 3 -- Compare borrow options:**

**Option A: All borrow from BP-1 (4 km, latosol, common excavation)**
- Suitable for all fill zones (CBR 18% >= GC minimum 15%). Simple material management.
- Overhaul: 5,500 m3 x (4.0 - 0.5) km = 19,250 m3-km
- Excavation cost: common rate (1.0x)
- EIA and restoration: one borrow pit
- Total relative cost index: 19,250 x 1.0 + restoration = moderate

**Option B: All borrow from BP-2 (8 km, basalt, hard material excavation)**
- Excellent material quality. But very expensive haul through steep gradient.
- Overhaul: 5,500 m3 x (8.0 - 0.5) km = 41,250 m3-km
- Gradient-adjusted: haul productivity reduced ~30% due to 10% gradient. Effective cost ~53,625 m3-km equivalent.
- Excavation cost: hard material rate (2.0x)
- Total relative cost index: very high

**Option C: All borrow from BP-3 (2 km, high-PI latosol)**
- WARNING: PI = 28 exceeds ERA GDM Section 2.3.1 threshold (PI > 25 = potentially expansive). Free swell test required.
- If free swell > 50%: REJECT -- material unsuitable for fill.
- If free swell < 50% and swell < 3%: may be used for general fill only (lower zones, not GC).
- Risk of using expansive material: unacceptable.
- Decision: REJECT BP-3 pending free swell test results.

**Option D: Split borrow -- BP-1 for all borrow (recommended)**
- BP-1 provides CBR 18% -- suitable for all zones including GC.
- Single source simplifies logistics.
- Moderate overhaul distance on flat terrain.
- One EIA and restoration plan.
- Total relative cost index: lowest

**Step 4 -- Recommended solution:**

Option D (all borrow from BP-1) is recommended. BP-3 is rejected due to PI exceeding ERA expansive soil threshold. BP-2 is technically excellent but economically unfavourable due to distance, gradient, and hard material excavation cost.

| Parameter | Value | Unit |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Total cut moved (within alignment) | 14,000 | m3 |
| Total fill required (shrinkage-adjusted) | 19,500 | m3 |
| Net borrow requirement | 5,500 | m3 |
| Recommended borrow source | BP-1 (4 km, latosol) | -- |
| Borrow material CBR | 18% (GC compliant) | -- |
| Excavation classification | Common | -- |
| Total overhaul quantity | 19,250 | m3-km |
| Free haul distance | 500 | m (per ERA Standard Technical Specifications) |

SENIOR REVIEW -- Borrow requirement of 5,500 m3 (28% of total fill). Single borrow source BP-1 recommended at 4 km. EIA required per ERA Environmental Procedures Manual. Material suitability confirmed (CBR 18%, PI 15 -- no problematic soil indicators).

NOTE -- BP-3 (latosol, PI 28) rejected due to potential expansive character per ERA GDM Section 2.3.1. If free swell test confirms swell < 3% and free swell < 50%, material may be reconsidered for general fill (not GC) zone only.

NOTE -- Free haul distance 500 m per ERA Standard Technical Specifications. Overhaul charged per m3-km beyond free haul. Verify free haul with contract Particular Conditions, especially if donor-funded project.
