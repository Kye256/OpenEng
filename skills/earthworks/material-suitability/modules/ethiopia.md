### M.1.2 Institutional Context

Material suitability assessment in Ethiopia follows the ERA GDM for design requirements and ERA standard technical specifications for construction acceptance criteria. The GDM provides the design framework (fill types, compaction standards, special soil treatment), while the ERA Standard Technical Specifications provide construction requirements.

ERA, Regional Roads Authorities, and Woreda administrations all follow the same ERA design manuals for earthworks material assessment.

> Cross-reference: See Skill #13 Ethiopia module (M.1.2) for full institutional context.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

ERA takes a material-type-based approach to earthworks suitability. Materials are classified by their physical properties and assessed for suitability as rock fill, earth fill, selected fill, or rejected material. The GDM provides detailed guidance on material types, compaction requirements, and treatment methods for problematic soils. ERA devotes particular attention to expansive soils (8 pages in GDM Section 2.3.1), reflecting the significance of black cotton soils in Ethiopian road construction.

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic

**Step 1: Classify material from laboratory results**
- Obtain AASHTO classification (from Skill #8 Ethiopia module).
- Obtain soaked CBR, PI, swell percentage, organic content.

**Step 2: Assess suitability tier**

```
IF CBR >= 30% AND PI <= 12 AND swell <= 1.5%:
    Material suitable for SUBBASE (GS code)
ELSE IF CBR >= 15% AND PI <= 20 AND swell <= 2.0%:
    Material suitable for SELECTED FILL (GC code)
ELSE IF CBR >= 5% AND PI <= 45 AND swell <= 3%:
    Material suitable for GENERAL FILL
ELSE:
    Material REJECTED for fill -- assess for treatment or disposal
```

**Step 3: Check for special soil conditions**
- If PI > 25 or swell > 2%: assess as potentially expansive per ERA GDM 2.3.1.
- If low natural density and granular: assess for collapsibility per ERA GDM 2.3.3.
- If sodium-rich or evidence of piping: assess for dispersivity per ERA GDM 2.3.4.

**Step 4: Determine treatment options (if applicable)**
- Expansive soil: lime stabilization (2-6%), removal and replacement, inert blanket.
- Collapsible soil: pre-wetting and compaction, removal and replacement.
- Weak but non-problematic: cement or lime modification.

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment

- Ethiopian black cotton soil (vertisol) is the most frequently encountered problematic soil. In the central highlands, it can extend to depths of 3-6 m. Removal and replacement is often impractical for deep deposits -- lime stabilization is the standard treatment.
- Laterite soils in western Ethiopia are typically excellent fill materials (CBR 15-40%) but may degrade when exposed to repeated wetting-drying cycles. Protect exposed embankment faces with grass or stone pitching.
- Volcanic scoria from the Rift Valley is lightweight and can provide good fill material, but verify durability -- some scoria is friable and breaks down under compaction.
- In mountainous areas, rock cuttings may produce fill material. Assess rock quality (weathering grade, durability) before using as rock fill.

### M.3.3 Common Errors

1. **Accepting black cotton soil as general fill without treatment.** Expansive soils (A-7-5/A-7-6 with PI >25) should not be used as fill without lime stabilization or other treatment per ERA GDM 2.3.1.
2. **Ignoring collapsible soil risk in laterite and aeolian deposits.** These soils may appear adequate at natural moisture but collapse when wetted. Always test at soaked condition.
3. **Applying a single compaction standard to the entire embankment.** ERA specifies different compaction standards for upper (93% Mod AASHTO), lower (95% Std Proctor), and core (90% Std Proctor) zones.
4. **Using unsoaked CBR for suitability assessment.** ERA requires soaked CBR for all material suitability decisions.

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Valid Range | Flag If Outside |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| CBR for fill (soaked) | 5-100% | CBR <5% material rejected; CBR >60% verify material type |
| PI for general fill | 0-45 | PI >45 material rejected |
| Swell for general fill | 0-3% | Swell >3% material rejected; swell >1% investigate expansion |
| Organic content | 0-5% | >2% material rejected for general fill |
| Embankment height | 0.5-20 m | >10 m requires stability analysis; >5 m on soft ground requires settlement analysis |
| Lime content (stabilization) | 2-8% | >6% verify mix design; <2% may be insufficient for expansive soil |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance

- Material suitability shall be assessed using soaked CBR at the appropriate compaction standard.
- Classification shall use AASHTO system per ERA requirements.
- Compaction standards shall specify the zone (upper, lower, core) and corresponding requirement.
- Special soil assessment shall follow ERA GDM Section 2.3.

### M.5.3 Departures Process

Any departure from ERA material suitability requirements (e.g., accepting fill with CBR <5%, relaxing swell limits) requires written approval through the regional directorate to the Quality Assurance, Road Inspection and Safety Directorate. Common departure requests include using marginally suitable materials in areas with limited borrow sources.

## M.6 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Expansive soil (black cotton) as subgrade or proposed fill | Volume change causes pavement distress; most common Ethiopian problem soil | Swell testing per ERA GDM 2.3.1; lime stabilization design; escalate to senior geotechnical engineer |
| Collapsible soil (latosol/aeolian) as subgrade or fill | Sudden volume reduction when wetted; settlement risk | Collapse potential test per ERA GDM 2.3.3; pre-wetting programme or removal; escalate |
| Embankment height >10 m | Stability analysis required per ERA GDM Section 3.6 | Full slope stability analysis; specialist geotechnical input required |
| Embankment on soft ground | Settlement and stability concerns per ERA GDM Section 3.1.3 | Consolidation analysis, staged construction assessment; escalate to senior geotechnical engineer |
| No suitable fill within economic haul distance | Cost and programme impact | Investigate stabilization of marginal materials; escalate for borrow pit alternatives |
| Dispersive soil encountered in embankment zone | Piping erosion risk to embankment | Filter protection and chemical treatment per ERA GDM 2.3.4; escalate to specialist |

## M.7 Worked Example

### Fill Material Assessment for Bahir Dar - Gondar Road

**Project:** Bahir Dar to Gondar road upgrade, borrow pit assessment at km 85+000 (Lake Tana basin area).

**Borrow pit investigation results:**

| Sample | AASHTO Class | CBR (soaked, T-99) | PI | LL | Swell (%) | Organic (%) |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|----|-----|-----------|-------------|
| BP-1 | A-6 (5) | 12 | 18 | 38 | 1.5 | 0.3 |
| BP-2 | A-6 (8) | 9 | 22 | 42 | 2.2 | 0.5 |
| BP-3 | A-2-6 (1) | 18 | 14 | 32 | 0.8 | 0.2 |
| BP-4 | A-7-5 (12) | 6 | 28 | 55 | 3.5 | 0.4 |
| BP-5 | A-6 (6) | 11 | 19 | 40 | 1.8 | 0.3 |

**Assessment:**

**BP-1 (A-6, CBR 12%, PI 18, swell 1.5%):**
- CBR 12% >= 5%: suitable for general fill.
- PI 18 <= 20, swell 1.5% <= 2.0%: suitable as **selected fill (GC)** with marginal swell.
- CBR <15%, so does not fully meet GC CBR requirement. **General fill only.**

**BP-2 (A-6, CBR 9%, PI 22, swell 2.2%):**
- CBR 9% >= 5%: suitable for general fill.
- PI 22 > 20 and swell 2.2% > 2.0%: **not suitable for selected fill.**
- Swell 2.2% < 3%: acceptable for general fill in lower embankment zones.

**BP-3 (A-2-6, CBR 18%, PI 14, swell 0.8%):**
- CBR 18% >= 15%, PI 14 <= 20, swell 0.8% <= 2.0%: **suitable as selected fill (GC).**
- Best quality material from this borrow pit. Reserve for upper 600 mm.

**BP-4 (A-7-5, CBR 6%, PI 28, swell 3.5%):**
- PI 28 > 25: **expansive soil suspected** per ERA GDM 2.3.1.
- Swell 3.5% > 3%: **rejected for general fill.**
- Escalate: requires swell testing programme; lime stabilization assessment if material must be used.

**BP-5 (A-6, CBR 11%, PI 19, swell 1.8%):**
- CBR 11% >= 5%, PI 19 <= 45, swell 1.8% <= 3%: suitable for **general fill.**
- PI 19 <= 20, swell 1.8% <= 2.0%: marginal for selected fill but CBR <15%. **General fill only.**

**Summary:**
- BP-3: Use as selected fill (GC) for upper 600 mm.
- BP-1, BP-5: Use as general fill for lower embankment.
- BP-2: Use in core fill zone only (lower compaction zone).
- BP-4: **Rejected** -- expansive soil. Escalate for treatment assessment or disposal.

> **All outputs are preliminary/screening level.** Verify material suitability against full laboratory programme and ERA GDM Chapter 3 requirements.
