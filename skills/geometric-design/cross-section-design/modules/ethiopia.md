## M.1 Standards & Institutional Context

### M.1.1 Applicable Standards

| Standard | Edition/Year | Sections Referenced | Status |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| ERA Geometric Design Manual | 2013 | Chapter 6 (Cross-Section Elements), Tables 2-1, 2-2 (Carriageway and Shoulder Widths), Table 6-1 (Side Slopes) | Current -- mandatory for all ERA roads |

### M.1.2 Institutional Context

The Ethiopian Roads Authority (ERA) is the custodian of national road design standards. ERA manages the federal road network (trunk and link roads). Regional Roads Authorities manage regional roads (main access, collector, feeder). Woreda (district) administrations manage community roads. All road authorities use the ERA design manuals as the national standard.

Cross-section design is driven by the Design Class (DC1-DC8), which is determined primarily by mid-life AADT, not by functional road class. The DC determines carriageway width, shoulder width, and the minimum cross-section elements. Departures from standard cross-section dimensions require written approval submitted through the regional directorate to the Quality Assurance, Road Inspection and Safety Directorate (Section 2.3).

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

ERA cross-sections are structured around the dual classification system: functional class (I-V) sets a minimum DC floor, but AADT drives the actual design class. Cross-section widths range from 4.5 m for DC1 feeder roads to dual 2x7.3 m for DC8 trunk roads. The ERA manual distinguishes between paved and unpaved standards for DC2-DC4, with unpaved roads receiving wider carriageways to compensate for edge degradation but no separately defined shoulders. This paved/unpaved distinction is a key difference from Kenya and Uganda, which either do not separately tabulate unpaved standards (Kenya) or embed them within a single table (Uganda).

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 ERA Carriageway and Shoulder Widths (ERA Tables 2-1, 2-2)

| DC8 | Dual 2x7.3 | Paved | 3.0 | 3.0 | 0.5-2.5 | 0.5-2.5 |
| DC7 | 7.3 | Paved | 3.0 | 3.0 | 0.5-2.5 | 0.5-2.5 |
| DC6 | 7.0 | Paved | 1.5-3.0 | 1.5-3.0 | 0.5-2.5 | 0.5-1.5 |
| DC5 | 7.0 | Paved | 1.5 | 1.5 | 0.5 | 0.5 |
| DC4 | 6.5 | Paved | 1.25 | 1.25 | 0.5 | 0.5 |
| DC4 | 7.0-7.5 | Unpaved | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) |
| DC3 | 6.0 | Paved | -- | -- | -- | -- |
| DC3 | 7.0-7.5 | Unpaved | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) |
| DC2 | 3.3 | Paved | 1.5 | 1.5 | 1.0 | 1.0 |
| DC2 | 5.0-6.0 | Unpaved | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) | (Note 1) |
| DC1 | 4.5 | Any | -- | -- | -- | -- |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Tables 2-1, 2-2 (Verified from PDF)

**Note 1:** Shoulders are not separately defined for unpaved roads. The carriageway width includes the running surface, and the unpaved edge forms a gradual transition to the embankment slope. Unpaved DC4 roads are wider (7.0-7.5 m) than paved DC4 (6.5 m) to compensate for edge degradation.

### M.2.2 Normal Crossfall (ERA Tables 2-6 to 2-17)

| Surface Type | Crossfall | Applied To |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Paved (DC5+) | 2.5% | DC8, DC7, DC6, DC5 |
| Paved (DC4 and below) | 3% | DC4, DC3, DC2 paved |
| Unpaved | 6% | DC4, DC3, DC2, DC1 unpaved |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Tables 2-6 to 2-17 (Verified from PDF)

ERA uses a three-tier crossfall system. The 2.5% crossfall for higher design classes matches Kenya (2.5%) and Uganda (2.5%). The 3% crossfall for lower paved classes is specific to ERA and provides better drainage on narrower, lower-speed roads. The 6% unpaved crossfall is the same as Uganda and higher than Kenya (4% gravel, 5% earth).

### M.2.3 Side Slopes (ERA Table 6-1)

**Fill Slopes:**

| Fill Height | Slope (V:H) | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| < 3 m | 1V:2H | Standard fill for all soil types |
| 3-6 m | 1V:1.5H | Requires compacted fill to specification |
| > 6 m | 1V:1.5H to 1V:2H | Engineering analysis required; may need benching |

**Cut Slopes:**

| Material | Slope (V:H) | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Rock (hard) | Near vertical to 1V:0.25H | Subject to geological assessment |
| Rock (weathered) | 1V:0.5H to 1V:1H | Depends on degree of weathering |
| Stiff clay | 1V:1H to 1V:1.5H | Check for expansive soils |
| Soft clay/silt | 1V:1.5H to 1V:2H | Stability analysis for heights > 3 m |
| Granular soil | 1V:1.5H | Standard for non-cohesive materials |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 6-1 (Verified from PDF)

### M.2.4 Roadside Ditches

ERA Chapter 6 specifies roadside ditch dimensions based on terrain and drainage requirements:

| Parameter | Minimum Dimension | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Depth | 0.6 m | Below subgrade level |
| Bottom width | 0.3 m | Trapezoidal section |
| Side slope (road side) | 1V:2H to 1V:3H | Flatter slopes preferred for safety |
| Side slope (back slope) | 1V:1.5H to 1V:2H | Depends on material |

**Lining requirements:** Ditches must be lined when longitudinal gradient exceeds 4% in erodible soils. Stone pitching, concrete lining, or gabion mattresses are specified depending on flow velocity and local material availability.

**Terrain adjustments:** In mountainous and escarpment terrain, deeper ditches (0.8-1.0 m) with concrete or stone lining are typical due to higher runoff intensity.

### M.2.5 Clear Zones

ERA Chapter 6 specifies clear zone requirements based on design speed and roadside conditions:

| Design Speed (km/h) | Clear Zone -- Fill (m) | Clear Zone -- Cut (m) |
|---------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| >= 100 | 9.0 | 6.0 |
| 85 | 7.0 | 5.0 |
| 70 | 5.0 | 3.5 |
| 60 | 4.0 | 3.0 |
| 50 | 3.0 | 2.5 |
| <= 40 | 2.0 | 2.0 |

Clear zones are measured from the edge of the travelled way. Where clear zones cannot be achieved (mountainous/escarpment terrain, constrained urban sections), safety barriers must be provided per ERA Chapter 13.

### M.2.6 Urban/Peri-Urban Cross-Section Additions (ERA Table 2-2)

| Element | Width | Applied To |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Footway | 2.5 m minimum | Urban and peri-urban sections |
| Parking lane | 3.5 m | Where parking is provided |
| Median | 5.0 m | DC8 dual carriageway |
| Wereda/Kebele seat additions | 3.5 m parking/activities + 2.5 m footpath per direction | When road passes through a Wereda seat or larger populated area |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-2 (Verified from PDF)

The Wereda/Kebele seat provision is specific to Ethiopia. When a road passes through a Wereda (district) seat or larger town, the cross-section must accommodate local commercial activity with parking/activities carriageway and footpaths on both sides. This adds 12.0 m to the total road width (2 x 3.5 m parking + 2 x 2.5 m footpath).

### M.2.7 Comparison with Kenya and Uganda

| Parameter | ERA (Ethiopia) | RDM (Kenya) | MoWT (Uganda) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| Dual carriageway width | 2x7.3 m (DC8) | 2x7.3 m (Class A) | 2x7.0 m (Class Ia) |
| Standard 2-lane width | 7.0-7.3 m (DC5-DC7) | 7.0-7.3 m (Class B/C) | 6.5-7.0 m (Class II/III) |
| Minimum paved width | 3.3 m (DC2) | 6.0 m (Class E) | 6.0 m (Class IV) |
| Paved crossfall (high class) | 2.5% | 2.5% | 2.5% |

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (ERA Branch)

```
Step 1 (ERA): Select carriageway width from DC
  +-- DC8 -> Dual 2x7.3 m (paved)
  +-- DC7 -> 7.3 m (paved)
  +-- DC6 -> 7.0 m (paved)
  +-- DC5 -> 7.0 m (paved)
  +-- DC4 -> 6.5 m (paved) or 7.0-7.5 m (unpaved)
  +-- DC3 -> 6.0 m (paved) or 7.0-7.5 m (unpaved)
  +-- DC2 -> 3.3 m (paved) or 5.0-6.0 m (unpaved)
  \-- DC1 -> 4.5 m

Step 2 (ERA): Select shoulder width from DC and terrain
  +-- DC8/DC7 flat/rolling -> 3.0 m
  +-- DC8/DC7 mountainous/escarpment -> 0.5-2.5 m
  +-- DC6 flat/rolling -> 1.5-3.0 m
  +-- DC5 flat/rolling -> 1.5 m
  +-- DC4 paved -> 1.25 m (flat/rolling), 0.5 m (mountainous/escarpment)
  +-- Unpaved roads -> shoulders not separately defined
  \-- DC2 -> 1.5 m (flat/rolling), 1.0 m (mountainous/escarpment)

Step 3 (ERA): Determine crossfall
  +-- Paved DC5+ -> 2.5%
  +-- Paved DC4 and below -> 3%
  \-- Unpaved -> 6%

Step 4 (ERA): Check side slopes from Table 6-1
  +-- Fill height determines fill slope (1V:2H to 1V:1.5H)
  +-- Material type determines cut slope
  \-- Flag if slopes steeper than ERA limits

Step 5 (ERA): Check clear zone
  +-- Design speed determines clear zone width
  +-- If clear zone cannot be achieved -> provide safety barriers
  \-- Flag constrained sections

Step 6 (ERA): If urban/peri-urban
  +-- Add footway (2.5 m minimum)
  +-- Add parking lane (3.5 m) where required
  +-- DC8 -> add 5.0 m median
  \-- Check NMT provisions (link to NMT module)

Step 7 (ERA): If Wereda/Kebele seat
  +-- Add 3.5 m parking/activities per direction
  +-- Add 2.5 m footpath per direction
  \-- Flag for traffic calming measures
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (ERA-Specific)

**DC4 is the critical paved/unpaved boundary.** DC4 roads can be either paved or unpaved, and the cross-section differs significantly. A paved DC4 road has a 6.5 m carriageway with 1.25 m shoulders, while an unpaved DC4 road has a 7.0-7.5 m carriageway with no separate shoulders. The decision between paved and unpaved should be based on traffic volume trends, maintenance capacity, and economic analysis.

**Unpaved road widths are wider than their paved equivalents.** This compensates for edge degradation and the tendency of drivers to avoid soft edges. ERA's approach is consistent with TRL practice for low-volume roads.

**Wereda seat provisions add substantial width.** Adding 12 m (2 x 3.5 m + 2 x 2.5 m) to the cross-section through a Wereda seat significantly increases right-of-way requirements. This should be flagged early in the design process for land acquisition.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (ERA-Specific)

1. **Using Kenya or Uganda cross-section widths for Ethiopian roads.** ERA carriageway widths differ from both Kenya and Uganda. Always use ERA Tables 2-1 and 2-2.
2. **Applying a single crossfall to all paved roads.** ERA uses 2.5% for DC5+ and 3% for DC4 and below. This three-tier system (2.5%/3%/6%) is unique to ERA.
3. **Defining shoulders for unpaved roads.** ERA does not separately define shoulders for unpaved roads. The wider carriageway width includes the running surface and transition.
4. **Omitting Wereda/Kebele seat provisions.** When a road passes through a Wereda seat, the additional parking and footpath elements are mandatory per ERA Table 2-2.

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Carriageway width matches DC requirement
- **Standard:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-1
- **Pass condition:** Carriageway width >= minimum for the design class
- **Fail action:** Warn -- carriageway width [X]m below minimum [Y]m for DC[Z]. Departure required.

- **Check:** Crossfall matches surface type and design class
- **Standard:** ERA GDM 2013, Tables 2-6 to 2-17
- **Pass condition:** Crossfall = 2.5% (DC5+ paved), 3% (DC4 and below paved), or 6% (unpaved)
- **Fail action:** Warn -- crossfall [X]% does not match ERA standard for DC[Z] [surface type].

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

ERA departures require written approval (Section 2.3). The designer submits to the respective regional directorate, which evaluates and forwards to the Quality Assurance, Road Inspection and Safety Directorate. The submission must include: the road name and number, the specific design parameter for departure, the standard value and proposed departure value, the reason for the departure, and any safety mitigation measures.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Ethiopia

### Example: Cross-Section for Awash to Kulubi Road (Route A10), DC5, Flat Terrain

**Given:**
- Road: Route A10, Awash to Kulubi section
- Design Class: DC5 (AADT 300-1,000)
- Surface: Paved
- Terrain: Flat
- Context: Rural, with a Wereda seat at Hirna

**Solution:**

**Step 1 -- Carriageway width:**
From ERA Table 2-1: DC5 paved = 7.0 m carriageway width.

**Step 2 -- Shoulder width:**
From ERA Table 2-1: DC5, flat terrain = 1.5 m shoulders each side.

**Step 3 -- Crossfall:**
Paved DC5 -> 2.5% crossfall (ERA Tables 2-6 to 2-9).

**Step 4 -- Side slopes:**
Fill sections (typical 1.5-2.5 m fill height): 1V:2H fill slope (ERA Table 6-1, fill < 3 m).
Cut sections: 1V:1.5H for granular soil (ERA Table 6-1).

**Step 5 -- Clear zone:**
Design speed for DC5, flat = 85 km/h. Clear zone = 7.0 m on fill side, 5.0 m on cut side.

**Step 6 -- Roadside ditch:**
Trapezoidal ditch: 0.6 m deep, 0.3 m bottom width, 1V:2H road-side slope.

**Step 7 -- Wereda seat section (Hirna):**
Through the Wereda seat at Hirna, add:
- 3.5 m parking/activities carriageway per direction
- 2.5 m footpath per direction
- Traffic calming measures at entry/exit

**Result -- Rural section:**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Carriageway width | 7.0 m |
| Shoulder width | 1.5 m each side |
| Total roadway width | 10.0 m |
| Crossfall | 2.5% |
| Fill slope | 1V:2H |
| Clear zone | 7.0 m (fill), 5.0 m (cut) |
| Ditch depth | 0.6 m |

**Result -- Hirna Wereda seat section:**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Carriageway width | 7.0 m |
| Parking/activities | 3.5 m each side |
| Footpath | 2.5 m each side |
| Total width | 19.0 m (excluding drainage) |
| Crossfall | 2.5% |

All outputs are preliminary/screening level. Verify against project-specific conditions and ERA departures process.
