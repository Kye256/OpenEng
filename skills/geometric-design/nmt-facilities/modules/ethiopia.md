## M.1 Standards & Institutional Context

### M.1.1 Applicable Standards

| Standard | Edition/Year | Sections Referenced | Status |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| ERA Geometric Design Manual | 2013 | Section 5.7 (NMT Traffic), Section 5.12 (Safety in Design), Chapter 13 (Safety Design Features), Tables 2-2, 2-5 | Current -- mandatory for all ERA roads |

### M.1.2 Institutional Context

The Ethiopian Roads Authority (ERA) is the custodian of national road design standards. ERA manages the federal road network (trunk and link roads). Regional Roads Authorities manage regional roads. All road authorities use the ERA design manuals as the national standard.

ERA does NOT have a separate NMT chapter or manual. NMT provisions are integrated throughout the cross-section design (Chapter 6, Tables 2-1, 2-2), safety design (Section 5.12), and traffic management (Chapter 13) chapters. This is structurally different from Kenya, which has dedicated NMT sections (RDM 1.3 Sections 3.6-3.8, 4.5-4.7). The ERA approach means NMT provisions must be assembled from multiple sections rather than read from a single chapter.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

ERA addresses NMT primarily through two mechanisms: (1) shoulder widening when PCU-converted NMT traffic exceeds a threshold, and (2) urban/peri-urban cross-section additions including footways and traffic calming. The PCU-based approach (Table 2-5) is distinctive -- it converts all NMT modes to Passenger Car Units using Ethiopia-specific factors that reflect the local NMT fleet (bajaj three-wheelers, bullock carts, donkey carts, bicycles with trailers). This quantitative approach differs from Kenya's warrants-based system and Uganda's general provisions.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 ERA PCU-Based Shoulder Widening (ERA Table 2-5)

When NMT traffic expressed in PCUs exceeds 300 AADT, shoulder widening is required to accommodate non-motorised and slow-moving traffic safely.

**ERA PCU Values for Ethiopian NMT Modes (Table 2-5):**

| NMT Mode | PCU Value | Typical Context |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Pedestrian | 0.3 | All road types, particularly urban/peri-urban |
| Bicycle | 0.2 | Urban and rural, increasing in towns |
| Bicycle with trailer | 0.35 | Rural areas, market access |
| Bajaj (3-wheeler) | 0.4 | Urban/peri-urban, major NMT mode in Ethiopia |
| Animal cart (donkey) | 1.0 | Rural areas, common on collector/feeder roads |
| Bullock cart | 2.0 | Agricultural areas, seasonal peaks |
| Motorcycle | 0.3 | Growing mode, urban and rural |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-5 (Verified from PDF)

**Threshold:** When the combined PCU count for NMT exceeds 300 AADT, shoulder widening is mandatory. The extent of widening depends on the PCU count above the threshold and the available right-of-way.

**Critical difference from Kenya:** Kenya RDM uses Car Equivalency Factors (CEF) with different values (e.g., bicycle with trailer = 0.35 CEF, small animal cart = 0.7 CEF). ERA's PCU values are not identical to Kenya's CEFs. Always use the ERA Table 2-5 values for Ethiopian roads.

### M.2.2 Urban/Peri-Urban NMT Provisions (ERA Table 2-2)

ERA Table 2-2 specifies cross-section additions for urban and peri-urban areas:

| Element | Width | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Footway | 2.5 m minimum | Both sides of road in urban/peri-urban sections |
| Parking lane | 3.5 m | Where parking is provided |
| Raised kerb segregation | -- | Physical separation between carriageway and footway |

**Source:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-2 (Verified from PDF)

Footways in urban sections must be physically segregated from the carriageway with raised kerbs. This is a safety requirement reflecting the high pedestrian volumes on Ethiopian roads, particularly through towns and trading centres.

### M.2.3 Wereda/Kebele Seat Provisions

When a road passes through a Wereda (district) seat or larger populated area, ERA Table 2-2 requires additional cross-section elements:

| Element | Width Per Direction | Total Addition |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Parking/activities carriageway | 3.5 m | 7.0 m (both sides) |
| Footpath | 2.5 m | 5.0 m (both sides) |
| **Total addition** | **6.0 m** | **12.0 m** |

This provision recognises that Wereda seats serve as local commercial centres. The parking/activities carriageway accommodates market stalls, loading/unloading, and informal commercial activity that would otherwise encroach on the through carriageway.

### M.2.4 Traffic Calming (ERA Section 13.3)

ERA Chapter 13 provides traffic calming measures for use through villages, trading centres, and school zones:

| Measure | Application | Dimensions |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Speed humps | Village entries, school zones | Height: 75-100 mm, length: 3.7 m (flat-topped) |
| Road narrowing | Village sections | Lane narrowing to 3.0-3.25 m with bollards or build-outs |
| Raised platforms | Pedestrian crossings | Full carriageway width, 75 mm above road level |
| Entry gates | Village approaches | Visual narrowing, painted kerbs, rumble strips |

**Application criteria:** Traffic calming is required when a road with design speed > 50 km/h passes through a populated area. The approach speed reduction should be gradual: entry gate -> rumble strips -> speed hump -> reduced speed zone.

### M.2.5 Bus Lay-Bys (ERA Section 13.4)

ERA specifies bus lay-by dimensions for public transport stops along classified roads:

| Parameter | Dimension | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Loading area length | 15 m per bus | For standard single unit bus (DV3) |
| Lay-by width | 3.5 m | Clear of through traffic lanes |
| Deceleration taper | 1:10 ratio | Longitudinal to transverse |
| Merging taper | 1:6 minimum | Return to through lane |
| Location | > 30 m from intersection | To avoid sight distance obstruction |

Bus lay-bys are particularly important on DC4-DC7 roads that serve intercity bus routes. In Ethiopia, intercity buses and minibuses are a primary mode of passenger transport between towns.

### M.2.6 Safety Design Principles (ERA Section 5.12)

ERA Section 5.12 specifies safety design principles that affect NMT provision:

1. **Physical segregation through villages:** Raised kerbs to separate pedestrians from vehicular traffic. This is mandatory for paved roads through populated areas.
2. **Avoid steep open side-drains:** Side-drains adjacent to pedestrian areas must be covered or relocated to avoid fall hazards, particularly for children and elderly pedestrians.
3. **Pedestrian guard rails:** Required at specific hazard locations including bridge approaches, sharp curves through villages, and school zones.
4. **Lighting:** Street lighting is recommended at all pedestrian crossing points in urban and peri-urban areas, though this is often a municipal rather than road authority responsibility.
5. **School zone treatment:** Reduced speed limits, warning signs, raised crossings, and pedestrian guard rails within 200 m of schools.

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (ERA Branch)

```
Step 1 (ERA): Count NMT traffic in PCUs
  +-- Convert each NMT mode to PCUs using Table 2-5:
  |     Pedestrians x 0.3
  |     Bicycles x 0.2
  |     Bicycles with trailer x 0.35
  |     Bajaj x 0.4
  |     Donkey carts x 1.0
  |     Bullock carts x 2.0
  |     Motorcycles x 0.3
  \-- Sum all PCU values to get total NMT PCU AADT

Step 2 (ERA): If PCUs > 300, apply shoulder widening
  +-- Shoulder widening extent based on PCU count
  +-- Coordinate with cross-section design (Skill #17)
  \-- Flag widening in design output

Step 3 (ERA): If urban/peri-urban, add footway provisions
  +-- Footway: 2.5 m minimum, both sides
  +-- Raised kerb segregation mandatory
  +-- Add parking lane (3.5 m) where required
  \-- Coordinate with cross-section module

Step 4 (ERA): If Wereda/Kebele seat, add parking and footpath
  +-- 3.5 m parking/activities per direction
  +-- 2.5 m footpath per direction
  +-- Total addition: 12.0 m to cross-section width
  \-- Flag for land acquisition implications

Step 5 (ERA): Check traffic calming needs
  +-- Design speed > 50 km/h through populated area -> traffic calming required
  +-- Entry gates at village approaches
  +-- Speed humps and raised crossings at desire lines
  +-- School zones: special treatment within 200 m
  \-- Bus lay-bys at public transport stops (> 30 m from junctions)
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (ERA-Specific)

**Bajaj traffic is a defining feature of Ethiopian towns.** The bajaj (three-wheel motorised rickshaw) is a primary urban transport mode in Ethiopian towns and cities. With a PCU value of 0.4, bajaj traffic can quickly push the total PCU count above the 300 threshold, particularly in Wereda seats and larger towns. This is a distinctly Ethiopian consideration.

**Bullock carts contribute disproportionately to PCU counts.** A single bullock cart has a PCU of 2.0 -- equivalent to ten bicycles. On agricultural roads during harvest season, bullock cart traffic can seasonally push PCU counts well above the 300 threshold. Design should account for seasonal peak conditions.

**The 300 PCU threshold is an AADT measure.** Daily or seasonal fluctuations may mean that the threshold is exceeded during peak periods even when the annual average is below 300. Professional judgment should consider seasonal peaks, particularly for agricultural areas where NMT traffic increases significantly during harvest and market days.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (ERA-Specific)

1. **Using Kenya CEF values instead of ERA PCU values.** ERA Table 2-5 PCU values differ from Kenya RDM CEF values. Always use ERA-specific values.
2. **Omitting Wereda/Kebele seat provisions.** The additional 12 m of cross-section width through a Wereda seat is mandatory per ERA Table 2-2. This significantly affects right-of-way and should be identified early in design.
3. **Not counting bajaj in PCU calculations.** Bajaj are a major NMT mode in Ethiopia but have no equivalent in Uganda or Kenya modules. They must be included in the PCU count.
4. **Treating NMT as a standalone requirement.** ERA integrates NMT into cross-section and safety design. NMT provisions must be coordinated with the cross-section module (Skill #17) and intersection module (Skill #19).

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

| Parameter | Minimum | Maximum | Unit | Standard Reference | Action if Violated |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Footway width (urban) | 2.5 | -- | m | ERA GDM Table 2-2 | Warn if below 2.5 m minimum |
| PCU threshold | 300 | -- | PCU/day | ERA GDM Table 2-5 | Trigger shoulder widening review if > 300 |
| Speed hump height | 75 | 100 | mm | ERA GDM Section 13.3 | Warn if outside 75-100 mm range |
| Bus lay-by distance from junction | 30 | -- | m | ERA GDM Section 13.4 | Warn if < 30 m from junction |

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Footway provided in urban/peri-urban sections
- **Standard:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-2
- **Pass condition:** Footway >= 2.5 m on both sides of road in urban/peri-urban sections
- **Fail action:** Warn -- footway omitted or below 2.5 m minimum in urban section. ERA Table 2-2 requires footway in urban/peri-urban areas.

- **Check:** Wereda/Kebele seat provisions applied where required
- **Standard:** ERA GDM 2013, Table 2-2
- **Pass condition:** 3.5 m parking + 2.5 m footpath per direction provided through Wereda seats
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Road passes through Wereda seat without ERA Table 2-2 provisions.

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

ERA departures require written approval (Section 2.3). NMT-related departures include: reduced footway width in constrained urban sections, omission of Wereda seat provisions (with justification), and shoulder widening below the calculated requirement. Submit through the regional directorate.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Ethiopia

### Example: NMT Assessment for DC4 Road through Wereda Seat near Debre Markos

**Given:**
- Road: Route C34 (access to Debre Markos Wereda seat)
- Design Class: DC4 paved
- Design speed: 60 km/h (rolling terrain)
- Context: Road passes through the Wereda seat at Debre Markos
- NMT count data:
  - Pedestrians: 50 per day
  - Bicycles: 30 per day
  - Donkey carts: 20 per day
  - Bajaj: 15 per day

**Solution:**

**Step 1 -- Calculate NMT PCU count:**

| NMT Mode | Count | PCU Factor | PCU Total |
|----------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Pedestrians | 50 | 0.3 | 15.0 |
| Bicycles | 30 | 0.2 | 6.0 |
| Donkey carts | 20 | 1.0 | 20.0 |
| Bajaj | 15 | 0.4 | 6.0 |
| **Total** | **115** | -- | **47.0 PCUs** |

**Step 2 -- Check against 300 PCU threshold:**
47.0 PCUs < 300 PCU threshold. Shoulder widening is NOT required based on PCU count.

**Step 3 -- Check Wereda seat provisions:**
Road passes through a Wereda seat. ERA Table 2-2 requires:
- 3.5 m parking/activities carriageway per direction
- 2.5 m footpath per direction
These provisions apply regardless of the PCU count.

**Step 4 -- Traffic calming:**
Design speed is 60 km/h, which exceeds 50 km/h through a populated area. Traffic calming required:
- Entry gates at both approaches to Debre Markos
- Speed humps (75 mm height, 3.7 m length) at key crossing points
- Raised pedestrian crossing at market area

**Step 5 -- Cross-section through Wereda seat:**

| Element | Width | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Carriageway | 6.5 m | DC4 paved (Table 2-1) |
| Parking/activities (left) | 3.5 m | Wereda seat provision |
| Parking/activities (right) | 3.5 m | Wereda seat provision |
| Footpath (left) | 2.5 m | Wereda seat provision |
| Footpath (right) | 2.5 m | Wereda seat provision |
| **Total width** | **18.5 m** | Excluding drainage |

**Result:**
- PCU count (47.0) is below 300 threshold -- no shoulder widening required
- Wereda seat provisions add 12.0 m to the standard cross-section width
- Traffic calming measures required through the Wereda seat section
- Right-of-way requirement: approximately 22-25 m including drainage

All outputs are preliminary/screening level. Verify against project-specific conditions and ERA departures process.
