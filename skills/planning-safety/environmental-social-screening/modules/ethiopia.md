### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**Ethiopian EPA / Environment, Forest and Climate Change Commission (EFCCC):** Environmental regulator. The EPA was established by proclamation and has undergone institutional reorganization. The regulatory function may now sit under the Environment, Forest and Climate Change Commission or the Ministry of Environment. Use Proclamation No. 299/2002 as the stable legal reference regardless of current institutional name.

**ERA (Ethiopian Roads Authority):** Road authority and project proponent. ERA manages the federal road network and commissions EIA studies. ERA's Design Manual 2013 includes environmental procedures for road projects, covering screening, assessment, mitigation, and monitoring requirements for all federal road projects.

**Regional Environmental Agencies:** Regional states have environmental offices that participate in EIA review for projects within their jurisdictions. Regional bureaus may issue environmental clearances for projects under regional jurisdiction.

**World Bank / AfDB / JICA / EU:** Development partners fund many ERA road projects. Donor-funded projects must comply with both Ethiopian law AND the donor's environmental and social framework. The more restrictive framework applies -- same dual-framework pattern as Skill #47 (procurement).

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Ethiopian environmental screening follows a dual-framework pattern: EPA/EFCCC requirements for nationally funded projects, and EPA plus World Bank ESF (or equivalent donor framework) for donor-funded projects. When both frameworks apply, the more restrictive requirement governs. EIA Proclamation 299/2002 categorizes projects by environmental impact level using project schedules. ERA's internal environmental procedures add road-specific screening requirements that supplement the national EIA framework.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 EPA Screening Framework

**EIA Proclamation 299/2002 Project Categorization:**

Proclamation 299/2002 establishes a schedule-based categorization system for projects based on their expected environmental impact:

- **Schedule 1:** Projects requiring full Environmental Impact Assessment due to significant potential environmental impacts
- **Schedule 2:** Projects requiring preliminary environmental assessment -- impacts expected to be less significant but still require evaluation
- **Schedule 3:** Projects not requiring EIA -- minimal or no environmental impact expected

**(Verify schedule numbering against actual proclamation text -- training data may use different category labels.)**

**Road Project Screening Criteria:**

| Project Type | EPA Category | Assessment Required | ERA Procedure |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|---------------|
| New trunk/link road construction | Schedule 1 (verify) | Full EIA | ERA Environmental Procedures -- full assessment |
| Major rehabilitation with realignment | Schedule 1 (verify) | Full EIA | ERA Environmental Procedures -- full assessment |
| Road rehabilitation in existing corridor | Schedule 2 (verify) | Preliminary assessment | ERA Environmental Procedures -- screening |
| Routine maintenance | Schedule 3 (verify) | None | ERA internal review only |
| Bridge construction | Schedule 1 (verify) | Full EIA | ERA Environmental Procedures -- full assessment |
| Projects in environmentally sensitive areas | Schedule 1 | Full EIA regardless of project type | ERA Environmental Procedures -- full assessment |

### M.2.2 Donor Framework Overlay (Donor-Funded Projects)

For donor-funded ERA road projects, both EPA requirements AND the donor framework apply. The more stringent requirement governs each aspect.

**World Bank Environmental and Social Framework (ESF):**

| ESS | Title | Key Relevance to Road Projects |
|-----|-------|-------------------------------|
| ESS 1 | Assessment and Management of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts | Overarching risk assessment and management framework |
| ESS 2 | Labour and Working Conditions | Construction worker safety, employment conditions |
| ESS 3 | Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention | Dust, noise, water pollution during construction |
| ESS 4 | Community Health and Safety | Road safety during construction, communicable diseases |
| ESS 5 | Land Acquisition, Restrictions on Land Use, and Involuntary Resettlement | Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) for affected persons |
| ESS 6 | Biodiversity Conservation and Sustainable Management of Living Natural Resources | Wildlife crossings, habitat protection |
| ESS 7 | Indigenous Peoples / Sub-Saharan African Historically Underserved Traditional Local Communities | Consultation with traditional communities |
| ESS 8 | Cultural Heritage | Archaeological sites, cultural properties |
| ESS 10 | Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure | Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) |

**World Bank ESF Risk Categories:** High, Substantial, Moderate, Low.

**AfDB Integrated Safeguards System (ISS):**

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Category 1 | Projects with significant potential adverse environmental or social impacts that are diverse, irreversible, or unprecedented |
| Category 2 | Projects with less significant potential adverse environmental or social impacts that are site-specific and largely reversible |
| Category 3 | Projects with negligible or no adverse environmental or social impacts |

**AfDB Operational Safeguard 1 (OS 1):** Environmental and Social Assessment -- governs the assessment process for all AfDB-funded projects.

**Key ESF/ISS Instruments for Donor-Funded Projects:**
- **Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP):** Overarching document committing project to framework compliance
- **Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP):** Required for all ESF projects
- **Resettlement Action Plan (RAP):** Required when land acquisition displaces people or affects livelihoods (ESS 5)
- **Labour Management Procedures (LMP):** Required for construction projects (ESS 2)
- **Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP):** Mitigation measures for identified impacts

### M.2.3 Social Impact Assessment

**Land Acquisition and Resettlement:**
ERA road projects frequently require land acquisition, particularly for new roads or realignment. Ethiopian land is state-owned under the Constitution, but landholders have use rights and are entitled to compensation for improvements and displacement. For donor-funded projects, Resettlement Action Plans must meet both Ethiopian law and donor requirements (ESS 5 for World Bank). Key considerations:
- Compensation for structures, crops, and displacement (per Ethiopian proclamation on expropriation)
- Livelihood restoration for affected persons
- Special attention to vulnerable groups (women-headed households, pastoralist communities, elderly)
- Grievance redress mechanism

**Stakeholder Consultation:**
Proclamation 299/2002 requires public participation in EIA. World Bank ESF requires a formal Stakeholder Engagement Plan. Ethiopia's diverse population requires culturally appropriate consultation, including:
- Community meetings at kebele (ward) and woreda (district) level
- Consultation with regional authorities
- Gender-inclusive consultation
- Documentation of concerns raised and responses
- Consultation in local languages where necessary

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Ethiopia-specific branching from core funding source determination:**
```
Determine funding source:
  |-- Government of Ethiopia funded:
  |     --> EPA/EFCCC framework applies
  |     --> Screen against EIA Proclamation 299/2002 schedules
  |     --> Full EIA (Schedule 1) or Preliminary Assessment (Schedule 2)
  |     --> EPA/EFCCC clearance required before construction
  |     --> ERA environmental procedures apply
  |
  |-- World Bank funded:
  |     --> BOTH EPA/EFCCC and ESF apply (more restrictive governs)
  |     --> ESF risk classification (High/Substantial/Moderate/Low)
  |     --> ESCP, SEP, and relevant instruments (RAP, ESMP, LMP)
  |     --> EPA/EFCCC clearance PLUS World Bank clearance required
  |
  |-- AfDB funded:
  |     --> BOTH EPA/EFCCC and AfDB ISS apply
  |     --> AfDB categorization (Category 1/2/3)
  |     --> Similar dual-framework approach
  |
  |-- JICA / EU / other donor funded:
  |     --> BOTH EPA/EFCCC and donor framework apply
  |     --> Apply donor-specific environmental safeguards
  |
  +-- Mixed funding:
        --> Apply MOST RESTRICTIVE framework
        --> Usually World Bank ESF or equivalent is more restrictive
        --> All framework clearances required
```

### M.3.2 Screening Decision Flow

1. **Identify project type and location** -- new construction, rehabilitation, maintenance; federal or regional road; geographic and ecological context
2. **Check EPA schedule categorization** -- classify against Proclamation 299/2002 schedules (Schedule 1, 2, or 3)
3. **If donor-funded:** overlay donor framework classification (WB ESF risk category or AfDB ISS category)
4. **Apply more restrictive category** -- where both frameworks apply, use the more stringent requirement at each decision point
5. **Determine assessment requirements** -- full EIA, preliminary assessment, or none; plus donor instruments if applicable
6. **Identify ERA-specific environmental procedures** -- ERA Design Manual 2013 environmental procedures apply in all cases for federal road projects

### M.3.3 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Ethiopia's diverse ecology creates variable environmental sensitivity.** Road projects may traverse highland plateaus, rift valley systems, lowland pastoralist areas, or forested regions. Each ecological zone has different environmental sensitivities and different affected communities. Screening must account for this diversity.

**Pastoralist communities require special consideration.** In lowland areas (Afar, Somali, southern regions), road projects may affect pastoralist migration routes and grazing areas. These impacts may not appear in standard screening checklists but are significant social impacts requiring assessment.

**Institutional complexity adds screening burden.** EPA/EFCCC institutional reorganization means the regulatory contact point may vary. Use proclamation numbers rather than institutional names as stable references. ERA's own environmental unit is the primary point of contact for federal road project screening.

### M.3.4 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Assuming EPA approval alone is sufficient for donor-funded projects.** Donor-funded ERA projects require BOTH EPA/EFCCC approval and donor framework compliance. The dual-framework is mandatory.
2. **Using outdated institutional names.** EPA has been reorganized multiple times. Reference Proclamation 299/2002 rather than institutional names for legal certainty.
3. **Not screening for pastoralist impacts.** Lowland road projects may affect migration routes and grazing areas. Standard screening checklists may miss these impacts.
4. **Underestimating resettlement complexity.** Ethiopian land tenure (state ownership with use rights) creates compensation complexities that differ from freehold systems. Early screening for land acquisition impacts is critical.

## M.5 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Suggested Action |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Project in Schedule 1 (or equivalent) category | Significant environmental impact expected | SENIOR REVIEW -- Full EIA required. Engage registered EIA practitioner. |
| Project in environmentally sensitive area | National parks, wetlands, forests, rift valley lakes, water catchments | SENIOR REVIEW -- Full EIA mandatory regardless of project type. Enhanced mitigation measures required. |
| Significant land acquisition or involuntary resettlement | Social impact requiring formal RAP | SENIOR REVIEW -- Resettlement Action Plan required. Budget and timeline implications. Early engagement with affected communities. |
| World Bank High or Substantial risk classification | Elevated donor framework requirements | SENIOR REVIEW -- Enhanced ESF instruments required. World Bank clearance needed at each project stage. |
| AfDB Category 1 classification | Significant potential adverse impacts | SENIOR REVIEW -- Full ESIA required under AfDB ISS. Independent review panel may be required. |
| Transboundary impacts | Projects near borders with Kenya, Somalia, Djibouti, Eritrea, Sudan, South Sudan | SENIOR REVIEW -- Transboundary environmental impacts may require notification of neighbouring states and regional coordination. |
| Pastoralist areas affected | Road through pastoralist migration routes or grazing areas | SENIOR REVIEW -- Social impact assessment must address pastoralist livelihoods. Consultation with pastoralist communities required. |

## M.6 Assumptions & Limitations

**Assumptions flagged for verification:**

1. **EPA institutional name:** The EPA has undergone reorganization. The regulatory function may now sit under EFCCC or Ministry of Environment. Use Proclamation No. 299/2002 as the stable legal reference regardless of current institutional name.
2. **Schedule categorization:** The Schedule 1/2/3 structure is assumed from training data. Verify schedule numbering and project categorization against the actual text of EIA Proclamation 299/2002.
3. **ERA Design Manual 2013 environmental procedures:** Content of the ERA environmental procedures section is assumed from training data. Verify against the actual ERA Design Manual 2013.
4. **Regional environmental agency roles:** The division of responsibility between federal EPA/EFCCC and regional environmental bureaus is assumed. Verify current institutional arrangements.
5. **Expropriation and compensation framework:** Ethiopian land acquisition and compensation procedures are summarized from training data. Verify against current Ethiopian expropriation proclamations.

**Limitations:**
- This module provides screening-level guidance only. It does not substitute for a project-specific EIA study by a qualified practitioner.
- All outputs are preliminary/screening level. Final assessment requirements must be confirmed with EPA/EFCCC and, for donor-funded projects, with the relevant development partner.
- The module does not cover regional state-specific environmental requirements that may apply to regional road projects managed outside ERA.
