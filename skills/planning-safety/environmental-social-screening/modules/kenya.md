| World Bank Environmental and Social Framework (ESF) | 2018 | Environmental and Social Standards (ESS 1-10) | Current (for WB-funded projects) |

**KNOWLEDGE GAP:** NEMA-specific screening guidelines and detailed regulatory thresholds are based on training data. Verify against current NEMA regulations and PAM-1 for project-specific application.

### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**NEMA (National Environment Management Authority):** Kenya's environmental regulator. NEMA administers the EMCA, reviews EIA reports, and issues Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) licences. All road projects above certain thresholds require NEMA approval before construction can commence.

**KeNHA / KeRRA / KURA:** Road authorities are the project proponents responsible for commissioning EIA studies and obtaining NEMA approval. Each authority has an environmental unit or focal point.

**County Governments:** County environment committees participate in EIA review for projects within their jurisdictions.

**National Land Commission (NLC):** Involved in land acquisition and resettlement aspects of road projects. Compensation and resettlement processes interface with social impact assessment.

**World Bank / AfDB / JICA:** Development partners fund many Kenya road projects. Donor-funded projects must comply with both NEMA requirements AND the donor's environmental and social framework. The more restrictive framework applies -- same dual-framework pattern as Skill #47 (procurement).

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Kenya environmental screening follows a dual-framework pattern: NEMA for nationally/locally funded projects, and NEMA plus World Bank ESF (or equivalent donor framework) for donor-funded projects. When both frameworks apply, the more restrictive requirement governs. EMCA Second Schedule lists projects requiring full EIA -- road projects above certain thresholds are included. The ESIA process includes screening, scoping, EIA study, EIA report, public review, and NEMA licensing.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 NEMA Screening Framework

**EMCA Second Schedule (projects requiring full EIA):**
Road projects requiring full EIA under EMCA include (training data -- verify against current EMCA Second Schedule):
- New road construction (all classes)
- Major road rehabilitation/upgrading involving realignment or widening
- Bridge construction
- Projects in environmentally sensitive areas (wetlands, forests, national parks, water catchments)
- Projects requiring significant land acquisition or resettlement

**EIA Process under EMCA/Regulations:**

| Step | Activity | Responsibility | Output |
|------|----------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | Screening | Project proponent / NEMA | Determination: Full EIA or Project Report |
| 2 | Scoping | EIA Lead Expert | Scoping report, Terms of Reference |
| 3 | EIA Study | EIA Lead Expert (registered with NEMA) | Environmental and Social Impact Assessment |
| 4 | EIA Report | EIA Lead Expert | EIA Study Report submitted to NEMA |
| 5 | Public Review | NEMA / Public | Public comments, stakeholder consultation |
| 6 | Review and Decision | NEMA | EIA licence (approved/approved with conditions/rejected) |
| 7 | Monitoring | Project proponent | Environmental audit during construction |

**Project Report vs Full EIA:**
- **Project Report:** For smaller projects with limited environmental impact. Less detailed than full EIA. NEMA may accept a Project Report for minor road rehabilitation/maintenance projects.
- **Full EIA:** For projects listed in EMCA Second Schedule. Requires registered EIA Lead Expert, scoping, detailed study, public participation, and NEMA review.

### M.2.2 World Bank ESF Overlay (Donor-Funded Projects)

For World Bank-funded road projects, both NEMA and ESF requirements apply:

**Environmental and Social Standards (ESS):**

| ESS | Title | Key Relevance to Road Projects |
|-----|-------|-------------------------------|
| ESS 1 | Assessment and Management of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts | Overarching risk assessment and management framework |
| ESS 2 | Labour and Working Conditions | Construction worker safety, employment conditions |
| ESS 3 | Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention | Dust, noise, water pollution during construction |
| ESS 4 | Community Health and Safety | Road safety during construction, communicable diseases |
| ESS 5 | Land Acquisition, Restrictions on Land Use, and Involuntary Resettlement | Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) for affected persons |
| ESS 6 | Biodiversity Conservation | Wildlife crossings, habitat protection |
| ESS 7 | Indigenous Peoples / Sub-Saharan African Historically Underserved Traditional Local Communities | Consultation with traditional communities |
| ESS 8 | Cultural Heritage | Archaeological sites, cultural properties |
| ESS 10 | Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure | Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) |

**Key ESF Instruments:**
- **Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP):** Overarching document committing project to ESF compliance
- **Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP):** Required for all ESF projects -- describes how stakeholders will be identified, consulted, and informed
- **Resettlement Action Plan (RAP):** Required when land acquisition displaces people or affects livelihoods (ESS 5)
- **Labour Management Procedures (LMP):** Required for construction projects (ESS 2)
- **Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP):** Mitigation measures for identified impacts

### M.2.3 Social Impact Assessment

**Land Acquisition and Resettlement:**
Road projects in Kenya frequently require land acquisition, particularly for new roads or widening. The Land Act 2012 and NLC procedures govern compensation. For donor-funded projects, Resettlement Action Plans must meet both Kenyan law and donor requirements (ESS 5 for World Bank). Key considerations:
- Compensation at replacement cost (not market value for informal structures)
- Livelihood restoration for affected persons
- Special attention to vulnerable groups (women-headed households, elderly, persons with disabilities)
- Grievance redress mechanism

**Stakeholder Consultation:**
EMCA requires public participation in EIA. World Bank ESF requires a formal Stakeholder Engagement Plan. Consultation should be culturally appropriate and include:
- Community meetings (barazas)
- Consultation with county government representatives
- Gender-inclusive consultation (separate sessions if needed)
- Documentation of concerns raised and responses

### M.2.4 NEMA Licence Conditions and Environmental Audit

NEMA EIA licences typically include conditions for:
- Dust and noise control during construction
- Water quality protection (especially near water crossings)
- Borrow pit restoration after material extraction
- Waste management on construction sites
- Protection of vegetation and wildlife habitats
- Environmental monitoring during construction and post-construction
- Annual environmental audits during construction phase

## M.3 Country-Specific Reasoning

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Country Branch)

**Kenya-specific branching from core funding source determination:**
```
Determine funding source:
  |-- Government of Kenya funded:
  |     --> NEMA framework applies
  |     --> Screen against EMCA Second Schedule
  |     --> Full EIA or Project Report
  |     --> NEMA licence required before construction
  |
  |-- World Bank funded:
  |     --> BOTH NEMA and ESF apply (more restrictive governs)
  |     --> ESF risk classification (High/Substantial/Moderate/Low)
  |     --> ESCP, SEP, and relevant instruments (RAP, ESMP, LMP)
  |     --> NEMA licence PLUS World Bank clearance required
  |
  |-- AfDB funded:
  |     --> BOTH NEMA and AfDB ISS apply
  |     --> AfDB Integrated Safeguards System
  |     --> Similar dual-framework approach
  |
  +-- Mixed funding:
        --> Apply MOST RESTRICTIVE framework
        --> Usually World Bank ESF or equivalent is more restrictive
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Resettlement is often the most complex social impact.** Kenya road projects frequently affect informal settlements, agricultural land, and community facilities. Early screening for resettlement impacts is critical to project planning and budgeting. Underestimating resettlement costs and timelines is a common cause of project delays.

**Borrow pit management is a significant environmental issue.** NEMA requires EIA and restoration for borrow pits. Borrow pit sourcing affects both environmental compliance and construction cost (see Skill #27 Kenya module -- NEMA requirements documented as significant cost factor).

**Community concerns about road projects are real and legitimate.** Road projects can divide communities, destroy livelihoods (roadside traders), and create safety hazards during construction. Meaningful stakeholder engagement (not just notification) is essential for social licence and project success.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Assuming NEMA approval alone is sufficient for donor-funded projects.** Donor-funded projects require BOTH NEMA approval and donor framework compliance. The dual-framework is mandatory.
2. **Starting construction before NEMA licence is obtained.** EMCA prohibits commencement of listed projects without EIA licence. Doing so is a criminal offence.
3. **Treating stakeholder consultation as a formality.** Consultation must be genuine, documented, and responsive to concerns raised. Box-ticking consultation creates social resistance and project delays.
4. **Not screening for resettlement early enough.** Resettlement impacts should be identified at feasibility stage (PAM-5), not during detailed design. Late identification leads to cost overruns and delays.

## M.5 Country-Specific Validation

### M.5.1 Range Checks

No numerical range checks for environmental screening -- this is a process-based assessment.

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Funding source determines applicable framework
- **Standard:** EMCA / World Bank ESF
- **Pass condition:** Framework correctly identified based on funding source
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Wrong framework applied. Verify funding source and applicable requirements.

- **Check:** EIA licence obtained before construction
- **Standard:** EMCA Part VI
- **Pass condition:** NEMA EIA licence in place before construction commences
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- EMCA requires EIA licence before project commencement. Construction without licence is a legal offence.

### M.5.3 Departures / Relaxations Process

No departures from EMCA requirements -- EIA is a legal requirement. NEMA may determine that a Project Report is sufficient instead of full EIA for smaller projects.

## M.6 Country-Specific Escalation

| Trigger | Reason | Suggested Action |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Project in environmentally sensitive area | High environmental risk | SENIOR REVIEW -- Project in sensitive area (wetland, forest, national park, water catchment). Full EIA mandatory. Enhanced mitigation measures required. |
| Resettlement of >200 households | Significant social impact | SENIOR REVIEW -- Large-scale resettlement. RAP required with livelihood restoration programme. Budget and timeline implications. |
| Cultural heritage site affected | ESS 8 triggered | SENIOR REVIEW -- Cultural heritage site identified. Chance finds procedure required. Archaeological assessment may be needed. |
| Project crosses major river / water body | Water quality risk | NOTE -- Water crossing requires assessment of construction impacts on water quality and aquatic ecology. NEMA conditions likely. |

## M.7 Worked Examples -- Kenya

### Example 1: Environmental Screening for World Bank-Funded Road Upgrade

**Project:** Environmental screening for a proposed 25km Kenya road upgrade project (gravel to paved) funded by the World Bank. The road passes through agricultural land with some homesteads requiring relocation.

**Given:**
- Project: 25km Class C road upgrade (gravel to paved)
- Funding: World Bank
- Land use: agricultural (maize, sugarcane), scattered homesteads
- 15 homesteads within road reserve requiring relocation
- No sensitive environmental areas (no forests, wetlands, or parks)
- Existing borrow pit sources identified
- County: Western Kenya

**Solution:**

Step 1: Determine applicable framework:
- World Bank funded --> BOTH NEMA (EMCA) and World Bank ESF apply
- More restrictive framework governs at each point

Step 2: NEMA screening:
- New road construction/upgrading --> EMCA Second Schedule --> Full EIA required
- NEMA EIA licence must be obtained before construction

Step 3: World Bank ESF risk classification:
- Environmental risk: Moderate (agricultural land, no sensitive habitats)
- Social risk: Substantial (15 homesteads require relocation = involuntary resettlement)
- Overall: Substantial risk

Step 4: ESF instruments required:
- ESCP (Environmental and Social Commitment Plan)
- SEP (Stakeholder Engagement Plan) -- including community baraza consultations
- RAP (Resettlement Action Plan) per ESS 5 -- for 15 affected homesteads
- ESMP (Environmental and Social Management Plan) -- construction phase mitigation
- LMP (Labour Management Procedures) per ESS 2

Step 5: Key social impacts:
- 15 homesteads requiring relocation -- RAP with compensation at replacement cost
- Agricultural land acquisition -- crop compensation required
- Livelihood restoration for affected families
- Grievance redress mechanism established

**Result:** Dual-framework applies (NEMA + World Bank ESF). Full EIA required under EMCA. ESF risk classification: Substantial (due to resettlement). Required instruments: ESCP, SEP, RAP, ESMP, LMP. Key social impact: 15 homesteads requiring RAP with compensation and livelihood restoration. NEMA licence AND World Bank environmental clearance required before construction.
