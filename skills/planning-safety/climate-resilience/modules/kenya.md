### M.1.2 Institutional Context

**KeNHA / KeRRA / KURA:** Road authorities responsible for implementing climate-resilient road design. Each authority should apply climate risk assessment to road projects, particularly in high-risk zones.

**Climate Change Directorate:** Under the Ministry of Environment, Climate Change, and Forestry. Responsible for climate policy coordination, including the NCCAP. The Directorate provides guidance on mainstreaming climate change into infrastructure planning.

**Kenya Meteorological Department (KMD):** Provides climate data, forecasts, and projections. KMD data informs climate risk assessment for road projects including rainfall intensity, temperature trends, and extreme event frequency.

**NEMA:** Environmental regulator -- climate change considerations are part of EIA requirements under EMCA (see Skill #62 Kenya module).

**County Governments:** Counties develop County Climate Change Action Plans that identify local climate risks and adaptation priorities. Road projects should align with county climate plans.

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Kenya's Climate Change Act 2016 requires mainstreaming of climate change in all development planning, including infrastructure. RDM 5.2 Chapter 12 provides a comprehensive framework for climate resilience in pavement maintenance and rehabilitation, including climate risk mapping (Figures 12.2-12.3), a catalogue of solutions (Tables 12.1-12.3), and design guidance for climate-resilient pavements. RDM 2.1 addresses climate resilience for drainage design. The approach is practical: identify climate risks, apply appropriate adjustment factors, and select adapted design solutions.

## M.2 Country-Specific Knowledge

### M.2.1 Kenya Climate Zones

Kenya's climate is highly varied. Approximately 80% of land area is arid or semi-arid (ASALs), with the remaining 20% being humid/sub-humid highlands and coastal areas. Climate risks differ significantly by zone.

**Climate Zones and Road Infrastructure Risks:**

| Zone | Approximate Coverage | Key Climate Risks for Roads | Example Regions |
|------|---------------------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Arid / Semi-Arid | ~80% land area | Flash floods (seasonal), extreme temperatures (>40C), drought (material quality), low moisture for compaction | North Eastern, Upper Eastern, Turkana, Garissa, Wajir, Marsabit |
| Humid Highlands | Central, Western highlands | Increased rainfall intensity, landslides, saturated subgrades, high water tables | Central Kenya, Western Kenya highlands, parts of Rift Valley |
| Coastal | Coast strip | Sea level rise (long-term), flooding (seasonal), high temperatures, salt spray corrosion | Mombasa, Kwale, Kilifi, Lamu |
| Lake Victoria Basin | Western lowlands | Flooding, high water tables, saturated subgrades | Kisumu, Siaya, Homa Bay, parts of Busia |
| Rift Valley | Variable | Flash floods in valley floor, landslides on escarpments, temperature extremes | Nakuru, Naivasha, Baringo, Turkana |

### M.2.2 Climate Adjustment Factors by Zone

**ILLUSTRATIVE climate adjustment factors for road design. These values are indicative only and should be verified against current Kenya climate projections and RDM 2.1 Chapter 7 requirements.**

| Zone | Drainage CF | Pavement CF | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Coastal | 1.2 - 1.3 | 1.1 - 1.2 | Sea level rise affects drainage outfalls. High temperature increases pavement distress. Salt spray requires modified binder. |
| Arid / Semi-Arid | 1.2 - 1.3 | 1.1 - 1.2 | Flash flood risk requires increased drainage capacity. Extreme temperatures affect binder performance. Low moisture complicates compaction. |
| Highland | 1.3 - 1.5 | 1.1 - 1.3 | Increased rainfall intensity requires significantly enhanced drainage. High rainfall areas may need modified pavement materials. |
| Lake Victoria Basin | 1.3 | 1.1 - 1.2 | Flooding risk. High water tables require subgrade protection. |
| Rift Valley (floor) | 1.2 - 1.3 | 1.1 | Flash flood risk in valley floor. |
| Rift Valley (escarpment) | 1.3 - 1.5 | 1.1 | Landslide risk on escarpments. Enhanced drainage and slope protection. |

**Application:** Multiply design storm intensity or drainage design flow by the drainage CF. Multiply pavement design thickness by the pavement CF where climate exposure is significant. All CF values are ILLUSTRATIVE per training data -- verify against current KMD climate projections and RDM 2.1/5.2 guidance.

### M.2.3 Climate Resilience Solutions (RDM 5.2 Chapter 12)

RDM 5.2 Chapter 12 provides a comprehensive catalogue of climate resilience solutions:

**Catalogue of Solutions (Table 12.1):**

| Code | Category | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| CCC1 | Climate Risk Mapping | Identify flood and temperature risk areas (Figures 12.2, 12.3) |
| CCC2 | Vulnerability Evaluation | Criteria for vulnerability level assessment (Table 12.2) |
| CCC3 | Level of Risk | Categories of risk and intervention levels (Table 12.3) |
| CPM1 | Subgrade Materials | Climate-adapted subgrade treatment (Table 12.4) |
| CPM2 | Base and Subbase Materials | Climate-adapted granular and stabilised materials |
| CPM3 | Surfacing/Wearing Course | Climate-adapted surfacing options |
| CMM1 | Carbon Footprint | Reducing carbon footprint of road construction materials |
| CMM2 | Transportation | Low-carbon transport of materials |
| CMM3 | Construction Process | Climate-aware construction practices |

**Key Climate Resilience Design Measures:**

1. **Drainage enhancement:** Increase design storm return period, apply climate adjustment factors per RDM 2.1, provide enhanced cross-drainage capacity (cross-reference Skills #29-32 Kenya modules)
2. **Pavement adaptation:** Use PMB (polymer modified bitumen) for high-temperature zones, increase subgrade protection in high-moisture areas, apply armoured bases for flood-prone roads (RDM 5.2 Figures 12.4, 12.5)
3. **Earthworks protection:** Enhanced erosion protection for embankments in high-rainfall areas, bio-engineering per RDM 2.2 Section 6.3.6 (cross-reference Skills #26-27 Kenya modules)
4. **Structural adaptation:** Increased freeboard for bridges in flood-prone areas, scour protection enhancement (cross-reference Skills #33-41 Kenya modules)
5. **Do-Nou technology:** For soft ground improvement on low-volume roads in waterlogged areas (RDM 5.2 Section 14.5)

### M.2.4 RDM 5.2 Climate Risk Mapping

RDM 5.2 Figures 12.2 and 12.3 provide Kenya-specific climate risk maps:
- **Figure 12.2 -- Flood Risk Areas:** Shows high flood risk in Tana River basin, Lake Victoria basin, coastal areas, and seasonal flood-prone areas in arid zones
- **Figure 12.3 -- Temperature Risk Areas:** Shows high temperature risk in North Eastern, Coast, and parts of Eastern and Rift Valley

These maps support project-level climate risk screening -- project location can be mapped against flood and temperature risk zones to determine required climate resilience measures.

### M.2.5 Carbon Footprint Considerations

RDM 5.2 Chapter 12 addresses climate mitigation alongside adaptation:
- CMM1: Reducing carbon footprint of road building materials (recycled materials, local sourcing)
- CMM2: Transportation -- reducing material haulage distances
- CMM3: Construction process -- energy-efficient equipment, reduced waste
- Carbon footprint assessment is increasingly required for donor-funded projects

```
  |     Primary risks: flash floods, extreme temperature, drought

        Zone-specific assessment required
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Country-Specific)

**Kenya's 80% ASAL coverage makes climate resilience critical.** Roads in arid areas must accommodate extreme temperature swings (>40C day, cold nights) and seasonal flash floods after prolonged drought periods. These conditions are challenging for both pavement materials and drainage design.

**Highland roads face increasing rainfall intensity.** Kenya's central and western highlands are experiencing more intense rainfall events. Design drainage capacity should be enhanced using climate adjustment factors. The Check Frequency column in RDM 2.1 Table 4.1 provides guidance for climate-resilient design return periods.

**Flash flood damage to roads in arid areas is a recurring problem.** Roads in North Eastern Kenya and other arid zones suffer periodic destruction from flash floods. Climate-resilient design should include enhanced drainage structures, flood-resistant embankments, and drift/causeway options for flood-prone crossings (cross-reference Skill #40 Kenya module).

**All CF values are ILLUSTRATIVE.** The climate adjustment factors in this module are indicative values from training data. Project-specific climate risk assessment using KMD projections should be used for detailed design.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Country-Specific)

1. **Not adjusting drainage design for climate change.** Using historical IDF data without climate adjustment factors underestimates future design flows. Apply RDM 2.1 Chapter 7 guidance.
2. **Ignoring temperature effects on pavement.** Kenya's arid zones experience very high temperatures that accelerate binder aging and rutting. Standard bitumen may be inadequate -- PMB should be considered.
3. **Applying a single national CF value.** Kenya's climate variation is extreme -- a CF appropriate for highland Kenya is inappropriate for the coast. Zone-specific factors are essential.
4. **Not considering carbon footprint for donor-funded projects.** World Bank and other donors increasingly require climate mitigation considerations. RDM 5.2 Chapter 12 provides guidance on reducing construction carbon footprint.

## M.7 Worked Examples -- Kenya

### Example 1: Climate Resilience Assessment for Arid Zone Road

**Project:** Climate resilience assessment for a proposed 40km Class C road in Kenya's arid North Eastern region (Garissa County).

**Given:**
- Location: Garissa County (arid zone)
- Climate risks: flash floods (seasonal), extreme temperatures (>40C), prolonged drought
- Terrain: flat arid plain with seasonal wadi crossings
- 3 seasonal wadi crossings identified on alignment
- Nearest KMD station data shows increasing rainfall intensity trend

**Solution:**

Step 1: Climate zone identification:
- Location: Arid/semi-arid zone (RDM 5.2 Figure 12.2 -- flood risk area, Figure 12.3 -- high temperature risk)

Step 2: Climate risks for road infrastructure:
- **Flash flooding:** Seasonal wadis carry high flows after rare but intense rainfall. Roads crossing wadis are vulnerable to washout.
- **Extreme temperature:** >40C daytime temperatures accelerate binder aging, increase rutting risk, and affect construction quality (compaction in extreme heat).
- **Drought:** Low moisture content affects compaction during construction. Material quality in arid areas may be marginal.

Step 3: Climate adjustment factors (ILLUSTRATIVE):
- Drainage CF: 1.2-1.3 (enhanced for flash flood risk)
- Pavement CF: 1.1-1.2 (for temperature effects)

Step 4: Climate resilience measures:
- **Drainage:** Apply drainage CF 1.25 to design flows per RDM 2.1 Chapter 7. Design wadi crossings as drifts/causeways per Skill #40 Kenya module for the 3 seasonal crossings. Enhanced scour protection at all drainage structures.
- **Pavement:** Use PMB (polymer modified bitumen) for surfacing to resist temperature extremes per RDM 5.2 Chapter 12. Consider armoured base design (RDM 5.2 Figure 12.4) for flood-exposed sections.
- **Earthworks:** Enhanced erosion protection at embankment slopes near wadi crossings. Bio-engineering options may be limited in arid zone -- consider gabion/rip-rap protection.
- **Construction:** Schedule earthworks and compaction for periods of higher moisture availability. Protect fresh construction from flash flood damage.

Step 5: Cross-references:
- Drainage: Skills #29-32 Kenya modules (climate adjustment per RDM 2.1 Chapter 7)
- Pavement: Skills #21-25 Kenya modules (material selection for temperature extremes)
- Structures: Skill #40 Kenya module (drift/causeway for seasonal crossings)
- Climate Change Act 2016: project should align with NCCAP adaptation priorities

**Result:** Climate resilience assessment identifies flash flooding, extreme temperature, and drought as key risks. ILLUSTRATIVE drainage CF 1.25 applied. PMB recommended for surfacing. Drift/causeway design for 3 wadi crossings. All CF values are ILLUSTRATIVE -- verify against current KMD climate projections for Garissa County. Carbon footprint considerations per RDM 5.2 CMM1-CMM3 should be assessed if donor-funded.
