### M.1.2 Institutional Context

Same institutional framework as Skill #13 Kenya module. The Ministry of Roads and Transport (MoR) has overall responsibility for road marking planning and supervision. Under the MoR, various authorities are responsible for different road classes:

- **KeNHA** (Kenya National Highways Authority): International trunk roads (Class A) and national trunk roads (Class B).
- **KeRRA** (Kenya Rural Roads Authority): Rural roads (Class C to W).
- **KURA** (Kenya Urban Roads Authority): Urban roads (Class UA, UC, UL).
- **County Governments:** Unclassified roads under county jurisdiction.

Kenya drives on the left (left-hand traffic). Signs and markings are placed on the left-hand side of the road. This affects road stud colour assignments (red on the nearside/left edge, yellow on the offside/right/median edge).

### M.1.3 Standard Philosophy

Kenya RDM 6.1 establishes a uniform system for road markings applicable to all categories of roads in rural and urban areas. The manual follows the Vienna Convention on Road Signs and Signals (1968, amended 1993/2006) with Kenya-specific adaptations. Key features of the Kenya approach:

- **Yellow centre line, white edge line:** Kenya uses yellow for centre lines separating opposing traffic (per Traffic Act CAP 403, Sections 15-16) and white for edge lines and lane lines.
- **Colour coding for special facilities:** Blue for bus lanes/BRT, green for cycle lanes/NMT crossings, red for hazardous conflict areas.
- **Comprehensive coding system:** Every marking type has a unique code (LM01-LM33 for longitudinal, TM01-TM08 for transverse, HM01-HM26 for hazard, BM01-BM08 for block, AM01-AM24 for arrows).
- **Road stud integration:** Road studs are mandatory on Class A roads and desirable on Class B roads, with detailed spacing and colour specifications.
- **Performance-based:** Chapter 14 specifies retroreflectivity thresholds and inspection requirements.

### M.2.2 Visibility and Combined Line System

From RDM 6.1 Table 4.4 -- the Combined Line System (CLS) is used where overtaking must be restricted:

| Road Class | 85th Percentile Speed (km/h) | Visibility Distance (m) | Critical Value (m) |
|-----------|------------------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| E, UL, minor roads | 40 | 40 | 65 |
| C, D, UC | 60 | 80 | 75 |
| B, UA | 80 | 130 | 95 |
| A | >90 | 300 | 135 |

Where visibility is less than the critical value, a continuous yellow line must be used. The CLS consists of a 0.1 m wide continuous yellow line accompanied by either a continuous or broken line, with bi-directional yellow studs at 6 m intervals.

### M.2.3 Transverse Markings

From RDM 6.1 Chapter 5 and Table 5.1:

- **Stop line (TM01):** 0.4 m wide continuous white line. STOP word message (DM01/DM02) accompanies the line. Letter height 1.8 m for speeds up to 65 km/h, 2.8 m for higher speeds.
- **Give Way line (TM04/TM07):** 0.2-0.4 m wide broken white line, 0.6 m marks with 0.3 m gaps.
- **Pedestrian crossing (BM01/BM02/BM03):** White rectangular blocks 0.5 m wide with 0.5 m gaps. Crossing length 2.0-4.0 m depending on road width.
- **No Entry:** Continuous white line combined with broken line (1 m marks, 1 m gaps, 0.2 m wide, 0.4 m apart). NO ENTRY word message 0.4 m lettering.

### M.2.6 Road Studs

Kenya RDM 6.1 Chapter 13 provides comprehensive road stud requirements. Kenya-specific details are in `tables/kenya_road_stud_colours.json`. Key points:

- **Mandatory on:** Class A roads for centre line, lane lines, and edge lines.
- **Desirable on:** Class B roads at vulnerable sections (curves, junctions).
- **Colour coding:** White = lane line, Red = nearside edge (left, should not be crossed), Yellow = centre line / median side, Green = crossable continuous lines.
- **Spacing:** 9-18 m depending on road type and section.
- **Solar studs:** Same spacing, used at hazardous locations (crash-prone areas, bridges, sharp curves).

### M.2.7 Performance Criteria

From RDM 6.1 Chapter 14:

**Dry retroreflectivity (Table 14.1):**

| Speed (km/h) | Initial (7 days) mcd/m2/lux | Minimum Threshold (2 years) mcd/m2/lux |
|--------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Up to 65 | 200 | 80 |
| 65-100 | 250 | 110 |
| Above 100 | 350 | 150 |

**Wet retroreflectivity (Table 14.2):** Initial 100 mcd/m2/lux, minimum threshold 50 mcd/m2/lux (all speeds).

**Skid resistance (Table 14.3):** Initial 55 BPN, minimum threshold 45 BPN (urban/city).

**Daytime visibility:** Minimum luminance coefficient 100 mcd/m2/lux (asphalt), 130 mcd/m2/lux (concrete).

### M.2.8 Raised Profile Edge Lines

From RDM 6.1 Section 4.7:
- Width: 150-200 mm
- Rib height: 4-8 mm (maximum 11 mm for expressways)
- Rib spacing: not exceeding 500 mm (250 mm recommended for expressways)
- Drainage gap: 100-150 mm between each 36 m set
- Not recommended on curves with radius <1000 m (destabilises motorcyclists)

### M.2.9 Marking on Concrete Pavement

From RDM 6.1 Section 2.13:
- Same colour conventions as flexible pavement
- Thermoplastic thickness 1.5-2.3 mm
- Epoxy material 0.6-0.63 mm alternatively
- Road studs: same spacing, but avoid placing at joints (+/- 100 mm tolerance at joints)

### M.3.1 Decision Logic (Kenya Branch)

```
Step 2 (Kenya): Longitudinal marking selection
  +-- Road class from Skill #13?

  |     +-- Class C, D, UC (speed <80 km/h)?

  |     |     +-- Edge line: LM26 (continuous, 100mm, white)
  |     +-- Class E, UL, minor?
  |           +-- Centre line: LM01 if carriageway >=5.5 m width
  |           +-- Edge line: LM26 if warranted
  |
  +-- Combined Line System required?

  |     +-- If visibility < critical value: use continuous yellow centre line
  |     +-- CLS: 0.1m continuous yellow + broken/continuous companion line
  |     +-- Bi-directional yellow studs at 6 m intervals along CLS
  |
  +-- Expressway?
        +-- Edge line width: 200 mm (Section 4.2.3)
        +-- Raised profile edge lines recommended (Section 4.7)
        +-- Road studs: 18 m spacing (normal), 12 m (no-overtaking)
```

### M.3.2 Professional Judgment (Kenya-Specific)

**Kenya uses yellow centre lines by law.** The Traffic Act CAP 403 (Sections 15-16) mandates yellow longitudinal marking for centre lines and white for edge lines. This is a legal requirement, not merely a convention. Using white for centre lines in Kenya would violate the Traffic Act.

**Motorcycle safety and road markings.** Kenya's exponential growth in motorcycle (boda-boda) traffic requires attention to marking-related hazards for two-wheelers. Raised profile edge lines should not be used on curves with radius <1000 m as they destabilise motorcycles (Section 4.7). Thermoplastic markings should maintain skid resistance (minimum 45 BPN) as smooth markings are hazardous for motorcycles in wet conditions.

**Road studs are critical for Kenya's driving conditions.** Many Kenyan trunk roads lack road lighting. Combined with frequent heavy rain, flat markings alone are inadequate for night-time visibility. Road studs should be specified on all Class A and B roads, not treated as optional.

### M.3.3 Common Errors (Kenya-Specific)

1. **Using white for centre lines.** Kenya law requires yellow centre lines. White centre lines are non-compliant.
2. **Omitting road studs on Class A roads.** RDM 6.1 makes studs effectively mandatory on Class A roads.
3. **Wrong stud colour on divided carriageways.** Red on nearside (left), yellow on median side. Confusion between these is dangerous.
4. **Using Uganda marking dimensions.** Kenya Tables 4.2/4.3 differ from Uganda MoWT marking tables (different mark/gap ratios and widths). Always use Kenya tables.
5. **Not checking visibility for CLS decisions.** The Combined Line System decision depends on visibility distance vs critical value (Table 4.4). Defaulting to broken lines without checking visibility is unsafe.

### M.5.2 Standards Compliance Checks

- **Check:** Centre line colour is yellow (not white)
- **Standard:** Traffic Act CAP 403, Sections 15-16; RDM 6.1, Section 2.10
- **Pass condition:** Centre line specified as yellow
- **Fail action:** SENIOR REVIEW -- Centre line specified as white. Kenya Traffic Act requires yellow centre line marking.

- **Check:** Road studs specified for Class A roads
- **Standard:** RDM 6.1, Section 13.3
- **Pass condition:** Road stud schedule included for Class A road
- **Fail action:** NOTE -- Class A road without road studs. Studs are advisable per Section 13.3.

## M.7 Worked Example -- Kenya

### Example: Road Marking Design for Class B Rural Highway

**Given:**
- Road: Class B national trunk road
- Design speed: 100 km/h (85th percentile speed 80 km/h)
- Carriageway: Two-lane two-way, 3.5 m lanes, 1.5 m paved shoulder
- Carriageway width: 7.0 m plus shoulders
- Context: Rural, no street lighting
- AADT: 3,000 vpd

**Solution:**

**Step 1 -- Longitudinal markings (Table 4.2, >=80 km/h):**
- Centre line: LM02 -- broken yellow, 3 m mark, 9 m gap, 150 mm wide
- Edge line: LM27 -- continuous white, 150 mm wide
- At no-overtaking sections (visibility < 95 m per Table 4.4): LM25 continuous yellow, 150 mm wide, or combined line LM29

**Step 2 -- Marking material:**
- Thermoplastic (Section 2.4) for all permanent markings
- Glass beads per KS 1610 / AASHTO M247
- Initial dry retroreflectivity >= 250 mcd/m2/lux (Table 14.1, 65-100 km/h)
- Threshold after 2 years >= 110 mcd/m2/lux

**Step 3 -- Road studs (Table 13.1, >=60 km/h, Type A/B):**
- Centre line: bi-directional yellow-yellow, 12 m spacing (normal), 9 m spacing (no-overtaking)
- Edge line: bi-directional red-white, 18 m spacing (normal), 9 m spacing (no-overtaking)

**Step 4 -- No junction markings required for midblock section.**

**Result:**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Centre line | LM02: broken yellow, 3 m / 9 m, 150 mm |
| Edge line | LM27: continuous white, 150 mm |
| Material | Hot-applied thermoplastic, KS EAS 928-1 |
| Retroreflectivity (initial) | >= 250 mcd/m2/lux (dry) |
| Road studs (centre) | Yellow-yellow bi-directional, 12 m |
| Road studs (edge) | Red-white bi-directional, 18 m |
